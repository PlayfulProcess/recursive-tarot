Trionfi — the tarot game, the way it began

Before any fortune was ever told with them, tarot cards were for playing. The Italian novelty of the 1440s was a set of trionfi — trumps — laid over an ordinary four-suit pack, cards that triumph over the suits in a trick-taking game. Here is that idea, simplified to a quick duel, dealt from the public-domain Tarot de Marseille (Conver, 1760). For the divinatory side, try the Caster or the Oracle on recursive.eco ↗.

Rules

A 10-trick duel. The deck is six trumps (I–VI) plus the Coins and Cups one through seven. You lead first.

  • Follow suit. When a suit is led you must play that suit if you can; only if you have none may you play a trump or throw another card.
  • Trumps triumph. Any trump beats any suit card. Between two trumps, or two cards of the led suit, the higher number wins. A card of neither the led suit nor a trump cannot win the trick.
  • The trick's winner leads the next. Win the most of the ten tricks to win the game.
