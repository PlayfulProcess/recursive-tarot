Trionfi — the tarot game, the way it began

Before any fortune was told with them, tarot cards were for playing. The Italian novelty of the 1440s was a set of trionfi — trumps — laid over an ordinary four-suit pack, cards that triumph over the suits in a trick-taking game. Here is that idea as a quick duel, dealt from the public-domain Tarot de Marseille (Conver, 1760). For the divinatory side, try the Caster or the Oracle ↗.

How to play

A 12-trick duel. Each round you and your opponent play one card; whoever plays the stronger card wins the trick and leads the next. Take the most of the twelve tricks to win. You lead first.

Follow suit

When a suit is led you must play that suit if you hold it (illegal cards are greyed out and can't be clicked). Only when you have none of the led suit may you play a trump or throw any card.

Who wins the trick

  • A trump beats any suit card, always.
  • Two trumps, or two cards of the led suit: the higher-ranked one wins.
  • A card that is neither a trump nor of the led suit cannot win — it's a discard.

The deck

Dealt from the Tarot de Marseille (Nicolas Conver, 1760) — a real deck people played with for centuries. The purpose-built Tarocchino of Bologna and the Florentine Minchiate are in the library too, and coming as their own games.
