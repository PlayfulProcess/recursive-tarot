Tarocchino di Bologna — the historical 62-card partnership game

Bologna's own shrunken tarot, played continuously since the 1500s: a fast four-player, two-partnership trick game on a 62-card pack (the 2–5 of every suit removed). You sit South; North is your partner. It is the most faithful surviving witness to Dummett's A-order of the trumps. You win by capturing card points, not tricks.

Teams. You + North vs. West + East. Play goes clockwise; the trick winner leads next.
Follow, then trump. Follow the led suit if you can. Void in it, you must play a trump if you hold one; only then may you discard.
The Fool is the Excuse. The Matto can be played any time, never wins the trick, and stays with your side for its 5 points.
Win by points. Honours (Matto · Bagattino · Angel · World) & Kings = 5, Queens 4, Knights 3, Knaves 2; all else 0. Take over half of the 76.

How it was played

Pack, A-order, suit ranking, the Excuse and the point values follow the Bolognese tradition — see John McLeod's Ottocento rules (pagat.com), Tarocco Bolognese, and this deck's research dossier.
