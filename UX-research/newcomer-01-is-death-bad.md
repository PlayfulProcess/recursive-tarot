# A newcomer's report: "Is the Death card bad?"

*Persona: I know almost nothing about tarot. I saw the Death card in a movie and it
looked ominous. A friend sent me this site. I just want to know — is the Death card
actually bad? — and maybe poke around. I'm not going to read documentation.
Date of visit: Jun 2026.*

---

## What I came to do

Land on the site and quickly find out whether the Death card means something
terrible. Ten-minute attention span, no patience for jargon.

## What I found out

**The landing page is welcoming and gives me one clear thing to do.** "The Recursive
Tarot — a free, public-domain collection of historical tarot decks." There's a big
**🔮 Draw a Spread** button, which is inviting. The one-line pitch even told me
something I didn't know: tarot started as "the 1440s Milanese **card game**," and
divination was "the 1781 occult reframing that **invented** divination." So within
the first sentence I learned tarot wasn't originally fortune-telling. Nice.

**Once I found a Death card, the answer was reassuring** — but I had to dig. The
cards themselves say Death is **not** about literal dying:
- Wirth: *"transformation, not extinction."*
- Golden Dawn: *"Death in GD is not annihilation but transformation… what has
  fulfilled its purpose is composted into the material for new growth."*
- And the older decks frame it as *"the great leveller"* / an allegory from
  Petrarch's *Triumph of Death* — history, not doom.

So the answer to my question is here and it's comforting: **the Death card is about
change, not death.** Good.

## UX bugs & friction

1. **There is no search box.** This is the big one. I wanted to type "Death" and go.
   I couldn't. There's no search anywhere on the landing page or header. For someone
   who came with exactly one card in mind, that's the most natural action and it's
   missing.
2. **Getting to a single card is not obvious.** To find Death I had to figure out
   that "Cards" or "Explorer" in the header leads to a deck, then scroll/hunt for
   card 13. A newcomer doesn't know Death is "trump XIII" or which deck to open. I
   nearly gave up before finding it.
3. **The framing is scholarly.** Header links are "Overview history", "Origins &
   predecessors", "The genealogy", "Tree of Life", "Timeline", "Genealogy". For a
   curious newcomer that reads as a museum, not a "what does my card mean?" tool. The
   reassurance I wanted exists but is buried under historian-first navigation.
4. **"Draw a Spread" gives names, not meanings.** I clicked the friendly button,
   got three card names, and then… didn't know what they meant or where to click for
   that. The most inviting button led to a small dead end for someone like me.

## Things I couldn't understand

1. **Which deck's "Death" am I looking at?** There are 15 different Death cards. As a
   newcomer I didn't know there were *multiple* tarots, so landing on, say, the
   Golden Dawn Scorpio/Hebrew-letter version was baffling ("why is there a Hebrew
   letter and a planet?"). I needed a plain-language default before the esoteric
   detail.
2. **Is it telling my fortune or teaching me history?** The site does both and I
   couldn't tell which it wanted to be for *me*. The honest "it was a game" message
   is interesting but slightly deflating when I came for a little magic.

## Verdict / for the team to assess

- **The reassurance a newcomer needs exists** (Death = transformation) and the
  honest origin story is a genuine, friendly hook.
- **But the path to it is too long for a casual visitor.** The two highest-impact
  fixes for this persona: **(a) add a search box** ("find a card") and **(b) a
  plain-language layer** on each card that comes *before* the esoteric/correspondence
  detail — so "Death = change, not dying" is the first thing a newcomer reads, with
  the Hebrew letters and Scorpio available but secondary.
- **Consider a "Start here / What is tarot?" entry** distinct from the historian
  navigation, and make "Draw a Spread" lead somewhere that explains the cards it
  drew.
