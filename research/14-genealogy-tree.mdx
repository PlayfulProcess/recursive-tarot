# The Tree of Tarot — Genealogy

## At a glance

The meta-grammar whose *items are the decks*. It maps descent using Michael Dummett's
**A/B/C trump-order** classification — the single most useful tool for telling real
lineage from romantic legend. The headline correction it encodes: **Marseille and the
Bolognese Tarocchino are cousins, not parent-and-child.**

## The three orders

Dummett noticed the early Italian decks number/sequence their trumps in three regional
patterns. The differences (especially where the Virtues and the World/Angel sit) are
stable enough to act as a fingerprint of descent.

- **A-order — Bologna / Florence.** Tarocchino di Bologna · Minchiate · Tarocco
  Siciliano.
- **B-order — Ferrara.** "Charles VI" (Gringonneur) · the d'Este cards.
- **C-order — Milan / the West.** Visconti & Cary-Yale → **Tarot de Marseille** → the
  whole occult lineage (Court de Gébelin → Etteilla → Lévi → Wirth → Golden Dawn → RWS).

**Sui generis:** Sola Busca (a learned offshoot, not a main trunk). **Not a tarot at
all:** the Mantegna "Tarocchi."

## The genealogy in one diagram

```
Italian trionfi (c.1440s)
├─ A-order (Bologna/Florence) ── Tarocchino · Minchiate · Siciliano
├─ B-order (Ferrara) ─────────── "Charles VI" · d'Este
└─ C-order (Milan/West) ──── Visconti / Cary-Yale ──► Tarot de Marseille ──► occult decks
   (Sola Busca = sui-generis offshoot)
```

## Why the tree matters for the "fear" and "suppression" questions

The tree is also a map of **when meaning was added**. Everything left of the Marseille is
**game** — no divination, no fear beyond gambling-moralism. Everything downstream of
1781 is **overlay** — theory (de Gébelin), then practice (Etteilla), then esoteric system
(Wirth, Golden Dawn), then mass culture (RWS). Reading the tree left-to-right is reading
the moment the cards acquired the "occult" reputation that made some users proud
(salon esotericists) and others prosecutable (commercial, often Romani, fortune-tellers).

The tree, kept honest, is the best defense against **both** errors: the popular myth that
tarot was *always* esoteric (it wasn't), and the over-correction that there is *no*
real history of suppressed practice (there is — just downstream of the game, and mostly
about who was allowed to read cards for money).
