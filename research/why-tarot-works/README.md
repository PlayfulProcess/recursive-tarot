# Why Tarot "Works" — research

Research workspace on the mechanisms behind a tarot reading feeling meaningful or
beneficial, and the intentions/stances that support that benefit. Feeds the
history-of-tarot course and the **why-it-works lenses** / mirror-vs-divination thread
that serve the project creed (`voices.json` → `shared_intention`: *read to know
yourself, not to be told your fate; relate to the card, never obey it*).

## The question

How and why does tarot "work" — what makes a reading meaningful/beneficial, and what
intention/stance helps? Investigated across four frames:

1. **Psychological & therapeutic mechanisms** — projection / projective hypothesis
   (Rorschach, TAT); Jungian synchronicity & active imagination; Barnum/Forer & cold
   reading; narrative therapy; what an art / expressive-arts therapist says about
   image & archetype cards; SoulCollage / phototherapy / bibliotherapy parallels;
   ritual & placebo/expectancy; randomness to bypass the conscious mind (Oblique
   Strategies, aleatory creativity).
2. **Scientific & skeptical view** — confirmation bias, subjective validation,
   apophenia, illusory pattern perception under low control (Whitson & Galinsky); the
   honest split between *divinatory accuracy* (no evidence) and *psychological
   usefulness as a structured reflection tool* (plausible mechanisms).
3. **Intention & stance** — mirror/reflective prompt vs. divination; "asking the
   cards"; the somatic "chill in the spine"; open vs. closed questions; ethics.
4. **Divination in religious traditions** — protocol / who / purpose / theology of
   randomness across I Ching, Ifá, Delphi, Urim & Thummim and casting lots, runes,
   Roman augury & haruspicy, Chinese oracle bones, Tibetan Mo, Christian sortes.
