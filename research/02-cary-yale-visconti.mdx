# Cary-Yale Tarot (Visconti di Modrone)

## At a glance

An even earlier, extravagantly expanded Milanese game deck — possibly the oldest tarot
fragment of all — notable for **six** ranks of court cards and for trumps depicting the
three **theological virtues** (Faith, Hope, Charity) found almost nowhere else. A
court **game** object, again from the Visconti world, again with no divinatory use.

## Origin & dating

Held at Yale's Beinecke Library (the "Cary" of Cary-Yale; also called *Visconti di
Modrone*). Giordano Berti dates it **c.1442–1447** on numismatic grounds: the coin
(*denari*) cards reproduce the gold florin struck by Filippo Maria Visconti in 1442 and
withdrawn at his death in 1447. If correct, this is among the very earliest surviving
tarot material.

## Provenance & evidence

A fragmentary set (surviving cards number in the dozens). Its structural oddities are the
key evidence: **six courts** (King, Queen, Knight, Jack plus a "Damsel" and a mounted
"Lady"), and **theological-virtue trumps** that otherwise appear only in the much later
Minchiate. This suggests the early trump set was not yet standardised — different courts
experimented with how many triumphs to include.

## Game or divination?

**Game.** The expansions are about enriching play and flattering a dynasty, not encoding
secret doctrine. The virtue cards are conventional Christian moral allegory, the common
visual currency of Renaissance courts.

## The fear question

**No fear** — same logic as the Visconti-Sforza. Elite, heraldic, openly commissioned.
If anything the Cary-Yale is *more* ostentatious (more gold, more courts), the opposite
of something hidden.

## Counter-voices

Because the Cary-Yale shares the theological virtues with Minchiate, some esoteric
readers treat it as evidence of a lost "complete" sacred system later censored down to
22 trumps. The simpler, better-supported reading is the reverse: the early trump set was
**unfixed**, and standardisation (not censorship) trimmed it over time.

## Open questions / corrections owed

- Whether the virtues *replaced* standard trumps or were *additional* is unsettled.
- The c.1442 date leans on the florin argument; not all scholars accept it as decisive.
