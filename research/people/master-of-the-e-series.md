# Master of the E-series (Mantegna Tarocchi engraver)

## At a glance

The **Master of the E-series** is the conventional name given to the anonymous North
Italian engraver who produced the **earlier and finer set** of the so-called
*Mantegna Tarocchi* — a sequence of **50 didactic engravings** depicting a cosmic
hierarchy from the lowest social estate (the beggar) to the highest sphere (the First
Mover). The name "E-series" comes from the series designation used in early print
catalogues; the companion *S-series* is a slightly later copy, probably by a different
hand [@web_mantegna_wp; @web_mantegna_wopc].

**The attribution to Andrea Mantegna is wrong** — this has been the consensus of art
historians since the early 20th century — but the label persists as a convenient
shorthand.

## Life & context

Nothing is known about the master's identity. The engravings belong to a distinctive
tradition of **North Italian line engraving** in the 1460s: firm, regular cross-hatching,
clean contours, classical figure types derived from ancient sculpture and from the
humanist visual culture of **Ferrara and Padua**. The intellectual programme — a
Neoplatonic ladder of being from social estates through arts and sciences to cosmic
spheres — suggests someone steeped in **Ferrarese humanist court culture**, possibly
connected to the **Este court** at Ferrara, which was simultaneously the patron of the
Este tarot and a major centre of humanist learning [@web_mantegna_wp].

Proposed attributions over the years include:
- A Ferrarese engraver close to the Este court (the most common scholarly position)
- A Paduan artist influenced by Mantegna's circle (hence the persistent misattribution)
- A Venetian printmaker connected to early Venetian book illustration

None of these has been conclusively established.

## What they made / did

- **The E-series Mantegna Tarocchi** (c. 1460–1470, 50 plates): five decades of ten
  engravings each —
  1. **Conditions of Man** (beggar → pope / emperor): social estates
  2. **Apollo and the Nine Muses**: the arts of inspiration
  3. **Liberal Arts** (grammar, logic, rhetoric, geometry, arithmetic, music, poetry,
     philosophy, theology): scholastic learning
  4. **Genii, Virtues and Sciences** (genius, memory, justice, temperance…): moral virtues
  5. **Planets and Spheres** (Moon through Saturn, eighth sphere, Prime Mover, First Cause):
     the Ptolemaic cosmos [@web_mantegna_wopc; @web_mantegna_wp]
- The engravings were widely copied, circulated in sets, and used as **model-books** by
  other artists. Several later tarot decks borrow figure types from the Mantegna series.

## The claim vs. the record

- **The myth**: Andrea Mantegna engraved this deck. **The record**: rejected by art
  historical consensus. Mantegna's engraving style is known from authenticated works
  (the *Battle of the Sea Gods*, the *Bacchanals*) and is markedly different in quality
  and technique from the E-series. The misattribution entered the literature through an
  18th-century manuscript note and was repeated until the 20th century
  [@web_mantegna_wp; @dummett1980].
- **The programme**: not a tarot in any game-playing sense — the 50 cards do not map
  onto tarot suits or the standard trump hierarchy. They are a **didactic series in the
  tradition of medieval cosmic hierarchies** (estates of man, liberal arts, planetary
  spheres), closer to an illustrated encyclopaedia than a card game.

## Why this matters for this collection

The Master of the E-series produced **the most intellectually systematic visual programme
in 15th-century Italian printmaking** connected to tarot's sphere. Understanding the
Neoplatonic ladder encoded in these 50 plates illuminates the intellectual world from
which the early tarot trumps also emerged — both are products of the same court humanist
culture that valued hierarchical allegory as a thinking tool.
