# Gertrude Moakley (1905–1998)

## At a glance

A cataloguer at the New York Public Library for some four decades, Gertrude Moakley produced
the decisive iconographic insight of modern tarot history: the 22 trumps are a **procession
of triumphs** in the manner of **Petrarch's _I Trionfi_** and the carnival *trionfo* — Love,
Chastity, Death, Fame, Time, Eternity — not a coded Egyptian or kabbalistic book. Her reading
grounded the trumps in the actual visual culture of 15th-century Italy.

## Life & context

Born 1905, died 1998. She worked within the NYPL system and studied the Pierpont
Morgan–Bergamo (Visconti-Sforza) cards, then believed the oldest surviving tarot. Her thesis
appeared first as an article and then as a book.

## What they made / did

- **"The Tarot Trumps and Petrarch's _Trionfi_: Some Suggestions on their Relationship"**,
  *Bulletin of the New York Public Library* (1956) [@moakley1956].
- **_The Tarot Cards Painted by Bonifacio Bembo for the Visconti-Sforza Family: An
  Iconographic and Historical Study_** (NYPL, 1966) [@moakley1966] — card-by-card iconography
  of the Visconti-Sforza pack and the Petrarchan/triumph reading.

## The claim vs the record

Moakley **dissolved** the occult origin-myth on iconographic grounds before Dummett
systematised the documentary case: the trumps are intelligible as Renaissance triumph-imagery,
so no Egyptian or Hebrew key is required to explain them. Her attribution to **Bonifacio
Bembo** is itself now hedged (the Zavattari workshop is a rival candidate) — preserve the
hedge.

## Connections

Predecessor and complement to **michael-dummett** (iconography + his game-history together
form the document-first case). Her subject is **bonifacio-bembo**'s Visconti-Sforza pack and
the related **cary-yale** deck; patrons **visconti-sforza** family.

## How they appear in this collection

Not a maker — the **iconographer**. Cited in the Visconti-Sforza and Cary-Yale dossiers and
in the trump-meaning notes wherever the "triumph procession" reading is invoked.

## Open questions / corrections owed

- Her Bembo attribution and her assumption that the Pierpont-Morgan pack is *the* oldest are
  both now qualified by later scholarship (Cary-Yale c. 1442). Keep those hedges in the
  dossiers that lean on her.
