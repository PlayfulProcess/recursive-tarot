# Éliphas Lévi (Alphonse-Louis Constant)

## At a glance

**Éliphas Lévi** — born **Alphonse-Louis Constant** (8 February 1810 – 31 May 1875) — is the
hinge of the *modern* occult tarot. In *Dogme et rituel de la haute magie* (two volumes,
**1854 and 1856**) he was the first to **systematically** weld the **22 tarot trumps to the 22
letters of the Hebrew alphabet** and to the Kabbalah, embedding the tarot in a complete magical
philosophy. He **designed no deck** of his own — but everything downstream (Wirth and de Guaita
in France, the Golden Dawn and Waite–Smith in England) builds on the framework he set. He is the
pivot from the 18th-century salon theorists (Gébelin, Mellet) to the 1889 occult deck
[@decker2002; @tarot_heritage; @levi1856].

## Life & context

Constant was born in Paris on 8 February 1810, the **son of a shoemaker**. Educated for the
Church, he entered the seminary of **Saint-Sulpice** (1832) to train for the Catholic
priesthood but **left roughly a week before ordination**. A failed marriage and the death of a
young daughter preceded his turn to magic and mysticism; from the **1850s** he reinvented
himself as the magus "Éliphas Lévi," the pen name being a **Hebraised rendering of "Alphonse
Louis."** He gathered around him much of the occultist and Kabbalist milieu of mid-century Paris
and died there on 31 May 1875 [@web_levi_wp; @tarot_heritage].

## What they made / did

- **`Dogme et rituel de la haute magie` (1854/1856; trans. A. E. Waite as *Transcendental
  Magic*).** Lévi's foundational work, published in two volumes — *Dogme* (1854) and *Rituel*
  (1856). It is the text in which tarot is fused with high magic [@levi1856; @web_levi_wp].
- **The tarot–Hebrew–Kabbalah system.** Lévi assigned **each of the 22 trumps a Hebrew letter**
  and located the cards on the Kabbalistic **Tree of Life**, treating the tarot as a complete
  key to the Western esoteric tradition. This is the move the Comte de Mellet had only sketched
  in 1781; Lévi made it a *system* [@decker2002; @tarot_heritage].
- **The TARO/ROTA word-play.** Lévi linked *tarot* to the Latin **ROTA** ("wheel," as in *rota
  fortunae*) and the Tetragrammaton, noting that ROTA read cyclically yields TARO(T) — an
  etymological flourish that became gospel in esoteric circles (and which the *documentary*
  record does **not** support as a real derivation) [@web_levi_rota; @decker2002].
- **The Baphomet image.** His famous drawing of the "Sabbatic Goat" (Baphomet) accompanies the
  work and shaped later occult iconography — relevant to the **Devil** trump in subsequent decks
  [@web_levi_wp].
- **No deck.** Lévi **never designed or published a tarot deck.** His influence is entirely
  textual and conceptual; the *cards* embodying his ideas were drawn by others — first **Oswald
  Wirth** under **Stanislas de Guaita** (1889) [@web_levi_rota; @decker2002].

## The claim vs. the record

- **Myth: the tarot–Hebrew–Kabbalah correspondence is an ancient, recovered key.** **The
  record:** Lévi's specific assignment of trumps to Hebrew letters is an **invention of the
  1850s**, an interpretive construction laid over a 15th-century Italian card game — not a
  medieval survival. It is powerful and generative, but its antiquity is asserted, not
  documented [@decker2002; @dummett1980].
- **Myth: "TAROT comes from Egyptian/Hebrew *ROTA*."** **The record:** the ROTA/TARO word-play
  is a mnemonic conceit Lévi popularised, not an etymology supported by the history of the word
  *tarocchi/tarot* [@web_levi_rota; @decker2002].
- **He is honest about being a *builder*, not always about being a *recoverer*.** Unlike the
  flat-false Egyptian-origin claim, Lévi's contribution is genuinely original synthesis; the
  caveat is only that he framed his new system as ancient wisdom. The grammar should record a
  **deliberate, dated esoteric construction (1850s)**, not a transmission [@decker2002].

## Connections

- **`court-de-gebelin` + `comte-de-mellet` → Lévi.** Lévi inherits the Egyptian-origin
  atmosphere (Gébelin) and, decisively, **Mellet's** 22-trumps ↔ 22-Hebrew-letters idea, which
  he expands into a full Kabbalistic system [@decker2002; @web_place_mellet].
- **Lévi → `oswald-wirth` via Stanislas de Guaita.** De Guaita, steeped in Lévi, **directed
  Oswald Wirth** to draw the 22 trumps "restored" along Lévi's lines; the 1889 Wirth deck is the
  first cards to *embody* Lévi's program. This is the teacher→patron→illustrator chain the
  collection should make explicit: **Lévi (text) → de Guaita (direction) → Wirth (deck)**
  [@decker2002; @web_wirth_wp].
- **Lévi → the Golden Dawn → Waite–Smith.** In England his system was adopted (with
  modifications — different letter assignments, the Fool repositioned) by the Hermetic Order of
  the Golden Dawn, and through it by A. E. Waite and Pamela Colman Smith (1909–10). Note the
  systems **diverge** and must not be conflated [@decker2002; @waite1910].

## How they appear in this collection

Lévi carries **no deck slug** (he made no cards) but is a **central person node**: the author
of the correspondence system the esoteric decks rely on. He should feature wherever the
collection narrates the **trump↔Hebrew-letter** mapping and the **Tree of Life** overlay — and
specifically behind the **Wheel of Fortune** (his ROTA/TARO play), the **Devil/Baphomet**
imagery, and as the named source "restored" by the **`oswald-wirth-tarot`** deck. A "people of
tarot" card should frame him as **the man who made tarot Kabbalistic — in the 1850s, by
construction.**
