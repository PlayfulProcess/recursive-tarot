# The Este of Ferrara (ducal patrons)

## At a glance

The **Este dynasty** ruled Ferrara from 1240 to 1597 and were among the most important
**early tarot patrons** in documented history. Their court at Ferrara was a centre of
**Quattrocento humanist culture** — the same milieu that produced the Mantegna Tarocchi
and the Ferrarese style of luxury card-making — and the **Este tarot** (c. 1441–1461)
is named for them. A **1457 Este inventory** lists tarot cards among the court's
possessions, providing one of the earliest dated references to tarot in any archival
source [@dummett1980; @web_este_wp].

## Key figures

- **Niccolò III d'Este** (1383–1441): the marquis under whose court early tarot luxury
  production almost certainly began. His marriage festivities in 1435 and 1441 are
  associated with card-game commissions in Ferrara.
- **Leonello d'Este** (1407–1450): Niccolò III's son and successor as Marquis; a
  humanist patron educated by Guarino da Verona. The surviving Este tarot cards
  are often attributed to the Leonello period.
- **Borso d'Este** (1413–1471): Leonello's successor, raised to Duke by the Pope
  (1471); the **Tarocchi di Mantegna** programme resonates with the Ferrarese
  court culture of his reign. The 1457 inventory entry falls in Borso's early period.

## What they made / did

- **The Este tarot** (`este-tarot`): a set of surviving hand-painted luxury cards in the
  Ferrarese style, closely related to the Visconti-Sforza tradition but produced at the
  Este court. The 18 surviving cards (Collezione Rothschild, Paris; and other collections)
  show Lombard-influenced figure types but a distinct Ferrarese palette.
- **The 1457 inventory**: the Este court records from that year list *trionfi* (tarot
  cards) among the household effects — one of the earliest dated archival mentions of
  tarot anywhere in Italy [@dummett1980; @pratesi_ferrara].
- **Cultural environment**: the Este court sustained the humanist tradition of
  **allegorical card programmes** that runs from Marziano da Tortona's Visconti deck
  through the Mantegna Tarocchi (Ferrarese attribution) to the Sola Busca's
  Roman-military humanist programme.

## The claim vs. the record

- **Common framing**: "Tarot was invented in Ferrara." **The record**: Ferrara is the
  site of some of the **earliest dated references** to tarot trump cards in archival
  sources, alongside Milan. Both cities have strong claims. Dummett argues for a Milanese
  or Ferrarese origin in the 1420s–1440s; Pratesi's archival work reinforces the early
  Ferrarese evidence. Neither city can claim exclusive invention from the surviving record.
- **The Este tarot attribution**: the surviving cards are attributed to the Este court by
  conventional scholarship but the specific patron (Leonello? Borso?) and date
  (c.1441–1461?) remain uncertain [@dummett1980].
