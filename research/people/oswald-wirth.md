# Oswald Wirth

## At a glance

**Joseph Paul Oswald Wirth** (5 August 1860 – 9 March 1943) was a Swiss-born occultist,
illustrator and Freemason who, under the direction of the Kabbalist **Stanislas de Guaita**,
**drew the first overtly occult tarot deck**: the **22 trumps (Major Arcana only)** of *Les 22
Arcanes du Tarot Kabbalistique*, **1889**. He turned **Éliphas Lévi's** tarot–Hebrew–Kabbalah
program into actual *cards*, and decades later issued a revised, far better-known version
alongside his book *Le Tarot des imagiers du Moyen Âge* (**1927**). He is the Continental
counterpart to the Golden Dawn's English work, and the bridge from Lévi's text to the modern
esoteric deck [@web_wirth_wp; @decker2002; @web_harrington_wirth].

## Life & context

Wirth was born on **5 August 1860 in Brienz, Canton of Bern, Switzerland**, and worked in
France. After dabbling in Theosophy he was **introduced to Stanislas de Guaita in 1887** and
was grateful for de Guaita's introduction to the tarot as a *magical* system; this began roughly
a decade as de Guaita's **secretary, disciple and collaborator**. Both were members of the
**Ordre Kabbalistique de la Rose-Croix**, and Wirth was a committed **Freemason** whose later
writing dwells on Masonic symbolism. He died on **9 March 1943** [@web_wirth_wp; @decker2002].

## What they made / did

- **`oswald-wirth-tarot` — *Les 22 Arcanes du Tarot Kabbalistique* (1889).** Twenty-two
  hand-coloured, gilt-backed trump cards, **designed and published under de Guaita's direction**.
  It follows the **Tarot de Marseille** closely in outline but adds **Kabbalistic, alchemical
  and Hermetic symbolism** and **Hebrew letters**, per **Lévi's** teaching. This is widely cited
  as **the first in the long line of deliberately occult/initiatory decks** — and it is
  **trumps-only**, a meditative/philosophical object rather than a game or a full 78-card pack
  [@web_wirth_wp; @web_22arcanes].
- **The revised deck + *Le Tarot des imagiers du Moyen Âge* (1926/1927).** Wirth issued a
  second, revised and coloured version of the 22 trumps — slightly altering some images (notably
  **La Papesse**) while keeping the gilt grounds and Hebrew lettering — published with his major
  book, dated **1926/1927**. This mature edition is the one most people now know as "the Wirth
  Tarot" [@web_harrington_wirth; @wirth1927; @web_gallica_wirth].
- **Author and Masonic writer.** Beyond the tarot he wrote extensively on Freemasonry and
  symbolism; *Le Tarot des imagiers du Moyen Âge* is as much an interpretive treatise as a deck
  companion [@wirth1927; @web_wirth_wp].

## The claim vs. the record

- **Myth: the deck *restores* an ancient symbolic tarot.** **The record:** the deck is an
  **honest construction**. Wirth openly builds a *new* symbolic system **on top of the
  Marseille**, following **Lévi's 1850s** Hebrew-letter scheme — itself a 19th-century invention.
  Unlike Gébelin's flat-false Egyptian *antiquity* claim, the Wirth deck is transparent about
  being a designed esoteric overlay; the grammar should record it as a **well-documented
  construction (1889 / 1926)**, not a recovered tradition [@decker2002; @web_wirth_wp].
- **Caveat on the correspondence.** Wirth's Hebrew-letter / trump assignments are **Lévi's
  Continental scheme**, which **differs** from the **Golden Dawn's** English system. Do **not**
  conflate the two correspondence systems [@decker2002; @web_wirth_wp].
- **Two distinct editions.** The **1889** de-Guaita-directed deck and Wirth's **1926** mature
  revision are different objects (e.g. the revised La Papesse) and should be distinguished in
  the grammar [@web_harrington_wirth; @web_wirth_wp].

## Connections

- **`eliphas-levi` → Wirth, via Stanislas de Guaita.** This is the spine of the dossier: **Lévi
  (text, 1850s) → de Guaita (patron/director) → Wirth (deck, 1889).** De Guaita, steeped in
  Lévi, commissioned and directed Wirth's drawings; Wirth's 1926 book is dedicated to him. De
  Guaita died young (1861–1897) [@decker2002; @web_wirth_wp].
- **Wirth ← `court-de-gebelin` + `comte-de-mellet` (distant ancestors).** The Egyptian-origin
  myth (Gébelin) and the trump↔Hebrew idea (Mellet) flow down through Lévi into Wirth's cards —
  Wirth is where that century-long theoretical line **first becomes a printed deck**
  [@decker1996; @decker2002].
- **Wirth vs. the Golden Dawn / Waite–Smith (parallel, not parent).** Wirth's trumps-only
  Continental deck is the **sibling** of the English esoteric work; Waite–Smith (1909–10) is a
  separate, full-78 line with different correspondences. Treat them as cousins, not as a single
  tradition [@decker2002; @waite1910].

## How they appear in this collection

Wirth is the maker of the deck slug **`oswald-wirth-tarot`** (1889 / 1926, **22 trumps only**).
He should feature on the trumps where his esoteric overlay is most visible — the **Magician**
(alchemical/Hermetic emblems), **La Papesse** (the card he most revised between editions), the
**Hebrew-lettered** trumps generally, and the **Wheel of Fortune** (Lévi's ROTA program made
visual). In a "people of tarot" set he is **"the man who first drew the occult tarot,"** the
illustrator who turned Lévi's theory into cards under de Guaita's direction.
