# Filippo Maria Visconti (1392–1447)

## At a glance

**Filippo Maria Visconti** was the **third and last Visconti Duke of Milan** (r. 1412–1447),
son of Gian Galeazzo Visconti who built the dynasty to its greatest extent. He is the
**patron behind the earliest documented tarot trump programme**: his secretary
**Marziano da Tortona** designed a bespoke sixteen-trump deck for him, and the luxury
hand-painted Visconti tarots — though made after his death for the Sforza who succeeded
him — belong to the world his court created [@dummett1980; @pratesi_marziano].

## Life & context

Filippo Maria was a **reclusive, paranoid, and cultivated** ruler — his biographer
**Pier Candido Decembrio** records him as an obsessive card player who kept
**humanist scholars** at his court and had a morbid fear of being seen
[@web_filippo_maria_wp]. He was physically large, reluctant to appear in public,
and delegated much of his military affairs to his generals — including the condottiere
**Francesco Sforza**, who would eventually succeed him by marrying his illegitimate
daughter **Bianca Maria**.

His court at the **Castello Visconteo in Pavia** and the Castello Sforzesco in Milan was
a centre of **Lombard humanist culture**: the environment that produced Marziano's
treatise, and that sustained the Bembo/Zavattari workshops whose paintings are the
headwaters of surviving tarot.

## What they made / did

- **Commissioned Marziano da Tortona's trump programme** (c. 1418–1425): sixteen Roman
  gods paired with birds, arranged in a hierarchical sequence. The first documented tarot
  trump design by a named person for a named patron [@pratesi_marziano; @dummett1980].
- **Sustained the court culture** that produced the luxury hand-painted packs. The
  Visconti-Sforza and Cary-Yale decks were likely made for his successors (the Sforza),
  but the iconographic tradition — Lombard miniature painting at the service of dynastic
  display — is his inheritance.
- His personal card collection: Decembrio records that Filippo Maria owned multiple decks
  and played cards intensively [@web_filippo_maria_wp].

## The claim vs. the record

- **Common assumption**: "The Visconti tarot" was made for Filippo Maria Visconti.
- **The precise record**: The surviving luxury packs (Visconti-Sforza, Cary-Yale) are more
  plausibly dated **after 1450** and associated with the Sforza who succeeded him. The
  Cary-Yale deck is sometimes linked to the **1441 marriage** of his daughter Bianca Maria
  to Francesco Sforza. Filippo Maria himself commissioned the earlier *Marziano* trump
  series, which did not survive physically.
- **Scholarly consensus** (Dummett, Pratesi): Filippo Maria is the earliest documented
  tarot patron, but the surviving luxury decks are Sforza-era objects, not his.
