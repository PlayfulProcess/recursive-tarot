# Stuart R. Kaplan (1932–2021)

## At a glance

Stuart Kaplan founded **U.S. Games Systems** (1968) and, more than anyone, drove tarot's
late-20th-century popular revival in the United States. As a collector-cataloguer he compiled
the four-volume **_Encyclopedia of Tarot_**, the most comprehensive descriptive catalogue of
historical decks; as a publisher he reissued the **Rider-Waite-Smith** deck and many others.

## Life & context

Born 1932, died 2021. A Wharton-trained businessman, he encountered tarot at the 1968
Nuremberg Toy Fair, licensed and republished the Rider-Waite deck for the US, and spent
decades acquiring, documenting, and reproducing decks.

## What they made / did

- **_The Encyclopedia of Tarot_**, Vols I–IV (1978, 1986, 1990, 2005) [@kaplan1978;
  @kaplan1986; @kaplan1990] — the standard reference for deck identification and provenance.
- Republished the Rider-Waite-Smith deck (giving Pamela Colman Smith renewed public credit in
  later years) and many historical reproductions through U.S. Games Systems.

## The claim vs the record

Kaplan is a **catalogue** authority more than an origins theorist; his Encyclopedia is
descriptive and is used here for *what a deck is and where copies are*, not for contested
origin claims (for those, defer to the Dummett spine). His commercial framing sometimes
carries the occult tradition's self-description — read those passages as tradition, not as
documented history.

## Connections

As publisher he is the modern link to **pamela-colman-smith** and **a-e-waite** (the RWS
deck) and to the wider market for the **Golden Dawn** lineage. Institution:
**U.S. Games Systems**.

## How they appear in this collection

Not a historical maker — a **cataloguer/publisher**. Cited where a dossier needs deck
identification, provenance of surviving copies, or the modern publication history of the RWS
line.
