# Bonifacio Bembo

## At a glance

**Bonifacio Bembo** was a mid-15th-century painter from a family of artists active in
**Cremona** and **Milan**, documented in the service of the Sforza dukes from roughly the
late 1440s into the 1470s [@web_bembo_wp]. He matters to tarot because the great
hand-painted **Visconti-Sforza** luxury packs — the headwaters of surviving tarot — are
**traditionally attributed to Bembo or his workshop** [@web_bembo_wp; @web_met_bembo;
@web_wopc_vs]. The attribution is **load-bearing but not secure**: the rival candidate is
the **Zavattari** workshop (Francesco Zavattari), and even Bembo's wider oeuvre has been
questioned by art historians [@web_vs_wp; @web_bembo_wp]. Treat "Bembo" as the conventional
label for the painter(s) behind these decks, not a settled fact.

## Life & context

Bembo belonged to a Cremonese family of painters and was active from **c. 1442**; he is
believed to have died **before 1482** [@web_bembo_wp]. He worked mainly in Cremona but was
**patronised by the Sforza**, for whom he is documented producing court work — including
portraits of **Francesco Sforza** and his wife **Bianca Maria Visconti** [@web_bembo_wp].
His milieu is the Milanese ducal court of the mid-Quattrocento: a world of tooled gold
leaf, dynastic heraldry, and luxury miniature painting, exactly the register of the
surviving Visconti-Sforza cards [@web_wopc_vs]. (confidence: medium — biographical dates
are floruit-based and his secure body of work is small.)

## What they made / did

- **`visconti-sforza-tarot`** — the best-known luxury pack (the Pierpont-Morgan–Bergamo /
  Colleoni-Baglioni deck, c. 1450–1460), traditionally attributed to **the workshop of
  Bonifacio Bembo** [@web_met_bembo; @web_wopc_vs; @web_vs_wp]. The Morgan holds part of
  the deck; other cards are in the Accademia Carrara and Colleoni collection in **Bergamo**
  [@web_wopc_vs; @morgan_visconti].
- **`cary-yale-visconti-tarot`** — the **Cary-Yale / Visconti di Modrone** pack, the most
  extravagant of the three luxury sets, also attributed to Bembo; its **67 surviving cards**
  are in the Cary Collection at Yale's **Beinecke** library, and the deck is commonly tied
  to the **1441 marriage** of Bianca Maria Visconti and Francesco Sforza
  [@web_beinecke_visconti; @web_cary_yale; @beinecke_cary].

## The claim vs. the record

- **The traditional claim — "painted by Bonifacio Bembo."** This is the attribution
  enshrined by **Gertrude Moakley**, whose 1966 study is literally titled *The Tarot Cards
  Painted by Bonifacio Bembo for the Visconti-Sforza Family* [@moakley1966]. It originates
  earlier: **Roberto Longhi** proposed Bembo in 1928, and the attribution was adopted by
  Wittgens (1936) and Rasmo (1939) [@web_vs_wp].
- **The rival claim — the Zavattari workshop.** **Giuliana Algeri** argued that the
  Brambilla and Visconti-Sforza packs were painted instead by **Francesco Zavattari**, on
  the grounds of stylistic resemblance to the Zavattari frescoes in the Chapel of
  Theodelinda at **Monza** [@web_vs_wp]. The Metropolitan Museum hedges by cataloguing its
  cards under **"Workshop of Bonifacio Bembo"** rather than Bembo's own hand
  [@web_met_bembo].
- **The honest position:** the cards are firmly **Milanese court work of the mid-15th
  century**, but the *individual hand* is genuinely debated. State the debate; do not assert
  Bembo as fact [@web_vs_wp; @web_bembo_wp].

## Connections

- **Patrons:** `visconti-sforza-patrons` — Filippo Maria Visconti, Francesco Sforza, Bianca
  Maria Visconti, who paid for the decks Bembo's workshop is credited with painting.
- **Iconographer:** `gertrude-moakley`, whose 1966 book both attributes the cards to Bembo
  *and* whose attribution she and later scholars came to hedge against the Zavattari
  alternative [@moakley1966].
- **Document-historian:** `michael-dummett`, who places these packs as the earliest
  surviving witnesses of the game of *trionfi*, independent of the attribution question
  [@dummett1980].
- **Rival hand (not yet a dossier):** the **Zavattari** workshop (Francesco Zavattari).

## How they appear in this collection

Bembo is the credited **maker** for two deck slugs: **`visconti-sforza-tarot`** and
**`cary-yale-visconti-tarot`**. Wherever either deck's authorship is named, the grammar
should render it as *attributed to the workshop of Bonifacio Bembo (Zavattari proposed as
an alternative)* — never as a flat "by Bembo." He is the maker-node that links to the
`visconti-sforza-patrons` family and to `gertrude-moakley`.

## Open questions / corrections owed

- **Birthplace/dates:** sources differ (some give a Brescia birth c. 1420); the safest
  anchor is the floruit **c. 1442 – d. before 1482** and the Cremonese family of painters
  [@web_bembo_wp]. Confirm against a primary art-historical source.
- **Which hand for which deck:** resolve, if possible, whether Bembo, the Zavattari, or
  multiple workshops are responsible for the Cary-Yale vs. the Pierpont-Morgan packs
  [@web_vs_wp].
- **Workshop vs. master:** even where "Bembo" is accepted, much may be *workshop* rather
  than autograph (the Met's hedge) [@web_met_bembo].
