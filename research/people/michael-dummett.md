# Sir Michael Dummett (1925–2011)

## At a glance

One of the major analytic philosophers of the 20th century (Wykeham Professor of Logic at
Oxford), Michael Dummett was also the most important modern historian of tarot — not as a
divinatory system but as a **trick-taking card game**. His trilogy is the documentary
baseline this collection defaults to: tarot is a 15th-century Italian game; the
occult/Egyptian/divinatory readings are a learned **overlay imposed after c. 1781**.

## Life & context

Dummett (1925–2011) was a philosopher of language and logic, knighted in 1999. A serious
card-game player and collector, he brought the same evidentiary rigour to playing-card
history that he brought to Frege, refusing claims that could not be grounded in dated
objects and documents. His work overturned a century of occultist origin-stories.

## What they made / did

- **_The Game of Tarot: From Ferrara to Salt Lake City_** (1980) [@dummett1980] — the
  foundational survey: where, when, and how the game arose and spread; the A/B/C ordering
  traditions of the trumps.
- **_A Wicked Pack of Cards: The Origins of the Occult Tarot_** (1996, with Ronald Decker
  and Thierry Depaulis) [@decker1996] — traces the occult turn to Court de Gébelin (1781)
  and Etteilla (1788).
- **_A History of the Occult Tarot, 1870–1970_** (2002, with Decker) [@decker2002].
- **_A History of Games Played with the Tarot Pack_** (2004, with John McLeod)
  [@dummett2004].

## The claim vs the record

Dummett **is** the record here. Where a deck's own tradition claims ancient Egyptian,
Hebrew-kabbalistic, or "suppressed wisdom" origins, this collection states the claim fairly
and then weighs it against Dummett's documentary findings. The collection does **not** treat
him as infallible — later scholars (Depaulis, Pratesi, Vitali) refine dates and the A/B/C
picture — but it treats the burden of proof as his framework sets it.

## Connections

Co-authors **Ronald Decker** and **Thierry Depaulis**; built on the iconographic work of
**Gertrude Moakley** (Petrarch's *Trionfi*). His skeptical conclusions are the foil against
which the occultists in this collection (**court-de-gebelin**, **etteilla**, **eliphas-levi**,
**oswald-wirth**, the **Golden Dawn**) are measured.

## How they appear in this collection

Not a maker — a **lens**. He is cited across nearly every deck dossier as the document-first
authority (`[@dummett1980]`, `[@decker1996]`, `[@dummett2004]`). He does not "feature" in
card art; he features in the collection's *method*.
