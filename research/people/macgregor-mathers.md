# Samuel Liddell MacGregor Mathers

## At a glance

**Samuel Liddell MacGregor Mathers** (8 January **1854** – November **1918**) was the creative
engine of the Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn and the **chief author of its tarot teaching,
Book T**. While **Westcott** supplied the Cipher Manuscripts and the order's (forged) pedigree,
Mathers wrote most of the rituals and built the **correspondence system** — Hebrew letters,
Tree-of-Life paths, and the astrological/decanic attributions — that still underlies how almost
everyone reads tarot today. He produced **no published deck**; his cards survived as a lodge
specimen to be hand-copied by initiates [@web_mathers_wp; @decker1996; @tarot_heritage].

## Life & context

Mathers was born in **Hackney, London, on 8 January 1854** and died in **Paris in November 1918**
(sources give 5 or 20 November; his death certificate lists no cause, so the common "Spanish flu"
attribution is inference, not record) [@web_mathers_wp]. A Freemason and member of the
Rosicrucian Society (S.R.I.A.), he was an autodidact steeped in Kabbalah, ritual magic and
comparative religion, and styled himself "MacGregor" and "Comte de Glenstrae" out of romantic
Jacobite-Highland identification. From the 1890s he lived mainly in Paris with his wife **Moïna
Mathers** (the artist Mina Bergson, sister of the philosopher Henri Bergson), running the Order's
"Ahathôor" temple and its Paris-based successor, **Alpha et Omega**, after the 1900 schism
[@decker1996; @decker2002].

## What they made / did

- **Book T (Liber T): The Tarot (c.1891).** The Order's confidential instructional paper on the
  cards, **written anonymously around 1891, in the main by Mathers**. It set out the trump
  attributions, the **Strength↔Justice (VIII↔XI) swap**, the **decanic** meanings of the pips,
  and the elemental court structure — the foundational document for all Golden-Dawn-based decks,
  RWS and Thoth included [@web_mathers_wp; @web_gd_swap].
- **Translations that fed the system.** *The Kabbalah Unveiled* (1887, from Knorr von Rosenroth's
  Latin *Kabbala Denudata*), *The Key of Solomon the King* (1889) and *The Book of the Sacred
  Magic of Abramelin the Mage* (1898) — the scholarly apparatus that gave the Order its Kabbalistic
  and magical vocabulary [@web_mathers_wp].
- **The Order's rituals.** Mathers rewrote the skeletal Cipher rituals into the Order's graded
  initiations on the Tree of Life — the ceremonial frame in which the tarot teaching sat
  [@decker1996].

## The claim vs. the record

- **Myth: Book T transmits an ancient Egyptian/Hermetic tarot key.** **The record:** Mathers'
  attributions are a **constructed synthesis of the 1880s–90s**, built on Lévi's trump↔Hebrew
  scheme and the Order's astrology, then projected backward as ancient wisdom [@decker2002].
- **Precision: he authored a *scheme*, not a marketed deck.** Mathers wrote the correspondences
  and painted a lodge specimen; the cards were **hand-copied in secret**, never published. The
  famous *public* cards are Smith's RWS art — a separate, later object [@web_gd_nodeck].
- **The schism candour.** In 1900 Mathers told Florence Farr that **Westcott had forged the
  Sprengel letters** — effectively admitting the Order's foundation legend was fraudulent, even as
  he defended his own authority [@decker1996].

## Connections

- **`wynn-westcott` + Mathers (+ Woodman) founded `golden-dawn`** (1888); the two were partners
  and later rivals [@decker1996].
- **Mathers wrote Book T**, the teaching behind the `golden-dawn-book-t-tarot` deck
  [@web_mathers_wp].
- **`eliphas-levi` → Mathers:** Mathers inherited and rebuilt Lévi's trump↔Hebrew-letter
  correspondence into the Order's fuller grid [@decker2002].
- **Mathers → `a-e-waite` + `pamela-colman-smith`:** both were Order members who carried his
  system into the public RWS deck [@decker2002; @waite1910].

## How they appear in this collection

Mathers is the **author node** for `golden-dawn-book-t-tarot`: the titles, Hebrew letters, Tree
paths and decanic meanings rendered in that grammar are his. A "people of tarot" leaf should name
him **the architect of the Book-T correspondences** and stress that he built, did not recover,
them — and that he published no deck.
