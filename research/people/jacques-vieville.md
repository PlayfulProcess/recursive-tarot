# Jacques Viéville

## At a glance

**Jacques Viéville** was a Paris cardmaker active around **1650** — the same city and decade
as **Jean Noblet** — but his tarot is **not** in the Marseille line. It belongs to a
**divergent tradition**, the so-called **"Rouen-Brussels" (Rouen-Bruxelles)** pattern, and is
the French **ancestor of the later Belgian / Flemish tarot** [@web_th_rouen_bruxelles;
@web_belgian_wp]. The only known copy is preserved in the **Bibliothèque nationale de France**
[@web_letarot_vieville]. He is, like Noblet, **thinly documented as a person** — known chiefly
through his deck — so this dossier is short and floruit-only (confidence: medium).

## Life & context

Viéville's mid-17th-century print shop stood in the **same Parisian neighbourhood** as Jean
Noblet's and as that of the printer of the anonymous *Tarot de Paris* [@web_th_rouen_bruxelles].
His is therefore a near-twin in **time and place** to Noblet — but a **rival lineage** in
**design**. Biographical specifics (birth, death, workshop) are essentially undocumented in
the sources consulted; the secure fact is the deck and its BnF survival [@web_letarot_vieville].

## What they made / did

- **`vieville-tarot`** — the **c. 1650 Paris tarot**, surviving in a single copy in the
  **BnF**, that founds (or shares a common ancestor with) the **Rouen-Brussels** tradition
  later carried by Belgian cardmakers; the earliest dated maker of that pattern is **Gérard
  Bodet of Liège, 1693**, who appears to have copied a Viéville deck or shared its source
  [@web_th_rouen_bruxelles; @web_belgian_wp].

## How it differs from the Marseille line

This is the precise point of the dossier — Viéville's pack diverges from the Marseille
standard in several documented ways:

- **A different trump tradition.** Per **Dummett**, the tarots of **Viéville**, Adam de
  Hautot, and the 18th-c. Brussels cardmakers form a tradition **distinct from the Tarot de
  Marseille**, inherited more or less directly from **Bologna** via **Piedmont and Savoy** —
  not from the Marseille line [@web_th_rouen_bruxelles; @dummett1980].
- **Re-ordered virtue trumps.** Justice, the Chariot, Strength (Force) and the Hermit are
  **ordered differently** from the Marseille sequence [@web_aeclectic_vieville].
- **The Tower is a tree / lightning.** Where Marseille shows a struck tower (*La Maison Dieu*),
  Viéville's card (and the Belgian tradition) shows a **tree struck by lightning**, often named
  ***La Foudre / La Fouldre*** ("the Lightning") [@web_th_rouen_bruxelles; @web_belgian_wp].
- **The Hanged Man upright.** The Hanged Man appears **upright** rather than inverted
  [@web_aeclectic_vieville].
- **Divergent World / Sun imagery.** The World and Sun cards differ from the Marseille
  treatment, in line with the broader Belgian pattern [@web_aeclectic_vieville].

## The claim vs. the record

- **Claim: "a variant Tarot de Marseille."** **The record:** it is better described as a
  **separate pattern** that happens to be contemporary with the earliest Marseille survivor
  (Noblet). Calling it "Marseille" obscures the Bologna-via-Piedmont ancestry Dummett
  identifies; it is the **Rouen-Brussels / Belgian** line, not the Marseille line
  [@dummett1980; @web_th_rouen_bruxelles].

## Connections

- **Near-contemporary, different line:** `jean-noblet` — same Paris milieu and c. 1650, but
  Noblet is the oldest **Marseille** survivor while Viéville founds the **Belgian** divergence
  [@web_th_rouen_bruxelles].
- **Down-stream Belgian makers:** `vandenborre` (F. I. Vandenborre, Brussels, c. 1780), whose
  Belgian/Flemish tarot descends from the Rouen-Brussels tradition Viéville anchors
  [@web_belgian_wp].
- **Document-historian:** `michael-dummett`, whose tradition-mapping (Bologna → Piedmont/Savoy
  → Belgium) places Viéville [@dummett1980].

## How they appear in this collection

Viéville is the maker of **`vieville-tarot`**. In the grammar he is the **counter-example to
the Marseille hegemony**: same time and place as Noblet, different lineage. Render the diff
explicitly — *La Foudre*-tree instead of the struck Tower, upright Hanged Man, re-ordered
virtues — and link him forward to the `vandenborre` Belgian deck as the same family's
18th-century continuation.

## Open questions / corrections owed

- **Dating precision:** "c. 1650" is approximate; confirm Viéville's documented working dates
  against the BnF object record [@web_letarot_vieville].
- **Ancestor vs. cousin of the Belgian line:** whether Bodet (1693) copied Viéville directly
  or both descend from a lost common source is unresolved [@web_th_rouen_bruxelles].
