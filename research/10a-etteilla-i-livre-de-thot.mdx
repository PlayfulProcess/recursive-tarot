# Etteilla I — Livre de Thot

> Part of the Etteilla lineage — see the hub at `10-etteilla.mdx`, then `10b` and `10c`.

## At a glance

The **original** and the genuine watershed: **Jean-Baptiste Alliette** ("Etteilla")
designs and publishes the **first tarot deck built expressly for divination** (1788–89).
Where every prior deck was a game pack later *read* as an oracle, this one was *born* an
oracle. This is the true birth-date of the divinatory tarot **object**.

## Origin & dating

Alliette (1738–1791) read ordinary cards professionally first, then — fired by de
Gébelin's 1781 Egyptian theory — built a tarot to match it. His method-books appear
**1783–85**; the deck, the **Grand Etteilla** / *Livre de Thot*, **1788–89**. He claimed
to "restore" the cards to their supposed Egyptian/hieroglyphic original.

## Provenance & evidence

The deck and books survive and are well reconstructed (Tarot Heritage; benebell wen). Its
departures from the Marseille are deliberate and documented:

- **Renumbered and reimagined trumps** — e.g. **Death is renumbered to 17**, breaking the
  standard order entirely.
- **Added cards** — querent/"etteilla" cards and the four **elements** as figures.
- **Upright/reversed keyword meanings** printed into the system — the ancestor of modern
  "card meanings."

## Game or divination?

**Divination — the first true case.** The grammar should mark **1788** as the birth of a
purpose-built divinatory deck (distinct from de Gébelin's 1781 birth of the *theory*).

## The fear question

**Conspicuously unafraid.** Etteilla ran a **"New School of Magic,"** advertised courses
in Paris papers, and signed his books. In late-18th-century Paris, cartomancy was
fashionable and lucrative. The literate male entrepreneur operated in broad daylight —
the sharpest contrast with the prosecuted, working-class fortune-tellers across the
Channel.

## Counter-voices

Etteilla is the original voice of the "recovered suppressed wisdom" story — he sincerely
(and profitably) presented his system as ancient Egyptian. Both things are true at once:
he **founded a real, lasting cartomantic practice** *and* propagated a **false Egyptian
pedigree**. Honour the first; flag the second.
