# The so-called "Mantegna Tarocchi" — a Ladder of Being (NOT a tarot)

## At a glance

A set of **fifty fifteenth-century engravings**, organised in **five decades of ten**, that is
**neither a card game nor a tarot deck nor the work of Andrea Mantegna**. It is a **didactic
image cycle** — a Neoplatonic "ladder of being" rising from the Beggar (1) to the First Cause
(50). It is in this library precisely to **explain a confusion**: later writers pinned the name
"Tarocchi" on it, and esotericists have repeatedly raided it for "hidden keys" to tarot. There is
no descent: it has no suits, no number cards, no court cards, no trumps, and no game
[@web_mantegna_wopc], [@web_mantegna_met].

## Origin & dating

Two near-identical series survive: the **E-series** (the older, **c. 1465**) and the **S-series**
(**c. 1470–1475**), both probably made in **Ferrara**; the two are by different unknown hands, now
called the **"Master of the E-series Tarocchi"** and the **"Master of the S-series Tarocchi"**
[@web_mantegna_wopc]. The series take their letters from the initial in the lower corner of each
plate [@web_mantegna_intomystery]. The E-series is dated **c. 1465** on documentary grounds: a
**Bolognese manuscript dated 1467** (State Archives, Bologna) contains a miniature closely copying
the print of the *Imperator* [@web_mantegna_intomystery]. The attribution to **Andrea Mantegna is
spurious** — it is a later mislabelling, not a workshop fact [@web_mantegna_wopc].

## Provenance & evidence

Complete impressions survive in major print rooms; the public-domain set used in this library is
the **Cleveland Museum of Art** impression of the E-series (accession 1924.432.1–50)
[@web_mantegna_cleveland]. Individual plates are catalogued by the Met (e.g. *Poetry*, Poesia
XXVII) [@web_mantegna_met] and the Cleveland Museum, which use the standard five-letter grouping.

## Structure

**Fifty engravings, numbered 1–50, in five decades** — and lettered in **reverse alphabetical
order top-to-bottom**, so that the *highest* group carries the *first* letter [@web_mantegna_wopc],
[@web_mantegna_cleveland]:

| Letter | Numbers | Group |
|---|---|---|
| **E** | 1–10 | Conditions / Estates of Man (Beggar → Pope) |
| **D** | 11–20 | Apollo and the Nine Muses |
| **C** | 21–30 | The Liberal Arts & Sciences (trivium + quadrivium + Poetry, Philosophy, Theology) |
| **B** | 31–40 | The three Cosmic Genii + the seven Virtues (4 cardinal + 3 theological) |
| **A** | 41–50 | The seven Planets, the Eighth Sphere, the Primum Mobile, and the First Cause |

It is a **single ascent** — a Renaissance encyclopedia in pictures, a *ladder of being* from the
lowest human condition to the divine origin of the cosmos [@web_mantegna_wopc],
[@web_mantegna_dailyart]. (Note: the three "Genii" of 31–33 — Iliaco/Sun, Chronico/Time,
Cosmico/World — derive from the threefold cosmical/chronical/heliacal rising in the 13th-century
astronomical text *De sphaera* [@web_mantegna_dailyart].)

## Game or divination?

**Neither.** No suits, no trick-taking structure, no fortune-telling. They are best understood as
**educational prints**, plausibly for instructing upper-class youths — though no document records
their actual use [@web_mantegna_wopc], [@web_mantegna_met]. The "Tarocchi" label was attached
later by writers who confused the picture-rich engravings with the tarot card-game (*tarocco*)
[@web_mantegna_wopc].

## What changed from its parent

**Omitted — there is no parent.** The set does not derive from any earlier card pack
(`derives_from: []`); it is a freestanding didactic cycle. Any resemblance between its program and
early *trionfi* trumps is a matter of **shared Neoplatonic culture / possible influence, not
descent** [@web_mantegna_wopc].

## The fear question

**Not applicable** — these are humanist study images, openly produced and circulated. Their
inclusion is corrective: to stop the genealogy absorbing material that merely *looks*
tarot-adjacent.

## Counter-voices

Esoteric authors (e.g. Adam McLean's *An Hermetic Origin of the Tarot Cards*) have repeatedly read
"hidden" Neoplatonic or Hermetic keys to tarot into the series [@web_mantegna_mclean]. The series
*is* genuinely Neoplatonic — that was mainstream learned culture — but it is **not an ancestor of
the tarot pack**. Treating it as one is exactly how false genealogies get built
[@web_mantegna_wopc].

## People

- **andrea-mantegna** — the great Mantuan painter to whom the set is **misattributed**; he did
  **not** make it (the name is a later error) [@web_mantegna_wopc]. He appears here only as the
  (mis)attribution to be corrected.
- **michael-dummett** — the document-first spine; Dummett's separation of the tarot card-game's
  real history from absorbed look-alikes is the method this dossier applies [@dummett1980].
