# Minchiate (Florence) — the 97-card expanded tarot

## At a glance

A Florentine **super-sized tarot game**: **97 cards** — **56 suit cards**, **40 numbered
trumps**, and the unnumbered **Fool (Il Matto)** — i.e. roughly *double* the trump count of the
ordinary 22-trump pack [@web_minchiate_wp; @web_pagat_minchiate; @dummett1980]. To the standard
trump series it **adds the twelve signs of the zodiac, the four classical elements, and the
remaining virtues** — Prudence (the fourth cardinal virtue) plus the three theological virtues
Faith, Hope and Charity [@web_minchiate_wp; @web_williams_minchiate]. It is a flagship of the
**A-order** (Florentine) branch, and despite all the cosmic imagery it is, in every record, a
**trick-taking game**, hugely popular across Italy and into France for three centuries
[@web_pagat_minchiate; @dummett1980]. First recorded as ***germini*** in **1506**; the name
*minchiate* later migrated to this big pack when it eclipsed the older form [@web_minchiate_wp].

The deck this dossier follows for imagery is a **Florentine printed Minchiate of c. 1860–1890**
(the public-domain Wikimedia Commons scan used by the grammar) — a very late survivor of a
pattern essentially fixed by the 18th century.

## Origin & dating

The 97-card expansion is first attested under the name ***germini*** in **1506**, the name taken
from the **Gemini** card (no. XXXV), highest of the newly added signs [@web_minchiate_wp;
@dummett1980]. The older, smaller Florentine pack was called *minchiate*; the expanded pack so
outsold it that the smaller form ceased production and the name *minchiate* transferred to the
big deck during the **17th century** [@web_minchiate_wp]. From Florence the game spread across
Italy (Genoa, Rome, and beyond) and into France through the 1600s–1700s [@web_minchiate_wp;
@web_pagat_minchiate].

The trump *subjects* are older than 1506: the two-Emperor/Grand-Duke arrangement (see Structure)
appears to **predate the fall of Constantinople (1453)**, since it depicts a Western and an
Eastern Emperor as living offices [@web_minchiate_wp] (confidence: medium — an argued inference
from iconography, repeated in the secondary literature, not a dated document).

## Provenance & evidence

The headline evidence is the pack itself: many printed editions survive, and the pattern is
stable enough that 18th–19th-century Florentine *Minchiate Fiorentine* sheets can be read against
each other [@web_steve_minchiate; @web_wopc_etruria_minchiate]. The **game's rules and card-ranks
are preserved in the gaming literature** — most fully in **Dummett & McLeod, *A History of Games
Played with the Tarot Pack* (2004), chapter 12**, and accessibly in **John McLeod's pagat.com**
write-up [@dummett2004; @web_pagat_minchiate]. Dummett's earlier *Il Mondo e l'Angelo* treats the
Florentine printed cards directly [@web_dummett_mondo_ch10]. The grammar's imagery is the
**Florentine Minchiate of c. 1860–1890** on Wikimedia Commons (public domain).

## Structure

**97 cards**, counted as **40 numbered trumps + 56 suit cards + the unnumbered Fool**
[@web_minchiate_wp; @web_pagat_minchiate]. (Some sources phrase the same fact as "**41 trumps
including the Fool**"; the Fool is unnumbered and has no trick-taking power but can be played to
any trick — the *excuse* — so whether one counts it among the trumps is a matter of convention,
not substance [@web_pagat_minchiate].)

**Suit cards (56).** Four Latin suits — **Batons (Bastoni / Fire), Coins (Denari / Earth), Cups
(Coppe / Water), Swords (Spade / Air)** — each Ace–10 plus a **four-card court**: King (Re),
Queen (Regina/Donna), Knight (Cavallo), Page (Fante) [@web_minchiate_wp; @web_pagat_minchiate].
Two peculiarities the grammar correctly preserves: the **knights are centaurs (or sphinxes /
beasts)** rather than ordinary mounted men, and in **Cups and Coins the page is a woman — a
*fantina* (maid)** rather than the usual male *fante* [@web_minchiate_wp]. (Note: this dossier
keeps the repo's suit→element scheme; the Marseille-French convention often pairs Swords→Air and
Batons→Fire as here, but esoteric schemes vary — the elemental tags are a modern overlay, not a
game feature.)

**Trumps (40 numbered + Fool).** The series breaks into recognisable groups, which the grammar
encodes as `trump_group`:

- **The Fool (Il Matto)** — unnumbered, the *excuse*; outside the rank order.
- **The five Papi (I–V)** — the five lowest trumps, collectively called *papi* ("popes"), each
  individually *Papa uno … Papa cinque*. **This is where the Minchiate departs most sharply from
  ordinary tarot, and where the repo grammar is in error** (see Open questions). The corroborated
  scholarly identities are: **I = the Bagatto (the Juggler/Magician); II = the Grand Duke; III =
  the Western Emperor; IV = the Eastern Emperor; V = Love** [@web_minchiate_wp;
  @web_pagat_minchiate; @web_tarotforum_granduke]. There is **no Popess and no Pope** here at all
  (see *What changed*).
- **Three cardinal virtues, low (VI–VIII):** Temperance, Fortitude (Forza), Justice.
- **The standard middle trumps (IX–XV):** Wheel of Fortune, Chariot, the **Hunchback / Time
  (*Il Gobbo*, = the Hermit)**, Hanged Man, Death, the Devil, and the **House of the Devil
  (*La Casa del Diavolo*, = the Tower)** [@web_steve_minchiate; @web_minchiate_wp].
- **The added virtues (XVI–XIX):** **Hope, Prudence, Faith, Charity** — i.e. the missing fourth
  cardinal virtue (Prudence) plus the three theological virtues. With Temperance/Fortitude/Justice
  this gives the Minchiate the **complete set of seven virtues** [@web_minchiate_wp;
  @web_williams_minchiate].
- **The four elements (XX–XXIII):** Fire, Water, Earth, Air [@web_minchiate_wp].
- **The twelve zodiac signs (XXIV–XXXV)** in an **idiosyncratic, non-astrological order**:
  Libra, Virgo, Scorpio, Aries, Capricorn, Sagittarius, Cancer, Pisces, Aquarius, Leo, Taurus,
  Gemini [@web_minchiate_wp; @web_steve_minchiate].
- **The five *Arie* (XXXVI–XL),** the supreme cards: **Star, Moon, Sun, World, and the Trumpets
  (*Le Trombe*, the Last Judgement / Fame)** — *trombe* (no. 40) is the highest trump
  [@web_pagat_minchiate; @web_minchiate_wp].

The **20 new trumps** (Prudence + 3 theological virtues + 4 elements + 12 signs) were essentially
**inserted as a block** above the old Tower, pushing Star/Moon/Sun/World up into the Arie at the
top [@web_minchiate_wp].

## Game or divination?

**Game, unambiguously.** Every record treats these cards as **trump ranks in a trick-taking
game** with elaborate scoring (the *Arie* carry premium points; the Fool is the *excuse*;
*versicole* are scoring sequences) [@web_pagat_minchiate; @dummett2004]. The zodiac, elements and
virtues are **decorative/encyclopedic Renaissance cosmology** — the same visual vocabulary as the
Mantegna prints — not an oracle key. Cartomantic use of Minchiate is a **much later overlay**, so
the card-by-card file describes **iconography and the trump/suit each card is**, not
upright/reversed fortune-telling meanings, which this deck does not natively have.

## What changed from its parent

The Minchiate is best read as a **descendant/expansion of the standard A-order (Florentine)
tarot**, not of any one surviving pack (`derives_from: []` because no single parent deck is in the
repo as its direct ancestor). Against the ordinary 22-trump series it makes three moves:

1. **It drops the Popess and the Pope and recasts the imperial cards.** Where standard tarot has
   Popess, Empress, Emperor, Pope, Love (and a Bagatto/Magician at I), the Minchiate's five Papi
   are **Bagatto, Grand Duke, Western Emperor, Eastern Emperor, Love** — **the Popess is gone
   entirely and the Pope is replaced by a second (Eastern) Emperor**, the figures secularised
   (secular crowns, not papal tiaras) [@web_minchiate_wp; @web_pagat_minchiate;
   @web_tarotforum_granduke]. The standard explanation is **proximity to Rome and Church
   sensitivity** to the Pope/Papess subjects — Florence felt papal influence directly
   [@web_minchiate_wp] (confidence: medium — a plausible, widely-repeated motive, not a documented
   one). Because the five lowest trumps were *all* called *papi*, **Love was demoted into the papi
   group**, and the middle papi identities were so widely forgotten that players simply called
   them by number (pope 2, pope 3 …) [@web_minchiate_wp].
2. **It completes the virtue programme.** It restores **Prudence** (the cardinal virtue missing
   from ordinary tarot) and adds the three **theological virtues** (Faith, Hope, Charity) — giving
   all seven. Among historical packs only the Cary-Yale (three theological virtues) and the
   Minchiate (all seven) treat the trumps as a complete moral cosmos [@web_minchiate_wp;
   @web_williams_minchiate].
3. **It builds in a cosmology with no standard tarot equivalent** — the **four elements** and the
   **twelve zodiac signs** as trumps in their own right. **These have no one-to-one counterpart in
   the 22-trump tarot and must not be normalised onto it** [@web_minchiate_wp; @web_steve_minchiate].

## The fear question

**No fear.** Minchiate was **mainstream parlour and gambling entertainment** for three centuries —
openly printed, sold, and played, with rules recorded in print [@web_pagat_minchiate;
@dummett2004]. The familiar caveat applies (moralists disliked *gambling*, not the imagery), but
the Minchiate as such was respectable. If anything the deck moves *away* from danger: the very
edits that drop the Popess and disguise the Pope as an Emperor read as a **prudent
accommodation** to Counter-Reformation Florence, the opposite of a hidden heresy
[@web_minchiate_wp].

## Counter-voices

Because the Minchiate looks like a "complete" astrological tarot — zodiac! elements! the full
seven virtues! — occult writers sometimes present it as a survival of the *true, larger sacred
system* that the 22-trump decks supposedly censored. **The evidence runs the other way:** the
22-trump form is **older**, and the 40-trump Minchiate is a documented **expansion for richer play
and encyclopedic display**, first attested in 1506 as a Florentine *game* called *germini* — not a
recovered esoteric original [@web_minchiate_wp; @dummett1980]. The cosmology is decorative, in the
ordinary Renaissance idiom (cf. the Mantegna "Tarocchi").

## Open questions / corrections owed

- **GRAMMAR ERROR — the five Papi are mislabelled.** The grammar names the Papi as
  *Papa One*, *Papa Two (The Empress)*, *Papa Three (The Emperor)*, *Papa Four (The Pope)*,
  *Papa Five (Love)*, and its `emg-papi` note says the Minchiate "dissolves the Popess, Empress,
  Emperor, Pope, and Love." The corroborated scholarly identities are **I = Bagatto (Juggler),
  II = Grand Duke, III = Western Emperor, IV = Eastern Emperor, V = Love** — there is **no Pope
  card** among them, and the Popess is **dropped, not folded in** [@web_minchiate_wp;
  @web_pagat_minchiate; @web_tarotforum_granduke]. *Correct the grammar's Papi names/Meanings and
  the `emg-papi` "About these Cards" text.* (confidence: high for the Bagatto/Grand-Duke/two-Emperor
  identification; the older single-source claim that II/III/IV are simply "Empress/Emperor/Pope
  secularised" is a competing, weaker gloss.)
- **GRAMMAR NUANCE — Il Gobbo's mapping.** The grammar maps *Il Gobbo* (the Hunchback, no. XI) to
  `arcana:the-hermit` with `mapping_confidence: exact`. The figure is the standard **Time/Old Man**
  that becomes the Hermit — defensible, but "exact" overstates it; consider "loose," as already
  done for *Le Trombe* → Judgement [@web_steve_minchiate].
- **The two-Emperor dating** ("predates 1453") is an iconographic inference repeated in secondary
  sources, not a dated document — keep the hedge [@web_minchiate_wp].
- **Suit→element pairing** (Swords→Air, Batons→Fire) is a modern overlay carried by the grammar;
  it is not a feature of the game and esoteric schemes disagree. Flag, do not assert as historical.
- **97 vs "41 trumps."** Standardise the phrasing: 40 numbered trumps + Fool + 56 suit cards = 97;
  "41 trumps" counts the Fool [@web_pagat_minchiate].
