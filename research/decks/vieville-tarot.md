# Jacques Viéville Tarot — Paris, c. 1650

## At a glance

A 78-card woodcut tarot printed in **Paris around 1650** by the *maître-cartier* **Jacques
Viéville**, surviving in a **single known copy** at the **Bibliothèque nationale de France**
and digitised on **Gallica** (ark:/12148/btv1b10510963k) [@web_letarot_vieville;
@web_pollett_vieville]. It is the exact contemporary of **Jean Noblet** — same city, same
decade — but it is **not** in the Marseille line. Viéville's pack is the great **outlier** of
the early Parisian tarots: it is the **earliest surviving witness of the divergent pattern**
that Michael Dummett traces to **Bologna, via Piedmont and Savoy**, and that runs forward
into the 18th-century **"Rouen-Brussels" / Belgian (Flemish) tarot** rather than the
Marseille mainstream [@dummett1980; @web_th_rouen_bruxelles; @web_th_french_connection]. It
should **not be called a "Tarot de Marseille"** — and the grammar's current "Marseille
family / branch: marseille" label is the central error this dossier corrects (see *Open
questions*). Like Noblet's deck, it is a **game pack**: a playing-and-trick-taking tarot from
more than a century before cartomancy attached itself to the cards; no divinatory meaning is
native to it [@dummett1980; @dummett2004].

## Origin & dating

Viéville and Noblet "both published a Tarot in Paris circa 1650," and the only known Viéville
copy is conserved at the BnF [@web_letarot_vieville; @web_pollett_vieville]. Precise working
dates for Viéville are undocumented; **"c. 1650"** is the standard approximation, and the
maker is known almost entirely through this object (see `people/jacques-vieville.md`)
(confidence: medium — the *date* rests on style and trade context, not a dated colophon)
[@web_letarot_vieville]. The significance of the dating is comparative: it places a **second,
competing tarot pattern in Paris at the very moment the Marseille standard (Noblet, the
oldest Marseille survivor) was being printed a few streets away** [@web_th_french_connection;
@web_th_rouen_bruxelles].

## Provenance & evidence

The **single surviving copy** is in the **Bibliothèque nationale de France** and is fully
public-domain on **Gallica** (ark:/12148/btv1b10510963k); the card faces shown in this
library's grammar are from that scan [@bnf_gallica; @web_letarot_vieville]. The deck is one of
the few essentially **complete 17th-century tarots** to survive [@web_pollett_vieville]. Its
descent line is documented through the later Rouen-Brussels witnesses: **Gérard Bodet of
Liège, 1693** (the oldest *complete* deck of the pattern), **Adam de Hautot of Rouen** (early
18th c., a bridge), and **F. I. Vandenborre of Brussels** (the "Tarot Flamand," c. 1762–80),
each of which carries the divergent trump designs Viéville already shows
[@web_th_bodet; @web_thf_dehautot; @web_wopc_vandenborre; @web_belgian_wp].

## Structure

A standard **78-card pack** in composition, but a **non-Marseille pattern** in its trumps:

- **22 atouts (trumps):** 21 carry printed Roman numerals (read from the BnF scan) plus the
  **unnumbered Mat** (Fool). Numerals use the additive **IIII / VIIII** forms.
- **The trump order is reordered relative to Marseille.** On the Viéville cards
  **Justice = VII, the Chariot = VIII, Force (Strength) = VIIII, and L'Hermite = XI** — exactly
  the four cards the Marseille "C" sequence places differently (Justice VIII, Strength XI,
  Hermit VIIII, Chariot VII) [@web_aeclectic_vieville; @web_th_french_connection;
  @dummett1980]. This virtue reordering is itself a **fingerprint of the non-Marseille
  lineage**.
  - On the placement of this deck in the catalogue's order scheme: it is filed under **order
    "C"** for shelving consistency with its Parisian/French peers, but with the explicit
    caveat that its trump sequence carries the **Belgian-line affinity** — a Bologna-derived
    reordering — and is **not** the canonical Marseille "C" running order. Treat "C" here as
    *family bucket*, not *exact sequence* (confidence: medium) [@dummett1980;
    @web_th_rouen_bruxelles].
- **56 suit cards:** four Italian suits — **Épées** (Swords), **Deniers** (Coins), **Bâtons**
  (Batons), **Coupes** (Cups) — each King, Queen, Cavalier, Valet and ten **non-scenic /
  emblematic pips**, as in the whole woodcut tradition [@web_pollett_vieville;
  @web_letarot_vieville].
- **Mirror-reversed figures.** A documented technical quirk: several figures face the
  *opposite* direction from the Marseille norm because blocks were cut without mirror-imaging,
  printing reversed [@web_th_french_connection; @web_aeclectic_vieville].

## Game or divination?

**Game, unambiguously.** This is a trick-taking tarot pack from c. 1650, a century before any
attested cartomantic reading of tarot trumps (which begins with Court de Gébelin in 1781 and
Etteilla thereafter, and even then over the *Marseille* image, not this one) [@dummett1980;
@dummett2004; @decker1996]. No "upright / reversed" meanings are native to the Viéville; any
that circulate with modern reproductions are a later overlay imported from the Marseille
divinatory tradition. The grammar should label the deck **"game"**.

## What changed from its parent — the divergence from the Marseille line

This is a **root / ancestral** deck in the catalogue (`derives_from: []`): it has no parent
*within* the collection. Its meaningful "diff" is **against the Marseille line it is so often
wrongly grouped with**. Per **Dummett**, the tarots of **Viéville, Adam de Hautot, and the
18th-century Brussels cardmakers** form a tradition **distinct from the Tarot de Marseille,
inherited more or less directly from Bologna via Piedmont and Savoy** [@dummett1980;
@web_th_rouen_bruxelles]. The documented divergences:

- **La Foudre, not a struck Tower.** Where Marseille's XVI is *La Maison-Dieu* — a tower
  struck by celestial fire — the Viéville XVI shows a **tree** under a shower of fire/hail,
  with a **shepherd-like figure** gazing up; the Belgian tradition names this card **La Foudre
  / La Fouldre** ("the Lightning") [@web_th_rouen_bruxelles; @web_belgian_wp;
  @web_th_french_connection]. (Note: the grammar's own card text still reads "tower struck and
  falling" — flagged below.)
- **Divergent celestial trumps (Star / Moon / Sun).** The Viéville **Star (XVII)** is an
  **astronomer/astrologer with compasses (calipers)**, not the Marseille water-pourer; the
  **Moon (XVIII)** is a **woman under a tree holding a spindle** (an image Tarot Heritage
  traces to a Bolognese *Sun* card, transferred onto the Viéville *Moon*); the **Sun (XVIIII)**
  is a **youth on horseback** [@web_tarotforum_vieville_sms; @web_th_celestial;
  @web_th_french_connection]. All three depart sharply from Marseille.
- **Reordered virtues** (see *Structure*): Justice VII, Chariot VIII, Force VIIII, Hermite XI
  [@web_aeclectic_vieville; @dummett1980].
- **An upright Hanged Man.** *Le Pendu* (XII) is shown **upright** rather than suspended head-
  down [@web_aeclectic_vieville]. (The grammar's card text does not yet note this — flagged.)
- **A divergent World and other higher trumps**, in line with the broader Belgian pattern
  [@web_aeclectic_vieville; @web_th_french_connection].
- **Sphinx-like chariot-pullers** (human-faced beings rather than horses) on the Chariot
  [@web_aeclectic_vieville].

The often-repeated shorthand that "**trumps 1–11 are the same as the standard Marseille while
the higher trumps differ**" describes the **later Belgian/Vandenborre** pattern more cleanly
than Viéville itself — in Viéville the **virtue reorder already disturbs trumps 7–11**, so the
"1–11 identical" claim should not be applied to this deck without that caveat (confidence:
medium) [@web_th_french_connection; @web_aeclectic_vieville].

## The fear question

**No fear as a game.** Like the Marseille, the Viéville was an ordinary regulated product of
the French card trade. Its divergent imagery (a Popess, a Pope, a Devil, La Foudre) is the
standard Christian-allegorical trump cycle, carved a little differently — nothing the Church
or the law singled out. The later cartomantic anxiety is entirely downstream and external to
this object [@dummett1980].

## Counter-voices

The persistent counter-claim is the **cataloguing one**: that the Viéville is "just an early
/ variant Tarot de Marseille" (it is so listed on several collector and review pages, and the
grammar inherited that framing) [@web_aeclectic_vieville]. The document-first reading,
following **Dummett**, is that this **obscures a genuinely separate ancestry** (Bologna →
Piedmont/Savoy → the Belgian line) and the concrete iconographic evidence for it — the
reordered virtues, *La Foudre*, the astronomer-Star, the spinner-Moon, the horseman-Sun
[@dummett1980; @web_th_rouen_bruxelles; @web_th_celestial]. Both facts are true at once: it is
**Parisian and contemporary with Noblet**, *and* it is the **road not taken** — the head of
the Belgian/Flemish divergence, not the Marseille mainstream.

## Open questions / corrections owed

- **GRAMMAR ERROR — mislabelled as Marseille.** `metadata.tradition` reads "Tarot de Marseille
  family (Parisian)" and `metadata.branch` is `"marseille"`; the deck description repeatedly
  calls it a "Tarot de Marseille-family" deck. Per this dossier and `people/jacques-vieville.md`
  it is the **divergent Rouen-Brussels / Belgian-line** pattern, **not** Marseille. The
  tradition/branch fields and the description should be corrected (e.g. branch
  "rouen-brussels" / "belgian-line"). [maintainer action; not edited here per task scope.]
- **GRAMMAR ERROR — La Foudre.** Trump-16 card text reads "The Tower / House of God, struck and
  falling," i.e. the Marseille reading. The Viéville card is **a tree under a fire/hail shower
  with a shepherd figure (La Foudre)**, not a struck tower — correct the card text.
- **GRAMMAR ERROR — upright Hanged Man.** Trump-12 text just says "The Hanged Man"; it should
  note the Viéville's **upright** posture, a documented divergence.
- **GRAMMAR ERROR — celestial trumps under-described.** Trump-17/18/19 read flatly ("The
  Star", "The Moon", "The Sun, with figures beneath"); they should record the **astronomer
  with compasses (Star)**, **woman with spindle under a tree (Moon)**, and **youth on
  horseback (Sun)** that mark the divergence.
- **GRAMMAR ERROR — order label.** `metadata.order` "Eastern/Belgian-leaning" is defensible in
  spirit but the deck description calls the sequence "the older **Eastern trump order**"; the
  scholarly frame is **Bologna-via-Piedmont/Savoy (Belgian line)**, not "Eastern." Prefer
  "Belgian-line / Bolognese-derived."
- **Dating precision:** confirm "c. 1650" against the BnF object record [@web_letarot_vieville].
- **Ancestor vs. cousin of the Belgian line:** whether Bodet (1693) copied Viéville directly or
  both descend from a lost common source is unresolved [@web_th_rouen_bruxelles].
- **Pip ranks** in the grammar are assigned by BnF folio position and may need correction
  [@web_pollett_vieville].
