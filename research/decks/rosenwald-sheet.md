# The Rosenwald Sheet

## At a glance

**Uncut woodcut sheets of an early Florentine tarot**, late 15th–early 16th century, in the
**National Gallery of Art, Washington** (the **Lessing J. Rosenwald Collection**, donated 1951)
[@web_th_rosenwald; @web_tsr_rosenwald; @nga_rosenwald]. They are a **southern (A-order)
counterpart** to the northern C-order Cary Sheet: austere, woodcut, and genuinely early, and stand
near the head of the **Florentine A-order line** that runs toward Minchiate [@web_th_rosenwald;
@web_minchiate_early_wp]. A **game** object — printed *trionfi* sheets, with no native divinatory
meaning [@dummett1980]. The NGA's open-access policy places the images in the public domain
[@nga_rosenwald]. (confidence: medium — the A-order classification is secure; the Florence
attribution and c. 1500 date are conventional but not documentary.)

## Origin & dating

Conventionally dated **c. 1500 (give or take a decade)** and attributed to **Florence**, on style:
the sheets "owe much stylistically to the Florentine minchiate decks of the time"
[@web_th_rosenwald; @web_ladyheather_rosenwald]. A striking provenance detail: the uncut sheets were
**discovered within a book binding** likely constructed around **Perugia, c. 1501** — i.e. they
survive as **printer's reject sheets reused in bookbinding**, which is part of why they are so early
and so plain [@web_tsr_rosenwald; @web_th_rosenwald] (confidence: medium — the binding date anchors a
*terminus ante quem*; the Florence attribution is stylistic).

## Provenance & evidence

- **National Gallery of Art, Washington — Rosenwald Collection** (open access; e.g. NGA object
  1951.16.x), three related sheets of the same size [@nga_rosenwald; @web_th_rosenwald].
- **Three sheets survive** — one **trump** sheet and two **court/number** sheets — but together they
  **do not complete any known pack**, implying a now-lost **fourth block** [@web_th_rosenwald;
  @web_tsr_rosenwald]. Franco Pratesi analysed the sheets (incl. the "fourth sheet" question)
  [@web_pratesi_rosenwald].

## Structure

- **Trumps in the A (Florentine–Bolognese) order**, woodcut and plain [@web_th_rosenwald;
  @dummett1980]. The A-order ordering of the Virtues runs **Justice, Fortitude, Chariot, Wheel** —
  diagnostic of the southern branch [@web_th_rosenwald]. The sheet notably includes a **Popess** as
  an early trump [@web_th_rosenwald] (confidence: medium).
- **Court and number (pip) cards** on the other two sheets, in the Italian four-suit system
  [@web_th_rosenwald; @web_tsr_rosenwald].
- Because a block is missing, the surviving sheets **do not add up to a complete pack** — do not
  assert a card count from them [@web_tsr_rosenwald].

## Game or divination?

**Game.** Printed Florentine *trionfi* sheets for play; no divinatory apparatus
[@dummett1980].

## Relation to Minchiate / the Florentine tradition

The Rosenwald sheets are stylistically tied to the **Florentine minchiate** milieu — the expanded
97-card Florentine pack that grew out of the A-order tarot [@web_th_rosenwald; @web_minchiate_early_wp].
They are among the **earliest surviving woodcut tarot**, and the clearest early witness to the
**A-order (Florentine–Bolognese)** trump sequence that distinguishes the southern branch from the
Milanese C-order [@dummett1980; @web_th_rosenwald] (confidence: medium — the minchiate kinship is
stylistic/structural, not a documented descent).

## The fear question

**No fear.** Cheap printed game sheets; the only contemporary anxiety was the friars' anti-**gambling**
campaign, not the imagery [@dummett1980].

## Counter-voices

No esoteric apparatus is present; the sheets show the A-order tarot as **plain printed game cards**
[@dummett1980]. Open questions are the **lost fourth block**, the **exact date**, and whether
"Florence" is precise or shorthand for a wider Florentine–Bolognese (A-order) production
[@web_tsr_rosenwald; @web_pratesi_rosenwald].
