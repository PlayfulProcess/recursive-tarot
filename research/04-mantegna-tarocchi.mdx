# Mantegna "Tarocchi" — *not* a tarot

## At a glance

A set of fifty 15th-century engravings, **not a card game and not a tarot deck at all**.
Included in this library precisely to explain the confusion: the name is a later error.
Not by Mantegna, not tarot, not for play or divination — a **didactic image cycle** of
the cosmos and the social order.

## Origin & dating

Two sets survive, the **E-series** (c.1465, the older) and the **S-series** (a little
later). Documentary support includes a Bolognese manuscript dated 1467. Stylistic links
to the Salone dei Mesi at the Palazzo Schifanoia point to artists in the **Ferrarese /
Este** orbit (around Francesco del Cossa); the true engraver is unknown. The attribution
to Andrea Mantegna is spurious.

## Provenance & evidence

Fifty engravings in **five decades** of ten: social ranks (Beggar → Pope), the Muses and
Apollo, the liberal arts, the virtues and cosmic principles, and the celestial spheres.
It is a **ladder of being** — a Renaissance encyclopedia in pictures.

## Game or divination?

**Neither.** No suits, no trick-taking structure, no fortune-telling. Best understood as
**educational prints**, plausibly for instructing upper-class youths — though, honestly,
no document records their actual use. The "Tarocchi" label was pinned on later by writers
who confused them with the picture-rich tarot game.

## The fear question

**Not applicable** — these are humanist study images, openly produced and circulated.
Their inclusion here is corrective: to stop the genealogy absorbing material that merely
*looks* tarot-adjacent.

## Counter-voices

Esoteric authors have repeatedly raided the Mantegna series for "hidden" Neoplatonic or
Hermetic keys to tarot. The series *is* genuinely Neoplatonic — that was mainstream
learned culture — but it is **not an ancestor of the tarot pack**. Treating it as one is
how false genealogies (and the Egyptian story) get built.
