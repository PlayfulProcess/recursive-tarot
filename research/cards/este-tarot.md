# d'Este Tarocchi — Card-by-Card

**Deck:** `este-tarot` · **Surviving cards:** 16 (8 trumps + 8 court) ·
**Source of imagery:** Yale Beinecke / Cary Collection of Playing Cards, open access, named from
Yale's catalogue labels [@web_yale_este]. These are a **B-order Ferrarese** fragment; the cards are
**unnumbered** and carry **no native divinatory meanings** (a game pack) [@dummett1980]. "Means"
lines are therefore omitted; "Changed from parent" lines are omitted (root fragment).

## Surviving trumps (8)

### Il Bagatto (The Magician)
- **Depicts:** The mountebank / juggler — the lowest trump (atout I) in the B-order, a market
  conjurer at his table [@web_yale_este; @dummett1980].
- **Confidence:** medium

### Il Papa (The Pope)
- **Depicts:** The Pope (the later Hierophant), an enthroned ecclesiastical authority figure
  [@web_yale_este].
- **Confidence:** medium

### Temperance
- **Depicts:** The virtue Temperance pouring liquid between two vessels [@web_yale_este].
- **Confidence:** medium

### The Star (La Stella)
- **Depicts:** A celestial-light trump; the d'Este Star figure has been the subject of a dedicated
  iconographic study (and is part of the case for a 1470s date) [@web_intomystery_este;
  @web_yale_este].
- **Confidence:** medium

### The Moon (La Luna)
- **Depicts:** The Moon — one of the three "celestial lights" trumps [@web_yale_este].
- **Confidence:** medium

### The Sun (Il Sole)
- **Depicts:** The Sun, the brightest of the celestial-light trumps [@web_yale_este].
- **Confidence:** medium

### The World (Il Mondo)
- **Depicts:** The World — the high trump, typically a personification/cosmos image
  [@web_yale_este].
- **Confidence:** medium

### Il Matto (The Fool)
- **Depicts:** The Fool / Mat — the unnumbered wanderer outside the trump sequence [@web_yale_este;
  @dummett1980].
- **Confidence:** medium

## Surviving court cards (8)

Eight courts across the four Italian suits — **Swords, Batons, Coins, Cups** — comprising kings,
queens, a cavalier and a jack [@web_yale_este]. Following the repo grammar's catalogue, the
identified courts include the **King, Queen and Cavalier of Swords; King, Cavalier and Jack of
Batons; King of Coins; and Queen of Cups** (precise per-suit roster per Yale's catalogue;
confidence: medium — court suit-assignments rest on the catalogue, not re-examined here).

- **Depicts (all):** standing or mounted court figures with the suit-emblem (sword, baton, coin,
  cup), in the Italian four-suit system [@web_yale_este; @web_trionfi_este].
- **Means:** none (game pack).
- **Confidence:** medium

## Note

The pips and the remaining trumps are **lost**; the original composition of the pack is **unknown**
[@web_yale_este]. This file covers only the 16 surviving cards.
