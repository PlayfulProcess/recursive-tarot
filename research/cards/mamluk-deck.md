# Mamluk Playing Cards — the surviving cards

A four-suit game pack has **no trumps and no native per-card meanings** (it is a game, not an
oracle). The complete pack held **52 cards** (4 suits × [Ace–10 + king + two deputies]); of
these, **47–48 survive** at the **Topkapı Sarayı Museum, Istanbul** — about **43 from the
original primary pack** plus a handful of cruder replacements and promoted pips [@mayer1971],
[@dummett_abudeeb1973], [@web_cardsoldno_mamluk]. Additional **loose fragments** survive outside
Topkapı (Keir/Dallas Museum of Art; Benaki, Athens; LACMA) — see the deck dossier.

**Source of imagery / identification:** Mayer's plates (1939/1971), the per-card reconstruction
and value-ordering by Tor Gjerde (cards.old.no/1500-mamluk), and Trzes/spiorad's structural
notes; corrected for count by Dummett & Abu-Deeb (1973) [@mayer1971], [@dummett_abudeeb1973],
[@web_cardsoldno_mamluk], [@web_spiorad_topkapi].

## Per-card survival map (Topkapı pack)

Status key: **original** = surviving card of the primary pack; **promoted** = an original pip
re-used to fill a vacant court slot (its top inscription panel made it suitable); **replacement**
= a cruder card imported from one of two other contemporary packs; **missing** = not preserved.
"fig NN" = Mayer's plate figure. Coins and polo-sticks ranked in *reverse* (low pip beats high),
which is why low pips were chosen for promotion in those suits [@web_cardsoldno_mamluk].

deputy of polo-sticks, ace of polo-sticks, nine of swords, ten of swords [@web_cardsoldno_mamluk],
[@dummett_abudeeb1973]. (confidence: high; minor uncertainty on the swords second-deputy slot.)

## The four suits (descriptive system nodes)

### 1 — Suit of Cups (ṭūmān)
- **Depicts:** Ace–10 plus three courts; the suit symbol is a rounded chalice. The Arabic name
  *ṭūmān* means "myriads / ten-thousands" [@web_pollett_mamluk], [@mayer1971].
- **European descendant:** carried over directly as **Cups** (*coppe* / *coupes*)
  [@web_wopc_mamluk_european].
- **Changed from parent:** plausibly a renaming of the Chinese **wàn** ("myriads") money suit —
  the word *ṭūmān* echoes *wàn* — recast from a currency denomination to an object. (confidence:
  low — suggestive, unproven) [@web_pollett_mamluk], [@web00c_islamic_chinese].
- **Confidence:** high (structure); low (parent link).

### 2 — Suit of Coins (darāhim)
- **Depicts:** Ace–10 plus three courts; a round disc coin. *Darāhim* = plural of *dirham*
  [@web_pollett_mamluk].
- **European descendant:** carried over as **Coins** (*denari* / *deniers*)
  [@web_wopc_mamluk_european].
- **Changed from parent:** the **coin is the suit that survived the long westward diffusion most
  recognisably** — continuous with the Chinese cash/coins suit [@web00c_islamic_chinese].
- **Confidence:** high.

### 3 — Suit of Swords (suyūf)
- **Depicts:** Ace–10 plus three courts; a **straight** blade (carried into the European straight
  Swords, unlike the curved Spanish later forms) [@web_pollett_mamluk], [@mayer1971].
- **European descendant:** **Swords** (*spade* / *épées*) [@web_wopc_mamluk_european].
- **Changed from parent:** no clean Chinese-money forerunner; an Islamic-world object suit, so a
  substitution rather than a continuation [@web00c_islamic_chinese].
- **Confidence:** high.

### 4 — Suit of Polo-sticks (jawkān)
- **Depicts:** Ace–10 plus three courts; the *jawkān* (polo mallet). Polo was the foremost
  Mamluk aristocratic sport [@web_pollett_mamluk], [@web_cairoscene_mamluk].
- **European descendant:** **Batons / Wands** (*bastoni* / *bâtons*; later French *clubs*) —
  Europeans didn't play polo, so the mallet was redrawn as a plain staff: the **clearest single
  fingerprint of the Islamic origin** [@web_cairoscene_mamluk], [@web_th_trzes_mamluk].
- **Changed from parent:** no clean Chinese-money descendant; the "strings of cash" suit has been
  compared *speculatively* to the baton line, but the mapping beyond *coins* is inference.
  (confidence: low) [@web00c_islamic_chinese].
- **Confidence:** high (European descent); low (Chinese parent mapping).

## 5 — The Court: Malik & two Nāʾib (aniconic)
- **Depicts:** each suit's three courts — **malik** (king), **nāʾib** (deputy), **thānī nāʾib**
  (second deputy). The pack's name, *Mulūk wa-nuwwāb* ("Kings and Deputies"), comes from this
  court [@mayer1971], [@web_spiorad_topkapi]. **Aniconic:** no figures, only ornament and Arabic
  inscriptions — rank named on a lower blue panel, verse on an upper one [@web_wopc_mamluk],
  [@web_cardsoldno_mamluk].
- **Survival:** only **two original deputies survive**, both in cups (fig 01, fig 02); elsewhere
  the court slots are filled by promoted pips or replacement cards (see the map above)
  [@web_cardsoldno_mamluk].
- **European descendant:** Europe kept the **King**, turned a deputy into a mounted **Knight**,
  kept a **Page** (valet), later **added a Queen**, and re-introduced figural human imagery
  [@web_wopc_mamluk], [@web_pollett_mamluk].
- **Confidence:** high (structure); low (the China parent comparison — undocumented transmission).

## Fragments outside Topkapı (identified cards)
- **Keir Collection / Dallas Museum of Art** — fragment of an **8 of cups**; plus two numeral-card
  fragments attributed to 13th-c. Egypt [@web_wopc_mamluk], [@web_keir_dma_fragments].
- **Benaki Museum, Athens** — a **2 of cups** of Islamic (Mamluk) origin (one of three fragments;
  the other two are an *Italian* 3 of cups and a non-card Islamic fragment) [@web_wopc_mamluk].
- **LACMA** — three fragmentary Mamluk cards, 13th–14th c. (M.2002.1.650/651/654); specific
  suits/ranks not all published [@web_lacma_mamluk_fragment].
- **cards.old.no c.1300 fragment** — possibly a low cup card; date and identity highly uncertain
  [@web_cardsoldno_mamluk1300].
