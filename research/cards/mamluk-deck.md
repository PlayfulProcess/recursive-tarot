# Mamluk Playing Cards — the Four Suits & the Court

A four-suit game pack has **no trumps and no native per-card meanings** (it is a game, not
an oracle), so the meaningful unit to document is the **suit** (four of them) plus the
**court structure** shared across all four. Each suit runs **Ace–10 plus three aniconic
court cards**; the complete pack held 52 (4 × 13), of which ~48 survive (~43 original) at
the Topkapı Palace Museum [@mayer1971], [@web_wopc_mamluk], [@web_cardsoldno_mamluk].

**Source of imagery:** the Topkapı pack as published by Mayer (1939/1971) and reconstructed
in modern editions; web descriptions from WOPC and Andy Pollett's pages
[@mayer1971], [@web_wopc_mamluk], [@web_pollett_mamluk].

The **"Changed from parent"** line throughout records the **China → Islam** shift — the
transmission from the Chinese money-suited pack (`madiao-money-cards`) to the Mamluk object
suits. This is an **undocumented transmission**, not a traced descent: the route is one of
the great unsolved puzzles of gaming history, so the diffs below are hedged
[@web00c_islamic_chinese].

## 1 — Suit of Cups (ṭūmān)

- **Depicts:** Ten pip cards (Ace–10) plus three courts. The suit symbol is a cup; the
  Arabic name *ṭūmān* notably means "myriads / ten-thousands" [@web_pollett_mamluk],
  [@mayer1971].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** None — a game suit has no native cartomantic meaning.
- **European descendant:** Carried into Europe **directly as Cups** (*coppe* / *coupes*),
  the Latin Cups/Hearts suit [@web_wopc_mamluk_european], [@web00c_islamic_chinese].
- **Changed from parent:** Plausibly a renaming of the Chinese **wàn** ("myriads") money
  suit — the very word *ṭūmān* echoes *wàn* — recast from a currency denomination into an
  object suit (a cup). (confidence: low — a suggestive but unproven correspondence)
  [@web_pollett_mamluk], [@web00c_islamic_chinese].
- **Sources:** [@mayer1971], [@web_pollett_mamluk], [@web_wopc_mamluk_european],
  [@web00c_islamic_chinese]
- **Confidence:** high (structure); low (the parent-suit link)

## 2 — Suit of Coins (darāhim)

- **Depicts:** Ten pips plus three courts; the suit symbol is a coin. *Darāhim* is the
  plural of *dirham*, the standard Mamluk silver currency [@web_pollett_mamluk].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** None.
- **European descendant:** Carried into Europe **as Coins** (*denari* / *deniers*), the
  Latin Coins/Diamonds suit [@web_wopc_mamluk_european], [@web00c_islamic_chinese].
- **Changed from parent:** The **coin is the suit that survived the long westward diffusion
  most recognisably** — directly continuous with the Chinese cash/coins money suit; the
  least-changed link in the chain [@web00c_islamic_chinese], [@web_suit_wiki].
- **Sources:** [@mayer1971], [@web_pollett_mamluk], [@web_wopc_mamluk_european],
  [@web00c_islamic_chinese]
- **Confidence:** high

## 3 — Suit of Swords (suyūf)

- **Depicts:** Ten pips plus three courts; the suit symbol is a **straight** sword/sabre
  (the sign drawn as a long blade with a hilt and bosses along the shaft)
  [@web_pollett_mamluk], [@mayer1971].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** None.
- **European descendant:** Carried into Europe **as Swords** (*spade* / *épées*); the Latin
  Swords suit preserved the straight-bladed form [@web_wopc_mamluk_european].
- **Changed from parent:** Has **no clean single descendant in the Chinese money-suits** —
  the swords suit is an Islamic-world object suit with no obvious currency forerunner, so
  the diff against the money pack is a substitution, not a continuation [@web00c_islamic_chinese].
- **Sources:** [@mayer1971], [@web_pollett_mamluk], [@web_wopc_mamluk_european]
- **Confidence:** high

## 4 — Suit of Polo-sticks (jawkān)

- **Depicts:** Ten pips plus three courts; the suit symbol is the **jawkān** (polo mallet),
  drawn as a long straight stick with an L-shaped end, sections coloured in gold and blue.
  Polo was the most popular aristocratic sport of the Mamluk Empire [@web_pollett_mamluk],
  [@web_cairoscene_mamluk].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** None.
- **European descendant:** **Polo-sticks → Batons / Wands** (*bastoni* / *bâtons*; later
  French *clubs*). Europeans didn't play polo, so this suit was reinterpreted as plain
  staves — the **clearest single fingerprint of the Islamic origin** of the European pack
  [@web_cairoscene_mamluk], [@web_th_trzes_mamluk], [@web00c_islamic_chinese].
- **Changed from parent:** No clean Chinese-money descendant; the "strings of cash" suit has
  been compared **speculatively** to the polo-stick/baton line, but the mapping beyond
  *coins* is inference, not documentation. (confidence: low) [@web_suit_wiki],
  [@web00c_islamic_chinese].
- **Sources:** [@mayer1971], [@web_pollett_mamluk], [@web_cairoscene_mamluk],
  [@web_th_trzes_mamluk], [@web00c_islamic_chinese]
- **Confidence:** high (the European → Batons descent); low (the Chinese-parent mapping)

## 5 — The Court: Malik & two Nā'ib (aniconic)

- **Depicts:** Each suit's three court cards: the **malik** (king), the **nā'ib** (deputy /
  viceroy / first lieutenant), and the **thānī nā'ib** (second deputy / second lieutenant).
  The pack's name, *Mulūk wa-nuwwāb* ("Kings and Deputies"), comes from this court
  [@mayer1971], [@web_pollett_mamluk]. **Aniconic:** no human or animal figures — only
  geometric ornament and **Arabic calligraphic inscriptions** naming the rank (on blue
  panels), with further panels of verse that read as good-luck or courtly-praise maxims
  [@web_pollett_mamluk], [@web_th_trzes_mamluk].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** None.
- **European descendant:** The three courts became the European court line: Europe kept the
  **King**, turned the second deputy into a mounted **Knight**, retained a Page (valet), and
  later **added a Queen** — and, crucially, **re-introduced figural human images** the
  Mamluk cards forbade [@web_wopc_mamluk], [@web_pollett_mamluk].
- **Changed from parent:** The fixed **three-card aniconic court** is the Mamluk pack's own
  standardisation; the Chinese money-suits carried honour/figural cards (e.g. the *Water
  Margin* heroes) but not this king-plus-two-deputies scheme, so the court structure is a
  Mamluk-world innovation over whatever crossed the gap. (confidence: low on the parent
  comparison — the transmission is undocumented) [@web00c_islamic_chinese], [@mayer1971].
- **Sources:** [@mayer1971], [@web_pollett_mamluk], [@web_th_trzes_mamluk],
  [@web_wopc_mamluk], [@web00c_islamic_chinese]
- **Confidence:** high (structure); low (the parent comparison)
