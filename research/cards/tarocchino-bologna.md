# Tarocchino di Bologna — Card by Card

**Source of imagery:** the standard modern **Bolognese pattern** (the double-headed Dal Negro /
Modiano "Al Mondo" design, IPCS pattern **PS-11**), as scanned on pagat.com for the **Ottocento**
rules [@web_pagat_ottocento; @web_ipcs_bolognese; @web_th_almondo]. The historic copper-engraved
reference edition is Mitelli's c.1660 Tarocchino [@web_th_mitelli_berti]. The cards bear **no
printed titles**, and **only trumps 5–16 are numbered**; the lowest and highest trumps are
unnumbered by Bolognese tradition [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp; @web_steve_bolo].

**62 cards =** 22 trumps (Fool + Bagattino + 4 Moors + 16 figured) **+** 40 suit cards (each suit:
Ace, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10 + Fante, Cavallo, Regina, Re — the **2–5 removed**) [@web_tarocchini_wp;
@web_ipcs_bolognese].

The headings below number the trumps **0–21 by position in the A-order** (matching the grammar's
`sort_order`); this is **not** the printed Bolognese numbering, which runs only 5–16. Each block
reads **iconography** and **rank**, not a fortune-telling meaning [@dummett1980].

## The Trumps (A-order)

## 0 — The Fool (Il Matto)
- **Depicts:** A ragged wanderer — the unnumbered "Excuse" of the game, exempt from following suit
  [@web_pagat_ottocento; @web_steve_bolo].
- **Rank in the Bolognese deck:** Stands **outside** the trump ranking, as the Fool does
  everywhere; played to escape following suit, not to take a trick [@web_pagat_ottocento].
- **Sources:** [@web_pagat_ottocento], [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 1 — The Bagattino (Il Bagattino)
- **Depicts:** A craftsman or conjuror at his table — the *bagattino*, a trifle [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Rank:** The **lowest trump proper**; the everyman who opens the parade. Unnumbered
  [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp], [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 2 — Papa / Moor I (Papa / Moro)
- **Depicts:** A turbaned Moor — one of **four near-identical figures** [@web_druidcraft_montieri].
- **Rank:** One of the **four equal-rank Moors/Papi**: they do not outrank one another; whichever is
  **played last to a trick wins** among them. They replace the Popess, Empress, Emperor and Pope
  [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp; @web_druidcraft_montieri].
- **Changed from parent:** In the inherited *trionfi* pack these were four distinct **papal/imperial
  trumps of ascending rank**; the Bolognese tradition first made them **equal in rank**, then a
  **1725 papal edict (Cardinal Ruffo, after the Montieri affair) re-imaged all four as Moors**,
  erasing the contentious Popess/Pope [@web_druidcraft_montieri; @web_tarocchini_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_druidcraft_montieri], [@web_tarocchini_wp], [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 3 — Papa / Moor II (Papa / Moro)
- **Depicts:** The second of the four equal Moors [@web_druidcraft_montieri].
- **Rank:** One of the four interchangeable Moors (see Moor I).
- **Changed from parent:** As Moor I — re-imaged from a papal/imperial trump by the 1725 edict
  [@web_druidcraft_montieri].
- **Sources:** [@web_druidcraft_montieri], [@web_tarocchini_wp].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 4 — Papa / Moor III (Papa / Moro)
- **Depicts:** The third of the four equal Moors. **Two of the four Moor cards are identical**, a
  documented Bolognese production quirk [@web_tarocchini_wp].
- **Rank:** One of the four interchangeable Moors (see Moor I).
- **Changed from parent:** As Moor I [@web_druidcraft_montieri].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocchini_wp], [@web_druidcraft_montieri].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 5 — Papa / Moor IV (Papa / Moro)
- **Depicts:** The fourth of the four equal Moors [@web_druidcraft_montieri].
- **Rank:** One of the four interchangeable Moors (see Moor I).
- **Changed from parent:** As Moor I [@web_druidcraft_montieri].
- **Sources:** [@web_druidcraft_montieri], [@web_tarocchini_wp].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 6 — Love (L'Amore)
- **Depicts:** A couple beneath a winged Cupid [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Rank:** The first numbered trump — **printed "5"** in the Bolognese pack, sitting just above the
  four Moors [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp], [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 7 — The Chariot (Il Carro)
- **Depicts:** A victor in a triumphal car [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Rank:** Printed **"6"**; opens the central virtue cluster [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp], [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 8 — Temperance (La Temperanza)
- **Depicts:** A figure pouring between two vessels [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Rank:** Printed **"7"** — **first of the three Virtues**, which the Bolognese order groups
  together (Temperance, Justice, Strength) right after the Chariot [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp;
  @web_th_almondo].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp], [@web_th_almondo].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 9 — Justice (La Giustizia)
- **Depicts:** An enthroned woman with sword and scales [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Rank:** Printed **"8"** — the middle of the clustered Virtues [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp], [@web_th_almondo].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 10 — Strength / Fortitude (La Forza)
- **Depicts:** A figure mastering a lion or breaking a column [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Rank:** Printed **"9"** — the third clustered Virtue [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp], [@web_th_almondo].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 11 — Wheel of Fortune (La Ruota)
- **Depicts:** A turning wheel with rising and falling figures [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Rank:** Printed **"10"**, above the Virtues. In the Bolognese order the **Wheel sits below the
  Old Man** — the reverse of the Marseille placement of these two [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp;
  @web_pollett_orders].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp], [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 12 — The Old Man / Hermit (Il Vecchio)
- **Depicts:** **Old Man Time** — a bent, winged old man, sometimes on crutches; the Bolognese
  "Hermit" reads as Time/age, not a lamp-bearing recluse [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp;
  @web_steve_bolo].
- **Rank:** Printed **"11"**, above the Wheel [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp], [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 13 — The Traitor / Hanged Man (Il Traditore)
- **Depicts:** A youth hung by one foot — the shaming *pittura infamante* image of a traitor
  [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Rank:** Printed **"12"** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp], [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 14 — Death (La Morte)
- **Depicts:** A skeletal reaper among the fallen [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Rank:** Printed **"13"** — the Bolognese order keeps Death in its traditional thirteenth place
  [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp], [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 15 — The Devil (Il Diavolo)
- **Depicts:** A horned figure over bound captives [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Rank:** Printed **"14"** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp], [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 16 — The Lightning / Tower (La Saetta / La Torre)
- **Depicts:** A tower struck by a bolt — called **La Saetta** ("the lightning") in Bologna
  [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp; @web_steve_bolo].
- **Rank:** Printed **"15"** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp], [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 17 — The Star (La Stella)
- **Depicts:** A figure beneath a great star — first of the three "heavenly lights"
  [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Rank:** Printed **"16"** — the **last numbered card**; numbering stops here, and the trumps
  above are unnumbered [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp], [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 18 — The Moon (La Luna)
- **Depicts:** The Moon over the night world [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Rank:** The second light; **unnumbered**, ranked above the Star [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp], [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 19 — The Sun (Il Sole)
- **Depicts:** A radiant sun over the day world [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Rank:** The third light; **unnumbered** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp], [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 20 — The World (Il Mondo)
- **Depicts:** A figure within a cosmic frame [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Rank:** Near the summit — **but NOT the highest in Bologna**: unnumbered, and **ranked below the
  Angel** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp; @web_th_almondo].
- **Changed from parent:** Where the Marseille C-order puts the **World highest**, the Bolognese
  A-order **demotes the World below the Angel** [@web_pollett_orders; @web_tarocco_bolognese_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp], [@web_th_almondo], [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Confidence:** high.

## 21 — The Angel / Judgement (L'Angelo)
- **Depicts:** Angels with trumpets; the dead rise [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Rank:** The **HIGHEST trump of the Bolognese deck** — uniquely ranked **above the World**;
  unnumbered [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp; @web_th_almondo].
- **Changed from parent:** In the C-order the Angel/Judgement sits *below* the World; the Bolognese
  A-order **raises it to the top** [@web_pollett_orders; @web_tarocco_bolognese_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp], [@web_th_almondo], [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Confidence:** high.

## The Suits (each: Ace, 6–10, Fante, Cavallo, Regina, Re — the 2–5 removed)

The four suits are structurally identical: in each, the **2, 3, 4 and 5 are removed**, leaving the
**Ace and 6–10** plus the **full four-card court** (Knave/Fante, Knight/Cavallo, Queen/Regina,
King/Re) [@web_tarocchini_wp; @web_ipcs_bolognese]. The pips are **non-scenic** suit-emblem cards;
the courts are the standard Italian four-court set [@web_ipcs_bolognese]. The Bolognese keeping of
**both Knight and Knave** (unlike the German/French three-court packs) is worth noting. To avoid 40
near-identical blocks, the cards are grouped by suit; the cut and the court structure are the only
load-bearing facts, and they are the same in every suit.

## Coins / Denari (Earth)
- **Ace of Coins** — suit-emblem; the Ace survives the cut [@web_ipcs_bolognese].
- **Six, Seven, Eight, Nine, Ten of Coins** — the surviving pips (2–5 removed)
  [@web_tarocchini_wp].
- **Knave (Fante), Knight (Cavallo), Queen (Regina), King (Re) of Coins** — the full four-card court
  [@web_ipcs_bolognese].
- **Changed from parent:** The 2–5 are removed; otherwise the suit is the inherited Latin-suited
  Coins [@web_tarocchini_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocchini_wp], [@web_ipcs_bolognese], [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Confidence:** high.

## Cups / Coppe (Water)
- **Ace; Six–Ten; Fante, Cavallo, Regina, Re of Cups** — Ace + 6–10 + four courts; 2–5 removed
  [@web_tarocchini_wp; @web_ipcs_bolognese].
- **Changed from parent:** 2–5 removed [@web_tarocchini_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocchini_wp], [@web_ipcs_bolognese], [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Confidence:** high.

## Swords / Spade (Air)
- **Ace; Six–Ten; Fante, Cavallo, Regina, Re of Swords** — Ace + 6–10 + four courts; 2–5 removed.
  In the Italian (non-Spanish) pattern the Sword pips are the curved, interlaced blades
  [@web_tarocchini_wp; @web_ipcs_bolognese].
- **Changed from parent:** 2–5 removed [@web_tarocchini_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocchini_wp], [@web_ipcs_bolognese], [@web_steve_bolo].
- **Confidence:** high.

## Batons / Bastoni (Fire)
- **Ace; Six–Ten; Fante, Cavallo, Regina, Re of Batons** — Ace + 6–10 + four courts; 2–5 removed;
  the interlaced batons of the Italian pattern [@web_tarocchini_wp; @web_ipcs_bolognese].
- **Changed from parent:** 2–5 removed [@web_tarocchini_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarocchini_wp], [@web_ipcs_bolognese], [@web_steve_bolo].

## Note on numbering and the grammar

The grammar's per-card `sort_order` (0–21 for trumps) is **positional**, not the printed Bolognese
number. On the real cards **only trumps 5–16 are numbered** (Love through the Star); the four
Moors, the Bagattino and the Fool below, and the Moon/Sun/World/Angel above, carry **no number**
[@web_tarocco_bolognese_wp; @web_steve_bolo]. The grammar correctly captures the two A-order
hallmarks — the clustered Virtues and the Angel above the World — and correctly shows **two of the
four Moors as the same image** (`trump-papa-c` reuses `trump-papa-b`'s scan), which matches the
documented "two identical Moors" quirk rather than being an error [@web_tarocchini_wp].
