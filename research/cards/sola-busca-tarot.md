# Sola Busca Tarot — Card-by-card

**Deck:** Sola Busca (N. Italy, engraved c. 1491; hand-coloured exemplar at the Pinacoteca di
Brera, Milan). **Source of imagery:** the Brera illuminated exemplar (public domain) and the
British-Museum engraved impressions [@web_sb_wp; @web_sb_pdr].

**How to read this file.** This is a **game / sui-generis** deck with **no native divinatory
meanings**, so the **"Means (upright/reversed)"** line is omitted throughout, and the
**"Changed from parent"** line is omitted (root deck, `derives_from: []`). For the **22 trumps**,
each block gives the **named classical/biblical figure** inscribed in Latin on the card —
**not** a Marseille-trump equivalence, which the deck does not support. For the **56 minors**,
each block documents the **scenic-pip fact** (the engraving is a figural scene, the deck's signal
innovation) and its **downstream significance** for Pamela Colman Smith's RWS minors; specific
scene content is described **only where a source attests it**, otherwise the block states the
documented general fact and is marked `confidence: medium` rather than inventing detail
[@web_sb_trumps; @web_sb_ciompi; @web_sb_pcs].

## Trumps (22) — named figures, not standard subjects

> **Every trump except Mato (0) bears a Latin personal name** [@web_sb_ciompi; @web_sb_trumps].
> The figures are Roman/antique and biblical heroes; loose modern mappings onto standard trumps
> exist but are **not** forced here.

## Minors (56) — the first scenic pips

> **All 56 number and court cards are fully-engraved figural scenes** — the deck's signal
> innovation and a documented precedent for Pamela Colman Smith's 1909 RWS minors
> [@web_sb_pdr; @web_sb_pcs]. Suit-emblems are stylised: **amphorae** for Cups, **discs** for
> Coins, **clubs** for Batons; Swords are conventional [@web_sb_ciompi]. **Kings, Queens and
> Knights are named** after classical figures (e.g. Alexander-cycle **Amone**, **Olinpia**,
> **Natanabo**); **Pages are unnamed** [@web_sb_ciompi]. Specific scene content is given only
> where attested; otherwise the block records the general scenic-pip fact.

## Open questions / corrections owed (cards)

- **Per-card scene content is largely undocumented at source level here.** Beyond the named-figure
  trumps, the named courts (Amone / Olinpia / Natanabo), and the two RWS-echo cards (Three of
  Swords, Ten of Swords→Ten of Wands), the **specific engraved scene of each minor is not
  described** in the consulted sources; the blocks record the documented general fact rather than
  invent detail. A primary pass over the Brera exemplar images is owed.
- **Garbled Latin names.** Several trump and court inscriptions are corrupt (scribe/engraver
  misspellings); identifications (Lenpio, Ipeo, Falco, Olivo, Venturio) are tentative
  [@web_sb_ciompi; @web_sb_trumps].
- **Court coverage.** Only three court names (Amone, Olinpia, Natanabo) are corroborated by name;
  the remaining named Kings/Queens/Knights are attested as a class ("courts are named") but not
  individually here.
- **Grammar mismatch (logged, not edited):** the grammar's per-card "Scene" texts are generic
  placeholders and do not record the named court figures; flagged for a maintainer pass.
