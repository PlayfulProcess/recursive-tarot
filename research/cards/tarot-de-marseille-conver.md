# Tarot de Marseille — Nicolas Conver, 1760 — card by card

**Source of imagery:** the woodblock-and-stencil Conver pattern dated **1760** (Type II),
public-domain scans on Wikimedia Commons [@web_camoin_conver1760; @bnf_gallica].

**A standing caution on every card below.** This is a **game deck**. The "**Seeds the occult
reading**" line records how each *image* was later re-read by the divinatory tradition
(Gébelin 1781, Etteilla from the 1780s, Wirth, the Golden Dawn) — it is **not** a native
meaning of the 1760 pack, and is given to show the deck as the picture the occultists worked
*from*, not to endorse the reading as original [@decker1996; @dummett1980]. Numerals are the
additive forms on the blocks (IIII, VIIII). The trump order is the **C order**: Justice VIII,
Strength XI (the pre-Golden-Dawn arrangement) [@dummett1980; @web_gd_swap].

## The 22 Trumps (Atouts)

## — — Le Mat (The Fool) — unnumbered
- **Depicts:** A traveller in motley walks rightward with a staff over his shoulder and a
  bundle, while a small animal (a dog or cat) tears at his trailing leg, baring his thigh. He
  alone carries **no number** — outside the running count of the trumps. *Le Mat* means "the
  madman" [@web_fool_wp; @dummett1980].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Its numberlessness let the occultists slot the Fool anywhere
  in the sequence; Gébelin and his heirs treated it as the zero / the uninitiated soul, and
  the Golden Dawn fixed it at 0 before the Hebrew letter Aleph [@decker1996; @web_levi_rota].
- **Sources:** [@web_fool_wp], [@dummett1980], [@decker1996]. **Confidence:** high.

## I — Le Bateleur (The Magician / Mountebank)
- **Depicts:** A young mountebank stands behind a table strewn with cups, coins, and knives,
  a wand in one hand and a small ball in the other, his wide hat-brim drawn as a lazy figure
  of eight. The lowest numbered trump — the fairground conjuror / *bateleur* ("sleight-of-hand
  artist") [@web_magician_wp; @dummett1980].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Re-read as the magus and the will: the table of four objects
  became the four suits / four elements, and the hat-brim was reinterpreted as the
  **lemniscate** of infinity (a later RWS embellishment read back onto the Marseille)
  [@decker1996; @waite1910].
- **Sources:** [@web_magician_wp], [@dummett1980], [@decker1996]. **Confidence:** high.

## II — La Papesse (The Popess / High Priestess)
- **Depicts:** A seated, veiled woman wearing the **papal tiara** holds an open book on her
  lap, a curtain or veil behind her. A frankly heterodox image — a female pope [@dummett1980;
  @tarot_heritage].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** The veil and book became "hidden knowledge"; Gébelin read her
  as the High Priestess / Isis, and the title "High Priestess" supplanted "Popess" in the
  occult decks to soften the scandal [@decker1996; @web_traditionaltarot_isis].
- **Sources:** [@dummett1980], [@decker1996]. **Confidence:** high.

## III — L'Impératrice (The Empress)
- **Depicts:** A crowned woman enthroned, holding a **sceptre** and a **shield bearing an
  eagle** (the heraldic imperial eagle, carried down from the Italian *trionfi*). She is
  numbered **III** [@web_empress_wp; @tarot_heritage].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Worldly feminine authority and fertility; the eagle and crown
  let her be read as terrestrial sovereignty paired against the Popess's spiritual one
  [@decker1996].
- **Sources:** [@web_empress_wp], [@tarot_heritage]. **Confidence:** high.
- **Grammar note:** the grammar's Iconography line says "Conver's block mislabels her 'II'; she
  is trump III." The Empress is firmly **III** in the tradition, and the grammar's own `number`
  field is 3; the "mislabel" claim could not be corroborated and is flagged as a likely error
  (confidence: low on the mislabel) — see ERRORS in the return.

## IIII — L'Empereur (The Emperor)
- **Depicts:** A bearded sovereign seated in profile, one leg crossed over the other in the
  **figure of four**, sceptre in hand and the **eagle shield** beside him. Numbered with the
  additive **IIII** [@web_marseille_wp; @dummett1980].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Authority, structure, reason ordering the world — the masculine
  counterpart the occultists paired with the Empress; the figure-4 posture was later mined for
  numerological symbolism [@decker1996].
- **Sources:** [@web_marseille_wp], [@dummett1980]. **Confidence:** high.

## V — Le Pape (The Pope / Hierophant)
- **Depicts:** A tiara'd pope raises his right hand in blessing over **two tonsured clerics**
  seen from behind, a pillar at either side, a triple cross / staff in his hand [@dummett1980;
  @tarot_heritage].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Teaching, tradition, the bridge between human and sacred; the
  occult decks renamed him "Hierophant" (and the Besançon variant replaced him with **Jupiter**)
  to detach him from the Roman Church [@decker1996].
- **Sources:** [@dummett1980], [@decker1996]. **Confidence:** high.

## VI — L'Amoureux (The Lover / The Lovers)
- **Depicts:** A youth stands **between two women**, under a **blindfolded Cupid** in a sunburst
  drawing his bow — the image of a choice between two ways (often read as virtue vs. vice)
  [@web_marseille_wp; @dummett1980].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Love and union, but above all a **choice / crossroads**; the
  RWS tradition later simplified the trio to a single couple under an angel, losing the choice
  motif the Marseille preserves [@decker1996; @web_benebell_lovers].
- **Sources:** [@web_marseille_wp], [@dummett1980], [@decker1996]. **Confidence:** high.

## VII — Le Chariot (The Chariot)
- **Depicts:** A crowned prince stands in a **canopied chariot drawn by two horses** facing
  slightly different ways; he holds **no reins**. The Conver block carries the engraver's
  initials **"V T" on the chariot's shield** — a key clue in the dating puzzle [@dummett1980;
  @web_vs_conver_heritage].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Victory and mastery — opposing forces (the two horses) held
  together and advanced by will [@decker1996].
- **Sources:** [@dummett1980], [@web_vs_conver_heritage]. **Confidence:** high (the "V T"
  detail: medium — reported by Camoin/letarot, consistent with the inherited-block thesis).

## VIII — La Justice (Justice)
- **Depicts:** An enthroned woman, eyes open, holds an **upright sword** in one hand and a
  **level balance** in the other. In this deck Justice is **VIII** [@web_marseille_wp;
  @dummett1980].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Balance, fairness, consequence — the exact weighing of acts.
  The Golden Dawn / RWS later **swapped** her with Strength (Justice → XI) to fit Libra; the
  Marseille keeps her at VIII [@web_gd_swap].
- **Sources:** [@web_marseille_wp], [@web_gd_swap], [@dummett1980]. **Confidence:** high.

## VIIII — L'Hermite (The Hermit)
- **Depicts:** A cloaked old man walks with a staff, lifting a **lantern half-shielded by his
  cloak**. Numbered with the additive **VIIII** [@dummett1980; @web_marseille_wp].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Solitude, prudence, the inner light carried one step at a time;
  Gébelin's circle read the shaded lantern as wisdom concealed [@decker1996].
- **Sources:** [@dummett1980], [@web_marseille_wp]. **Confidence:** high.

## X — La Roue de Fortune (Wheel of Fortune)
- **Depicts:** A wheel on a frame turns: **animal-eared creatures rise on one side and fall on
  the other**, with a sphinx-like or crowned figure perched at the top [@dummett1980;
  @web_marseille_wp].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Change, cycles, fortune turning; the occultists loaded the
  three figures with Egyptian/sphinx symbolism the medieval image never carried [@decker1996].
- **Sources:** [@dummett1980], [@web_marseille_wp]. **Confidence:** high.

## XI — La Force (Strength)
- **Depicts:** A woman in a **wide brimmed hat** (echoing the Bateleur's) calmly **parts the
  jaws of a lion**. In this deck Strength is **XI** [@web_marseille_wp; @web_gd_swap].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Courage, the gentle mastery of instinct — force governed by
  spirit. The Golden Dawn / RWS swapped her to **VIII** (for Leo); the Marseille keeps XI
  [@web_gd_swap].
- **Sources:** [@web_marseille_wp], [@web_gd_swap]. **Confidence:** high.

## XII — Le Pendu (The Hanged Man)
- **Depicts:** A youth hangs **by one ankle** from a beam slung between two lopped trees, his
  **hands behind his back** and his free leg crossed behind the other; his face is calm, not
  agonised [@web_marseille_wp; @dummett1980].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Suspension, sacrifice, the world seen upside-down. Gébelin
  notoriously claimed the card was "really" **Prudence** printed upside-down, and Etteilla
  built a Prudence trump from it — a famous misreading of a *traitor's-punishment* image
  [@web_researchgate_prudence; @decker1996].
- **Sources:** [@web_marseille_wp], [@decker1996], [@web_researchgate_prudence].
  **Confidence:** high.

## XIII — [Death] (La Mort) — numbered but unnamed
- **Depicts:** A **skeleton with a scythe** mows a black ground from which **hands, feet, and
  crowned heads** sprout. The card bears its number **XIII but no title** — a Marseille
  convention [@web_tower_wp; @dummett1980].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Transformation, the necessary clearing-away. The unnamed-13
  taboo became, in the occult tradition, the fixed "Death = XIII" association — which Etteilla
  pointedly *broke* by renumbering Death to 17 [@decker1996].
- **Sources:** [@dummett1980], [@decker1996]. **Confidence:** high.

## XIIII — Tempérance (Temperance)
- **Depicts:** A **winged angel** pours liquid **between two vessels** in an unbroken stream.
  Additive numeral **XIIII** [@web_marseille_wp; @dummett1980].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Moderation, harmony, the patient blending of opposites; the
  flowing stream became the alchemical "tempering" of the later esoteric decks [@decker1996].
- **Sources:** [@web_marseille_wp], [@dummett1980]. **Confidence:** high.

## XV — Le Diable (The Devil)
- **Depicts:** A horned, bat-winged figure with breasts and a second face on the belly stands
  on a **pedestal raising a torch**; **two small horned captives** are chained to the pedestal
  below [@web_marseille_wp; @dummett1980].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Instinct, desire, bondage — matter's magnetic pull; the
  occultists made him Baphomet/the shadow, Lévi explicitly identifying the Devil card with his
  Baphomet figure [@levi1856; @decker1996].
- **Sources:** [@web_marseille_wp], [@levi1856], [@decker1996]. **Confidence:** high.

## XVI — La Maison Dieu (The Tower)
- **Depicts:** **Lightning (or fire from the sky) strikes a tower**, knocking off its crown-like
  top; **two figures fall headlong** amid scattered coloured spheres / "tears." Its name, *La
  Maison Dieu* — "the House of God" — is older than "the Tower" [@web_tower_wp; @dummett1980].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Sudden upheaval, the breaking of false structures, liberation
  through shock; the occultists tied it variously to the Tower of Babel and to divine
  chastisement [@decker1996].
- **Sources:** [@web_tower_wp], [@dummett1980], [@decker1996]. **Confidence:** high.

## XVII — L'Étoile (The Star)
- **Depicts:** A **naked woman kneels by water**, pouring **two urns** — one onto the land, one
  into the stream — beneath **one great star and seven smaller stars**; a bird perches on a
  bush behind her [@web_marseille_wp; @dummett1980].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Hope, renewal, serenity, the gift freely poured; the eight
  stars and the figure were read as celestial influence / the soul under the stars
  [@decker1996; @web_traditionaltarot_star].
- **Sources:** [@web_marseille_wp], [@decker1996]. **Confidence:** high.

## XVIII — La Lune (The Moon)
- **Depicts:** Under a **dripping face-moon**, **two dogs (or a dog and a wolf) bay** between
  **two towers**, while a **crayfish / crustacean climbs from a pool** in the foreground
  [@web_marseille_wp; @dummett1980].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** The unconscious, dreams, illusion, the dim winding path; the
  crayfish and twin towers became symbols of the threshold and the lower world [@decker1996].
- **Sources:** [@web_marseille_wp], [@dummett1980]. **Confidence:** high.

## XVIIII — Le Soleil (The Sun)
- **Depicts:** **Two children** stand by a **low wall** beneath a great **rayed face-sun** that
  drips golden "tears." Additive numeral **XVIIII** [@web_marseille_wp; @dummett1980].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Joy, clarity, vitality, the warmth of conscious understanding
  and shared life; the twin children read as harmony / the reconciled pair [@decker1996].
- **Sources:** [@web_marseille_wp], [@dummett1980]. **Confidence:** high.

## XX — Le Jugement (Judgement)
- **Depicts:** An **angel sounds a trumpet** from the clouds (often with a banner); **three
  figures rise from a tomb**, arms raised [@web_marseille_wp; @dummett1980].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Awakening, renewal, a decisive call answered — the Christian
  Resurrection image re-read by the occultists as initiatory rebirth [@decker1996].
- **Sources:** [@web_marseille_wp], [@dummett1980]. **Confidence:** high.

## XXI — Le Monde (The World)
- **Depicts:** A **dancing figure within a pointed-oval wreath (mandorla)**, with the **four
  living creatures — angel, eagle, lion, bull** — in the four corners [@web_marseille_wp;
  @dummett1980].
- **Seeds the occult reading:** Completion, fulfilment, integration; the four creatures
  (the Evangelist tetramorph) were read as the four elements / fixed zodiac signs and the
  cosmos harmonised [@decker1996].
- **Sources:** [@web_marseille_wp], [@dummett1980]. **Confidence:** high.

## The 56 Minors (by suit)

The minors are **non-scenic / emblematic**: the pip cards show the suit symbol repeated in
the woodcut's symmetrical floral scrollwork, with **no narrative scene** — the contrast with
the later Rider-Waite illustrated pips [@web_marseille_wp; @thetarotreader_marseille]. Each
suit runs **Ace–Ten** plus four courts: **Roi (King), Reine (Queen), Cavalier (Knight),
Valet (Page)** [@web_marseille_wp; @depaulis1984]. The elemental labels below (Earth/Water/
Air/Fire) are the **later occult correspondence**, not native to the 1760 game pack
[@decker2002; @decker1996]. No native upright/reversed meaning exists for any of these cards;
the meanings circulating with them are the same post-1780s overlay [@decker1996].

### Deniers — Coins (Earth)
- **Depicts:** Coins (roundels) arranged symmetrically, the **Ace** a single large ornamental
  coin filling the card; the courts — **Valet, Cavalier, Reine, Roi** — are standing/seated
  figures bearing the coin emblem in flat woodcut colour [@web_marseille_wp].
- **Cards:** Ace, Two, Three, Four, Five, Six, Seven, Eight, Nine, Ten; Page (Valet), Knight
  (Cavalier), Queen (Reine), King (Roi).
- **Seeds the occult reading:** later tied to **money, body, work, the material world** (Earth);
  the ten pips were eventually mapped to numerological/astrological values by Etteilla and his
  heirs [@decker1996]. **Sources:** [@web_marseille_wp], [@depaulis1984]. **Confidence:** high
  (iconography); the elemental/numerological gloss is overlay.

### Coupes — Cups (Water)
- **Depicts:** Cups/chalices arranged symmetrically with floral scrollwork; the **Ace of Cups**
  is an elaborate **castle-like or fountain-like covered chalice** — the most ornate of the
  Cups; courts as above [@web_marseille_wp].
- **Cards:** Ace–Ten; Page, Knight, Queen, King.
- **Seeds the occult reading:** later tied to **love, feeling, relationship** (Water)
  [@decker1996]. **Sources:** [@web_marseille_wp], [@depaulis1984]. **Confidence:** high
  (iconography); elemental gloss is overlay.

### Épées — Swords (Air)
- **Depicts:** **Curved, interlaced swords** woven into a symmetrical lattice (the even-numbered
  cards curving to enclose a central motif), with **straight blade(s)** appearing among them;
  the **Ace** is a single upright sword, often crowned, gripped by a hand; courts as above
  [@web_marseille_wp].
- **Cards:** Ace–Ten; Page, Knight, Queen, King.
- **Seeds the occult reading:** later tied to **thought, conflict, the intellect** (Air)
  [@decker1996]. **Sources:** [@web_marseille_wp], [@depaulis1984]. **Confidence:** high
  (iconography); elemental gloss is overlay.

### Bâtons — Batons (Fire)
- **Depicts:** **Batons / staves** crossed in a symmetrical lattice (mirroring the Swords'
  construction but with straight clubs), the **Ace** a single large knobbed baton gripped by a
  hand; courts as above [@web_marseille_wp].
- **Cards:** Ace–Ten; Page, Knight, Queen, King.
- **Seeds the occult reading:** later tied to **energy, will, action** (Fire) [@decker1996].
  **Sources:** [@web_marseille_wp], [@depaulis1984]. **Confidence:** high (iconography);
  elemental gloss is overlay.
