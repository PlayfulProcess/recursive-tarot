# Belgian Tarot — Vandenborre / 'Tarot Flamand' (c. 1780) — card by card

**Source of imagery:** the woodcut-and-stencil **Tarot Flamand** of the Vandenborre /
Rouen-Brussels pattern, public-domain "Tarot Belgijski" scans on Wikimedia Commons (c. 1780)
[@web_aeclectic_flamand; @web_ipcs_belgian]. This grammar is **trumps-only** (22 atouts); the
56 minors are documented in the deck dossier but not imaged here [@web_aeclectic_flamand].

**A standing note on every card.** This is a **GAME deck** of the **Belgian / Rouen-Brussels**
line — descended from **Viéville** (c. 1650), *not* the Tarot de Marseille
[@dummett1980; @web_th_rouen_bruxelles]. It carries **no native divinatory meaning**, so no
"Means" line is given. The **"Changed from parent"** line measures each card against the
**Viéville** ancestor, and — where the contrast is sharper — against the **Marseille** standard.
Card titles are reproduced in the workshop's own (often misspelt) French. The order is the
southern **C order**: Justice VIII, Strength XI, Fool unnumbered [@web_aeclectic_flamand;
@web_belgian_wp].

---

## I — Le Bateleux (The Magician / Mountebank)
- **Depicts:** A standing mountebank behind his table of conjuror's implements — the
  fairground sleight-of-hand artist, the lowest trump [@web_aeclectic_flamand; @dummett1980].
- **Changed from parent:** Essentially unchanged from the Viéville/Marseille Bateleur; the
  workshop spelling *Le Bateleux* is the only oddity [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_flamand], [@dummett1980]. **Confidence:** high.

## II — L'Espagnol Capitano Eracasse (the Spanish Captain) — *replaces La Papesse*
- **Depicts:** A **swaggering soldier** — the braggart **Captain ("Fracasse") of the Commedia
  dell'arte**, a stock blustering coward in plumed hat — standing where the Popess sits in the
  Marseille. The card's design is reported to derive from an **engraving by Michel Lasne**
  [@web_ipcs_belgian; @web_th_spanish_captain].
- **Changed from parent:** **The signature Belgian substitution.** Where the Marseille (and the
  Viéville base) place **La Papesse** — the scandalous female pope — the Belgian pattern puts a
  secular comic soldier, removing the heterodox figure entirely. This swap is **not** in
  Viéville's surviving deck; it is a defining feature added by the Belgian/Vandenborre pattern
  [@web_ipcs_belgian; @web_th_spanish_captain; @web_belgian_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_ipcs_belgian], [@web_th_spanish_captain], [@web_belgian_wp].
  **Confidence:** high (the substitution); medium (the Lasne-engraving source — single-tier).

## III — L'Imperatris (The Empress)
- **Depicts:** A crowned, enthroned woman with sceptre — the Empress [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Changed from parent:** Unchanged in role from Viéville/Marseille; workshop spelling
  *L'Imperatris* [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_flamand], [@dummett1980]. **Confidence:** high.

## IIII — L'Empereur (The Emperor)
- **Depicts:** A bearded sovereign enthroned with sceptre; additive numeral **IIII**
  [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Changed from parent:** Unchanged from Viéville/Marseille [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_flamand], [@dummett1980]. **Confidence:** high.

## V — Bacus (Bacchus) — *replaces Le Pape*
- **Depicts:** **Bacchus, the wine-god, seated astride a barrel** — a playful, secular figure
  where the Marseille has the tiara'd **Pope** blessing two clerics [@web_ipcs_belgian;
  @web_cartes_de_suisses_wp].
- **Changed from parent:** **The second signature Belgian substitution.** The Pope (*Le Pape*)
  is replaced by the wine-god on his barrel; with the Spanish Captain at II, **both Catholic
  clergy are edited out of the trumps** — usually read as an anticlerical / market-widening
  move for the confessionally divided southern Netherlands (motive interpretive). Like the
  Captain, this is a Belgian-pattern addition not found in Viéville's deck
  [@web_ipcs_belgian; @web_th_spanish_captain; @web_cartes_de_suisses_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_ipcs_belgian], [@web_cartes_de_suisses_wp], [@web_th_spanish_captain].
  **Confidence:** high.

## VI — L'Amour (The Lover / The Lovers)
- **Depicts:** The lover(s) beneath a Cupid drawing his bow — the choice/union image
  [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Changed from parent:** Unchanged in substance from the Viéville/Marseille Amoureux; titled
  *L'Amour* [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_flamand], [@dummett1980]. **Confidence:** high.

## VII — Le Chariot (The Chariot)
- **Depicts:** A crowned figure in a canopied chariot drawn by horses [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Changed from parent:** Held at **VII** in the Belgian/Vandenborre running order (matching
  Marseille). Note that the **Viéville** ancestor ordered the virtue/Chariot block differently
  (the Chariot fell at a different number in Viéville); the Vandenborre pattern sits closer to
  the Marseille numbering here [@web_aeclectic_flamand; @web_pollett_vieville].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_flamand], [@dummett1980]. **Confidence:** high (the position);
  medium (the precise Viéville-vs-Belgian reorder detail).

## VIII — La Justice (Justice)
- **Depicts:** An enthroned woman with **sword and balance** — Justice, numbered **VIII**
  [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Changed from parent:** Justice is **VIII** and Strength **XI** — the pre-Golden-Dawn C
  order, as in Marseille (the Golden Dawn/RWS swap is much later and external to this game pack)
  [@web_aeclectic_flamand; @web_belgian_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_flamand], [@web_belgian_wp]. **Confidence:** high.

## VIIII — L'Ermite (The Hermit)
- **Depicts:** A cloaked old man with staff and shielded lantern; additive numeral **VIIII**
  [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Changed from parent:** Role unchanged; held at VIIII in the Belgian running order
  [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_flamand], [@dummett1980]. **Confidence:** high.

## X — La Roue de Fortune (Wheel of Fortune)
- **Depicts:** A turning wheel with figures rising and falling [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Changed from parent:** Unchanged in substance from Viéville/Marseille [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_flamand], [@dummett1980]. **Confidence:** high.

## XI — La Force (Strength)
- **Depicts:** A figure mastering a lion — Strength, numbered **XI** [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Changed from parent:** Strength at **XI** (paired with Justice VIII) — the C order
  [@web_aeclectic_flamand; @web_belgian_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_flamand], [@web_belgian_wp]. **Confidence:** high.

## XII — Lepen-du (The Hanged Man) — *right-side-up*
- **Depicts:** The hanged figure suspended from a beam — but here drawn **head up / right-side-
  up**, not inverted; the workshop misspells the title *Lepen-du* [@web_aeclectic_flamand;
  @web_belgian_wp].
- **Changed from parent:** A **Belgian/Viéville-line feature**: the figure hangs **upright**,
  against the Marseille's pointedly inverted Hanged Man — one of the clearest non-Marseille
  markers, carried forward from the Viéville ancestor [@web_aeclectic_flamand;
  @web_belgian_wp]. (See grammar errors: the grammar's "Le Pendu / The Hanged Man" note does
  not flag the upright orientation.)
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_flamand], [@web_belgian_wp]. **Confidence:** high.

## XIII — La Mort (Death) — *named*
- **Depicts:** A scythe-bearing skeleton; here the card **bears the title *La Mort***
  [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Changed from parent:** Where the **Marseille** deliberately leaves XIII **unnamed**, the
  Belgian card **names Death** outright [@web_aeclectic_flamand; @dummett1980].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_flamand], [@dummett1980]. **Confidence:** medium (the naming is
  reported across the trump-title lists; the unnamed-Marseille contrast is firm).

## XIIII — La Temperance (Temperance)
- **Depicts:** A winged angel pouring liquid between two vessels; additive **XIIII**
  [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Changed from parent:** Unchanged from Viéville/Marseille [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_flamand], [@dummett1980]. **Confidence:** high.

## XV — Le Diable (The Devil)
- **Depicts:** A horned devil figure with captives — the Devil [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Changed from parent:** Unchanged in substance from Viéville/Marseille [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_flamand], [@dummett1980]. **Confidence:** high.

## XVI — La Foudre (The Lightning) — *replaces La Maison Dieu / the Tower*
- **Depicts:** **A tree struck by lightning, a shepherd beneath it** — "the Lightning"
  (*La Foudre*) — not the Marseille's lightning-struck **tower** [@web_aeclectic_flamand;
  @web_belgian_wp].
- **Changed from parent:** A **Belgian/Viéville-line feature**: the struck **tree/shepherd**
  replaces the Marseille's **Maison Dieu** struck tower, carried forward from the Viéville
  ancestor [@web_aeclectic_flamand; @web_belgian_wp; @web_th_rouen_bruxelles]. (See grammar
  errors: the grammar titles this "La Foudre (La Maison-Dieu)" and describes a *tower*, missing
  the tree/shepherd image.)
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_flamand], [@web_belgian_wp]. **Confidence:** high.

## XVII — LE'toille (The Star)
- **Depicts:** The Star card — a star (or stars) above a figure; workshop spelling *LE'toille*
  [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Changed from parent:** The Belgian/Viéville-line Star differs in detail from the Marseille
  kneeling water-pourer (the Rouen-Brussels celestial trio diverges from Marseille); imagery
  here is at display resolution and not closely verified (confidence: medium on the precise
  figure) [@web_aeclectic_flamand; @web_th_rouen_bruxelles].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_flamand], [@web_th_rouen_bruxelles]. **Confidence:** medium.

## XVIII — La Lune (The Moon)
- **Depicts:** The Moon card — a face-moon over the lower world [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Changed from parent:** The Rouen-Brussels Moon differs from the Marseille twin-towers-and-
  crayfish treatment (the Viéville line's celestial cards diverge); precise figure not closely
  verified at this resolution (confidence: medium) [@web_aeclectic_flamand;
  @web_th_rouen_bruxelles].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_flamand], [@web_th_rouen_bruxelles]. **Confidence:** medium.

## XVIIII — Le Soleil (The Sun)
- **Depicts:** A great rayed sun over figure(s); additive numeral **XVIIII**
  [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
- **Changed from parent:** The Belgian/Viéville-line Sun differs from the Marseille twin-

- **Depicts:** An angel sounding a trumpet; the dead rising — Judgement [@web_aeclectic_flamand].
