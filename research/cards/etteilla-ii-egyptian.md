# Etteilla II — Grand Livre de Thot (Égyptien) — card-by-card

**Source of imagery:** the **Etteilla I — Livre de Thot** designs (Alliette / Basan, 1788–89),
reprinted by **Simon-François Blocquel** (as "Julia Orsini") c. 1838–40 in the new "Lismon" style:
**full French titles printed on both sides of each card**, four-corner numbering, top/bottom
upright-vs-reversed meaning blocks, a single black-line inner border, **stencilled blue borders**,
and a six-colour palette (light blue, red, green, yellow, gold, peach) [@web_mccloskys_lismon;
@web_steve_grandet; @web_druidcraft]. **Five trumps (1, 10, 12, 15, 21) are redrawn**; the rest are
faithful reprints [@web_steve_grandet; @web_benebell_etteilla].

**How to read this file.** Etteilla II inherits the *entire* Etteilla I system — same 1–78 numbering,
same cosmogony→virtues→reworked-Marseille trump order, same querent significators (male = 1 Le
Chaos, female = 8 Le Repos), same upright/reversed divinatory wordlists. So the **"Changed from
parent" line here is the delta against Etteilla I, not against Marseille** (for the Marseille
ancestry of each card, see `research/cards/etteilla-i-livre-de-thot.md`). For most cards the delta is
**"title now printed in full on both sides; image reprinted unchanged."** Only the redrawn trumps and
the re-titled Card 13 carry a substantive block.

> A numbering note carried from the parent: **La Folie** is the unnumbered **Fool / 0**, filed last
> (sheet position 78). The **female significator is Card 8 (Le Repos)**, *not* La Folie — the repo
> grammar mislabels Card 78 as "The Questioner — Female" and should be corrected to put the
> female-significator tag on Card 8 [@web_etteilla_org].

## The trumps (1–21) + the Fool — the delta

### Redrawn trumps (the substantive image changes)

## 1 — Le Chaos (The Void / male significator)
- **Identity & meaning:** unchanged from Etteilla I — the primordial void, male querent's
  significator; upright the matter full of unformed potential, reversed the void felt as emptiness.
- **Changed from parent (Etteilla I):** **image redrawn.** The pre-creation void is redrawn
  **without the sun** — logical, since in the cosmogony the sun is not yet created. Full title now
  printed both sides [@web_steve_grandet].
- **Sources:** [@web_steve_grandet], [@web_etteilla_org]
- **Confidence:** medium (redraw detail from steve-p.org, single strong secondary)

## 10 — La Tempérance (Temperance)
- **Identity & meaning:** unchanged — second cardinal virtue; upright moderation/management,
  reversed excess.
- **Changed from parent (Etteilla I):** **image redrawn.** The figure no longer pours liquid between
  two vessels; instead she holds an **elephant's bridle ("the Bridle of Restraint")** with an
  **elephant beside her** — a 17th-century animal correspondence for Temperance imported into the
  redraw [@web_steve_grandet; @web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Sources:** [@web_steve_grandet], [@web_benebell_etteilla]
- **Confidence:** medium-high (two corroborating secondaries)

## 12 — La Prudence (Prudence)
- **Identity & meaning:** unchanged — fourth cardinal virtue, Etteilla's substitute for the Hanged
  Man; upright foresight, reversed imprudence.
- **Changed from parent (Etteilla I):** **image redrawn.** Instead of a figure stepping around a
  serpent on the ground, the snake now **climbs her arm**, and she holds a **mirror** in that hand
  (self-knowledge + wisdom) — closer to the classical Prudence emblem [@web_steve_grandet].
- **Sources:** [@web_steve_grandet]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 15 — Le Magicien ou le Bateleur (The Magician / Illness)
- **Identity & meaning:** unchanged — the demoted Bateleur, governing **illness/the body** in
  Etteilla's system; upright skill/the body, reversed charlatanry/malady worsening.
- **Changed from parent (Etteilla I):** **image redrawn.** The magician now stands to the **right**
  of the table (was the left), with a small **statue (some read a homunculus)** added on the table
  before him [@web_steve_grandet].
- **Sources:** [@web_steve_grandet]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 21 — Le Despote Africain (The African Despot)
- **Identity & meaning:** unchanged — the reworked Marseille **Chariot** (not the Emperor — see
  parent file) read as a despotic sovereign; upright great power/protector, reversed tyranny.
- **Changed from parent (Etteilla I):** **image redrawn.** The chariot now drives **right-to-left**
  (was left-to-right) — a mirror-flip of the composition [@web_steve_grandet].
- **Sources:** [@web_steve_grandet]
- **Confidence:** medium

### The re-titled trump (the title delta that matters)

## 13 — Le Grand Prêtre (The High Priest) — was Etteilla I's "Le Mariage"
- **Identity & meaning:** the **same card** as Etteilla I's **Card 13, Le Mariage** ("Marriage /
  Union"), itself the reworked Marseille **Lovers / L'Amoureux (VI)**. The divinatory sense is
  inherited: upright marriage, union, counsel, sanction, a trusted guide; reversed a broken or false
  bond, hollow dogma, a bad counsellor [@web_etteillastrumps; @web_steve_grandet].
- **Changed from parent (Etteilla I):** **re-titled, not re-identified.** Etteilla II/III print the
  card as **"Le Grand Prêtre"** — naming the **officiant** who solemnises the marriage rather than
  the union itself. This is why modern keys (and the repo grammar) attach it to the **Hierophant**;
  but the card's lineage is the **Lovers → Etteilla's Marriage**, and the High-Priest reading is a
  **posthumous title drift**, shared with Etteilla III [@web_steve_grandet; @web_druidcraft;
  @web_etteillastrumps]. **— This is the title that misleads the grammar's `arcana:the-hierophant`
  mapping (see corrections).**
- **Sources:** [@web_steve_grandet], [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_druidcraft]
- **Confidence:** medium-high (Lovers ancestry well attested; "Grand Prêtre" Type II/III title
  documented)

### The faithfully reprinted trumps (title-printing delta only)

For each of the following the **identity, number, and divinatory meaning are exactly Etteilla I's**;
the only Etteilla II delta is that the **full French title is now printed on both sides** of the
reprinted image, in the new blue-bordered, four-corner-numbered style [@web_druidcraft;
@web_mccloskys_lismon]. See `research/cards/etteilla-i-livre-de-thot.md` for each card's depiction,
meaning, and Marseille ancestry.

## The minors (22–77) — the delta

**No systemic change from Etteilla I.** The four suits (Bâtons/Fire, Coupes/Water, Épées/Air,
Deniers/Earth), the element-and-domain coding, the **court-card-as-a-person** convention, the pip
keyword lists, and the upright/reversed structure are all **inherited unchanged** from Etteilla I
[@web_etteilla_org; @web_druidcraft]. The Etteilla II delta on the minors is **purely the new print
style**: the suit/rank title printed in full on both sides, four-corner numbering, blue stencil
border, six-colour palette [@web_mccloskys_lismon; @web_steve_grandet].

- **22–35 Bâtons** (Roi, Reine, Chevalier, Valet, Dix→As) — reprints in the new style; courts =
  persons of enterprise/work/countryside.
- **36–49 Coupes** — reprints; courts = persons of love/affection/the heart.
- **50–63 Épées** — reprints; courts = persons of conflict/law/arms.
- **64–77 Deniers** — reprints; courts = persons of money/trade/property; the Coins carry the
  planetary correspondences as in Etteilla I.
- **Changed from parent (Etteilla I):** new titling/border/colour only; no change of identity,
  meaning, element, or number [@web_druidcraft].
- **Confidence:** medium-high (system inheritance high; that *no* minor was redrawn is asserted from
  the "only trumps 1,10,12,15,21 changed" sources — confidence medium that no minor redraw exists).

> **Grammar caution (minors).** `tarot/etteilla-ii-egyptian/grammar.json` maps every minor to an
> **RWS `card:*` archetype** and describes **RWS scenic pips** ("sunflower," "bandage," "laurel,
> horse, crowd," "flight, arrows"). Etteilla minors are **Marseille-derived near-pips**, not
> Smith's 1909 scenes; the RWS imagery language is **anachronistic** and should be flagged or
> rewritten. The element/domain framing the sections also give is correct and inherited from
> Etteilla I.
