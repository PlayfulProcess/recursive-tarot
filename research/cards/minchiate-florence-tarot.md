# Minchiate (Florence) — card-by-card

Header. **97 cards** = the **Fool (unnumbered)** + **40 numbered trumps** + **56 suit cards**.
Imagery from a Florentine printed Minchiate, **c. 1860–1890** (public-domain Wikimedia Commons
scan). This is a **game** pack: the "Means" lines below give the **iconographic subject and the
card's place in the game**, not divinatory meanings, which this deck does not natively carry
[@web_minchiate_wp; @web_pagat_minchiate; @dummett1980]. Trumps are grouped by `trump_group`;
minors by suit. The **zodiac, the four elements, and the added virtues (Prudence + the three
theological virtues) have no standard 22-trump equivalent and are preserved, not normalised.**

---

## The Fool

### 0 — The Fool (Il Matto)
- **Depicts:** A ragged madman, sometimes with feathers and a hobby-horse; a man in a feathered
  hat holding a stick with two balls [@web_pagat_minchiate; @web_steve_minchiate].
- **Plays as:** The unnumbered *excuse* — no trick-taking power, but can be played to any trick
  instead of following suit or trumping; can be added to a scoring sequence (*versicola*)
  [@web_pagat_minchiate].
- **Sources:** [@web_pagat_minchiate], [@web_steve_minchiate], [@web_minchiate_wp]
- **Confidence:** high

## The Five Papi (I–V) — NO standard equivalent for II–IV; CORRECTION OWED

> The five lowest trumps are collectively the *papi* ("popes"). **The Minchiate has neither a
> Popess nor a Pope:** the Popess was dropped, and the Pope/Empress/Emperor cards were recast as
> a Grand Duke and two Emperors; Love was demoted into the group [@web_minchiate_wp;
> @web_pagat_minchiate; @web_tarotforum_granduke]. The repo grammar mislabels these — flagged
> per card below.

### I — Papa One (correctly: the Bagatto / Juggler)
- **Depicts:** A seated, crowned figure (grammar). Scholarly identity: **Papa uno = the Bagatto**,
  the Juggler/Magician of ordinary tarot, here absorbed into the papi [@web_minchiate_wp;
  @web_pagat_minchiate].
- **Plays as:** Lowest trump (I).
- **Changed from parent:** The Bagatto/Magician, normally trump I of the standard series, is here
  renamed and grouped with the "popes" [@web_minchiate_wp].
- **Grammar correction:** grammar labels this only "Papa One" and glosses the papi as
  Popess/Empress/Emperor/Pope/Love — should read **Bagatto** [@web_pagat_minchiate].
- **Sources:** [@web_minchiate_wp], [@web_pagat_minchiate]
- **Confidence:** high

### II — Papa Two (correctly: the Grand Duke; grammar says "The Empress")
- **Depicts:** A crowned papal-type figure (grammar). Scholarly identity: **the Grand Duke** — the
  old Empress recast as a secular, politically neutral figure [@web_minchiate_wp;
  @web_tarotforum_granduke].
- **Plays as:** Trump II.
- **Changed from parent:** The **Empress** is **secularised into the Grand Duke** (secular crown,
  not a papal/imperial diadem) — part of the Florentine accommodation to Church sensitivity
  [@web_minchiate_wp].
- **Grammar correction:** grammar's "(The Empress)" names the *source* card but not the Minchiate
  subject (**Grand Duke**) [@web_tarotforum_granduke].
- **Sources:** [@web_minchiate_wp], [@web_tarotforum_granduke]
- **Confidence:** high

### III — Papa Three (correctly: the Western Emperor; grammar says "The Emperor")
- **Depicts:** A crowned figure. Scholarly identity: **the Emperor of the West** [@web_minchiate_wp;
  @web_pagat_minchiate].
- **Plays as:** Trump III.
- **Changed from parent:** The single Emperor of standard tarot becomes a **pair** of Emperors
  (West/East); this is no. III, the **Western Emperor** [@web_minchiate_wp].
- **Grammar correction:** name should specify **Western Emperor** [@web_pagat_minchiate].
- **Sources:** [@web_minchiate_wp], [@web_pagat_minchiate]
- **Confidence:** high

### IV — Papa Four (correctly: the Eastern Emperor; grammar says "The Pope")
- **Depicts:** A crowned figure. Scholarly identity: **the Emperor of the East** — **the Pope's
  slot is occupied by a second Emperor, not a Pope** [@web_minchiate_wp; @web_pagat_minchiate].
- **Plays as:** Trump IV.
- **Changed from parent:** **The Pope is removed** and replaced by the **Eastern Emperor**; the
  West/East pairing is thought to predate 1453 (confidence: medium — iconographic inference)
  [@web_minchiate_wp].
- **Grammar correction:** grammar's "(The Pope)" is wrong for the Minchiate subject — there is **no
  Pope card**; this is the **Eastern Emperor** [@web_pagat_minchiate; @web_tarotforum_granduke].
- **Sources:** [@web_minchiate_wp], [@web_pagat_minchiate]
- **Confidence:** high

### V — Papa Five (Love)
- **Depicts:** A couple, sometimes beneath Cupid (the old Love card) [@web_steve_minchiate].
- **Plays as:** Highest of the five papi (V).
- **Changed from parent:** **Love is demoted into the papi group** as its top member, because the
  five lowest trumps were all called *papi* [@web_minchiate_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_minchiate_wp], [@web_steve_minchiate]
- **Confidence:** high

### XI — The Hunchback / Time (Il Gobbo)
- **Depicts:** A bent old man with a stick, sometimes an hourglass — *Il Gobbo*, the Hunchback.
- **Means:** **Time / the Old Man** — the figure that, in the wider tradition, becomes the Hermit;
  trump XI [@web_steve_minchiate; @web_minchiate_wp].
- **Changed from parent:** The standard Hermit/Time card is named the **Hunchback** here.
- **Grammar note:** grammar maps to `arcana:the-hermit` `mapping_confidence: exact`; "loose" is
  more honest (it is the Time/Old-Man ancestor of the Hermit).
- **Sources:** [@web_steve_minchiate], [@web_minchiate_wp]
- **Confidence:** medium (the Hermit equation is conventional, not exact)

### XII — The Hanged Man (L'Impiccato)

- **Confidence:** high

### XIII — Death (La Morte)

### XIV — The Devil (Il Diavolo)

### XV — The House of the Devil / Tower (La Casa del Diavolo)

## The added virtues (XVI–XIX) — Prudence + the three theological virtues; NO standard equivalent

> These four are the heart of the Minchiate's "complete seven virtues." **They have no
> counterpart in the 22-trump tarot** and are preserved as added trumps [@web_minchiate_wp;
> @web_williams_minchiate].

### XVI — Hope (La Speranza)
- **Depicts:** A woman with an anchor, gazing upward in prayer.
- **Means:** Hope — a **theological** virtue; trump XVI. **Added trump, no standard equivalent.**
- **Sources:** [@web_minchiate_wp], [@web_williams_minchiate]
- **Confidence:** high

### XVII — Prudence (La Prudenza)
- **Depicts:** A woman with a mirror and a serpent, contemplating herself.
- **Means:** Prudence — the **fourth cardinal** virtue, absent from standard tarot and **restored
  here**, completing the cardinal set; trump XVII. **Added trump, no standard equivalent.**
- **Sources:** [@web_minchiate_wp], [@web_williams_minchiate]
- **Confidence:** high

### XVIII — Faith (La Fede)
- **Depicts:** A woman with cross and chalice.
- **Means:** Faith — a **theological** virtue; trump XVIII. **Added trump, no standard equivalent.**
- **Sources:** [@web_minchiate_wp], [@web_williams_minchiate]
- **Confidence:** high

### XIX — Charity (La Carità)
- **Depicts:** A woman nursing children — the image of Caritas.
- **Means:** Charity — a **theological** virtue, "the greatest of the three"; trump XIX.
  **Added trump, no standard equivalent.**
- **Sources:** [@web_minchiate_wp], [@web_williams_minchiate]
- **Confidence:** high

## Suit cards (56)

> Four Latin suits, Ace–10 + four courts (King/Re, Queen/Regina, Knight/Cavallo, Page/Fante).
> **Peculiarities preserved:** the **knights are centaurs/sphinxes**, and in **Cups and Coins the
> page is a woman (a *fantina*/maid)** [@web_minchiate_wp]. Pips are **non-scenic** — suit-emblems
> in the Florentine pattern with the elaborate Minchiate borders [@web_steve_minchiate]. The
> suit→element pairings are a modern overlay, not a game feature. Card-level meanings are not
> native to this game pack.

### Batons (Bastoni) — paired with Fire
- **Ace–10 of Batons:** non-scenic pip cards, the baton-emblems in the Florentine pattern.
- **Page (Fante), Knight (Cavallo, centaur/beast), Queen (Regina), King (Re) of Batons:** the
  four-card court.
- **Sources:** [@web_minchiate_wp], [@web_steve_minchiate] — **Confidence:** high

### Coins (Denari) — paired with Earth
- **Ace–10 of Coins:** non-scenic pip cards, the coin-emblems.
- **Maid (fantina, female page), Knight (centaur/beast), Queen, King of Coins:** the court — note
  the **female page** [@web_minchiate_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_minchiate_wp], [@web_steve_minchiate] — **Confidence:** high

### Cups (Coppe) — paired with Water
- **Ace–10 of Cups:** non-scenic pip cards, the cup-emblems.
- **Maid (fantina, female page), Knight (centaur/beast), Queen, King of Cups:** the court — note
  the **female page** [@web_minchiate_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_minchiate_wp], [@web_steve_minchiate] — **Confidence:** high

### Swords (Spade) — paired with Air
- **Ace–10 of Swords:** non-scenic pip cards, the sword-emblems (curved/interlaced in the Latin
  pattern).
- **Page (Fante), Knight (Cavallo, centaur/beast), Queen (Regina), King (Re) of Swords:** the
  four-card court.
- **Sources:** [@web_minchiate_wp], [@web_steve_minchiate] — **Confidence:** high
