# "Charles VI" Tarot (Gringonneur) — card-by-card

**Deck:** `charles-vi-tarot` · **Surviving cards:** 17 (16 trumps + the Page of Swords) ·
**Held by:** Bibliothèque nationale de France, Cabinet des Estampes (Rés. Kh 24) ·
**Source of imagery:** the BnF Gallica/Essentiels scans of the original gold-ground paintings,
attributed by the BnF to the Florentine workshop of Apollonio di Giovanni & Marco del Buono,
c. 1460 [@web_bnf_cat_charlesvi; @web_gallica_charlesvi_fou; @web_th_charlesvi_benedetti].

> **No native meanings.** These are *trionfi* game cards; any upright/reversed "meaning" is a
> later (post-1781) overlay, so blocks below give iconography and the triumph depicted, not
> fortune-telling [@dummett1980]. **Order caveat:** the cards are unnumbered; the numbering is the
> conventional 22-trump scale, but this is a **southern / Florentine** sequence whose A-vs-B family
> is debated (all virtues below Death; Chariot below the virtues) [@web_pollett_orders; @dummett1980].

**The six trumps that do NOT survive:** Magician (Bagatto), Popess, Empress, Wheel of Fortune,
Devil, Star [@web_intomystery_oldest; @web_th_charlesvi_tag].

---

## 0 — The Fool (Il Matto)
- **Depicts:** A bearded man in belled motley with a club, with children or small figures about
  his legs — the licensed court fool [@web_bnf_essentiels_charlesvi; @web_gallica_charlesvi_fou].
- **Position in the order:** The Fool stands **outside / below the numbered trumps** — unnumbered,
  the "wild" card not part of the graded triumph sequence [@dummett1980].
- **Means (game deck):** No native divinatory meaning; iconographically, folly and the figure who
  stands apart from the order of estates.
- **Sources:** [@web_bnf_essentiels_charlesvi], [@web_gallica_charlesvi_fou], [@dummett1980].
- **Confidence:** high

## 4 — The Emperor (L'Imperatore)
- **Depicts:** A crowned, bearded emperor enthroned with orb and sceptre, attendants below — temporal
  majesty in gold leaf [@web_tarotgarden_charlesvi].
- **Position in the order:** Among the **low trumps**, the secular power paired against the Pope; in
  this southern/Florentine sequence the papal/imperial group sits beneath the virtues
  [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Means (game deck):** Worldly authority, the throne, the order of empire.
- **Sources:** [@web_tarotgarden_charlesvi], [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Confidence:** high

## 5 — The Pope (Il Papa)
- **Depicts:** A tiara'd pope enthroned, hand raised in blessing, cardinals/clerics at his feet —
  the spiritual power answering the Emperor [@web_tarotgarden_charlesvi].
- **Position in the order:** Paired with the Emperor in the low trumps [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Means (game deck):** Spiritual authority, blessing, the sanctioned hierophant.
- **Sources:** [@web_tarotgarden_charlesvi], [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Confidence:** high

## 6 — The Lovers (L'Amore)
- **Depicts:** A richly dressed couple beneath a winged Cupid drawing his bow — a courtly
  betrothal / dynastic-marriage scene [@web_tarotgarden_charlesvi; @web_intomystery_oldest].
- **Position in the order:** A low trump, above the imperial pair [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Means (game deck):** Union, love, the binding of two houses.
- **Sources:** [@web_tarotgarden_charlesvi], [@web_intomystery_oldest].
- **Confidence:** high

## 7 — The Chariot (Il Carro)
- **Depicts:** A crowned lady standing in a triumphal car drawn by two horses — the *trionfo* that
  names the game [@web_tarotgarden_charlesvi; @web_th_charlesvi_benedetti].
- **Position in the order:** Diagnostic. In the pseudo-Charles VI (Florentine) order the **Chariot
  sits *below* the virtues** — the placement Dummett uses to distinguish it from Bologna (where the
  Chariot is above); the Florentine line later raises it under the Wheel [@web_pollett_orders;
  @dummett1980].
- **Means (game deck):** Victory, the self carried forward in triumph.
- **Sources:** [@web_pollett_orders], [@dummett1980], [@web_tarotgarden_charlesvi].
- **Confidence:** medium (order placement is the load-bearing, debated point)

## 8 — Justice (La Giustizia)
- **Depicts:** An enthroned woman with raised sword and balance — the cardinal virtue
  [@web_tarotgarden_charlesvi].
- **Position in the order:** In B-type (Ferrarese) sequences **Justice sits high, in eighth place**;
  here, as in southern orders, the virtues group sits **below Death** [@web_pollett_orders;
  @web_thf_charlesvi].
- **Means (game deck):** Balance, judgment, the weighing of each act.
- **Sources:** [@web_pollett_orders], [@web_thf_charlesvi].
- **Confidence:** medium (exact rank depends on the A/B question)

## 9 — The Hermit / Time (Il Tempo)
- **Depicts:** An old bearded man with a staff and an **hourglass** — Time / the Old Man, not yet
  the later lantern-bearing Hermit [@web_th_charlesvi_benedetti; @web_tarotgarden_charlesvi].
- **Position in the order:** A middle trump; the hourglass marks him as *Il Tempo* rather than a
  solitary penitent [@web_th_charlesvi_benedetti].
- **Means (game deck):** Time, age, the solitary measure of hours.
- **Sources:** [@web_th_charlesvi_benedetti], [@web_tarotgarden_charlesvi].
- **Confidence:** medium

## 11 — Strength / Fortitude (La Forza)
- **Depicts:** A woman mastering a beast or breaking a column (the Fortitude / Samson type) — the
  cardinal virtue [@web_tarotgarden_charlesvi; @web_th_charlesvi_benedetti].
- **Position in the order:** Within the virtues, which in this southern sequence sit **below Death**
  [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Means (game deck):** Fortitude, courage, strength of soul over brute force.
- **Sources:** [@web_tarotgarden_charlesvi], [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Confidence:** medium

## 12 — The Hanged Man (L'Appeso)
- **Depicts:** A youth suspended by one ankle from a frame — a *pittura infamante*, the shaming
  portrait of a traitor hung upside-down [@web_th_charlesvi_benedetti; @web_intomystery_oldest].
- **Position in the order:** A high-middle trump, just under Death [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Means (game deck):** The punished betrayer; suspension and the world seen upside-down.
- **Sources:** [@web_th_charlesvi_benedetti], [@web_intomystery_oldest].
- **Confidence:** medium

## 13 — Death (La Morte)
- **Depicts:** A skeletal figure **on horseback** riding over fallen kings and commoners — the
  mounted Death of the *danse macabre* [@web_th_charlesvi_benedetti; @web_tarotgarden_charlesvi].
- **Position in the order:** The hinge of a southern order — **all four virtues sit below Death**,
  the cosmological/eschatological trumps above [@web_pollett_orders; @dummett1980].
- **Means (game deck):** The great leveller; the end that spares no estate.
- **Sources:** [@web_pollett_orders], [@dummett1980], [@web_th_charlesvi_benedetti].
- **Confidence:** high

## 14 — Temperance (La Temperanza)
- **Depicts:** A winged woman pouring liquid between two vessels — the cardinal virtue
  [@web_tarotgarden_charlesvi].
- **Position in the order:** A virtue; in B-type orders Temperance is noted as falling **below the
  lowest triumph of the upper (celestial) segment** — one of the markers used to classify the family
  [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Means (game deck):** Tempering, moderation, the patient blending of waters.
- **Sources:** [@web_tarotgarden_charlesvi], [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Confidence:** medium

## 16 — The Tower / House of the Devil (La Casa del Diavolo / La Maison-Dieu)
- **Depicts:** Figures falling from a burning/breaking structure — the early "House of the Devil"
  (Maison-Dieu) Tower; a rare 15th-century survival of this subject
  [@web_bnf_essentiels_charlesvi; @web_intomystery_oldest].
- **Position in the order:** Among the **upper (celestial/eschatological) trumps**, above Death
  [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Means (game deck):** Sudden ruin, the breaking of false structures.
- **Sources:** [@web_bnf_essentiels_charlesvi], [@web_intomystery_oldest], [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Confidence:** medium

## 18 — The Moon (La Luna)
- **Depicts:** **Two astronomers measuring with compasses** beneath a crescent moon — an
  astronomers' Moon, NOT the later dogs-towers-and-crayfish scene
  [@web_th_charlesvi_benedetti; @web_intomystery_oldest].
- **Position in the order:** One of the graded **celestial triad** (Star–Moon–Sun) characteristic of
  the eastern/B family; here the Star is the trump that did **not** survive [@web_pollett_orders;
  @web_intomystery_oldest].
- **Means (game deck):** The measured night; the sciences of the heavens.
- **Sources:** [@web_th_charlesvi_benedetti], [@web_intomystery_oldest], [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Confidence:** medium

## 19 — The Sun (Il Sole)
- **Depicts:** A **woman with a distaff** seated beneath a great rayed sun — an image distinctive
  to this deck [@web_th_charlesvi_benedetti; @web_tarotgarden_charlesvi].
- **Position in the order:** The top of the celestial triad, beneath the highest trumps
  [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Means (game deck):** Daylight, vitality, the lit and ordinary world.
- **Sources:** [@web_th_charlesvi_benedetti], [@web_tarotgarden_charlesvi].
- **Confidence:** medium

## 20 — Judgement (Il Giudizio)
- **Depicts:** An angel sounding a trumpet as the dead rise from their tombs — the Last Judgement
  [@web_tarotgarden_charlesvi].
- **Position in the order:** Among the highest trumps, near the close of the sequence
  [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Means (game deck):** The call and the reckoning; awakening.
- **Sources:** [@web_tarotgarden_charlesvi], [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Confidence:** medium

## 21 — The World (Il Mondo)
- **Depicts:** A **standing female figure holding a globe/orb** (sources also describe a censer in
  the other hand), a panorama of a walled city over water shown on the globe — the highest triumph
  [@web_th_charlesvi_benedetti; @web_intomystery_oldest].
- **Position in the order:** The **top trump** of the sequence in this deck [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Means (game deck):** Completion, the cosmos surveyed, the journey closed.
- **Sources:** [@web_th_charlesvi_benedetti], [@web_intomystery_oldest], [@web_pollett_orders].
- **Confidence:** medium (exact attributes — orb vs censer — need a direct look at the Gallica scan)

## Page (Knave) of Swords (Fante di Spade)
- **Depicts:** A young courtier holding an upright sword — the **sole surviving suit card** of the
  pack, showing the minors were painted to the same luxury standard as the trumps
  [@web_bnf_cat_charlesvi; @web_intomystery_oldest].
- **Position in the order:** Not a trump — a **court card of the Swords suit**, outside the triumph
  sequence [@web_bnf_cat_charlesvi].
- **Means (game deck):** None native; iconographically the junior court figure of the sword suit.
- **Sources:** [@web_bnf_cat_charlesvi], [@web_intomystery_oldest].
- **Confidence:** high
