# Golden Dawn (Book T) Tarot — card-by-card

**Deck:** `golden-dawn-book-t-tarot` · **Count:** 78 (22 Major + 56 Minor) ·
**Source of imagery:** Rider-Waite-Smith, 1909 — designed by A. E. Waite, **drawn in full by
Pamela Colman Smith** (`pamela-colman-smith`). **Source of system:** Book T (Liber T),
Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn, c.1891, principally MacGregor Mathers (`macgregor-mathers`).

**How to read each block.** Majors carry the **Marseille source** plus the **Book T
correspondence** (Hebrew letter, Tree-of-Life path, astrological attribution, GD title).
Minors carry the **suit/element**, and pips Two–Ten carry the **decan** and **"Lord of…"
title**; the *"Changed from parent"* line shows how **Smith's scene visualises that Book-T
decanic title** — her signal innovation, since Marseille pips were bare suit-signs.

**Cross-cutting note — the Strength↔Justice swap.** Book T assigns **Leo to Strength** (letter
Teth) and **Libra to Justice** (letter Lamed); because Teth precedes Lamed in the alphabet,
**Strength is renumbered VIII and Justice XI** — reversing the Marseille order. Both cards below
are documented with the swap applied (as in RWS) [@web_gd_swap; @web_mysticsym_swap;
@web_strength_wp].

## Major Arcana (22 trumps)

## 0 — The Fool (*The Spirit of Æther*)
- **Depicts:** A youth at a cliff-edge, white rose and wallet, small dog at his heels — innocence stepping into the abyss [@waite1910].
- **Correspondences:** Hebrew **א Aleph** ('ox'), path 11; attribution **Air** (a "mother letter" elemental trump) [@web_cunliffe_correspondences].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Boundless potential, beginnings, holy folly / recklessness, a leap taken in ignorance.
- **Changed from parent:** Marseille *Le Mat* is an unnumbered wanderer; Book T fixes it as **0/Air/Aleph** at the head of the alphabet, and RWS poses it at the cliff to stage "the spirit stepping into incarnation" [@web_cunliffe_correspondences; @waite1910].
- **Sources:** [@waite1910], [@web_cunliffe_correspondences]
- **Confidence:** high

## 1 — The Magician (*The Magus of Power*)
- **Depicts:** A figure with one hand raised to heaven, one to earth; the four suit-emblems on his table, lemniscate overhead [@waite1910].
- **Correspondences:** Hebrew **ב Beth** ('house'), path 12; attribution **Mercury** [@web_cunliffe_correspondences].
- **Means:** Will and skill uniting the elements, the word made act / trickery, willpower scattered or misused.
- **Changed from parent:** Marseille *Le Bateleur* is a market conjuror; Book T elevates him to **Mercury/Beth**, and RWS arrays all four suit-tools to show conscious direction of force [@web_cunliffe_correspondences; @waite1910].
- **Sources:** [@waite1910], [@web_cunliffe_correspondences]
- **Confidence:** high

## 2 — The High Priestess (*The Priestess of the Silver Star*)
- **Depicts:** Veiled woman enthroned between pillars B and J, lunar crown, Torah scroll [@waite1910].
- **Correspondences:** Hebrew **ג Gimel** ('camel'), path 13; attribution **the Moon** [@web_cunliffe_correspondences].
- **Means:** Hidden wisdom, intuition, the lunar current / secrets withheld, intuition clouded.
- **Changed from parent:** Marseille *La Papesse* becomes the GD's lunar **Gimel** path crossing the Abyss; RWS veils her before the sanctuary [@web_cunliffe_correspondences; @waite1910].
- **Sources:** [@waite1910], [@web_cunliffe_correspondences]
- **Confidence:** high

- **Majors & pip decans/titles: high.** Every Hebrew letter, Tree path, attribution, decan, and
  "Lord of…" title above is cross-checked against the deck's own `grammar.json`, the davidcunliffe
  GD correspondence tables, Mary K. Greer's GD Minor Arcana, and the Lotus Pond esoteric-titles
  list — and they agree [@web_cunliffe_correspondences; @web_greer_gd_minors; @web_silverlotus_titles].

  mismatch**: the RWS imagery uses **Page/Knight/Queen/King**, whereas Book T's own courts are
  **King/Queen/Prince/Princess**. The mapping used above (RWS Knight→GD King, RWS King→GD Prince,
  RWS Page→GD Princess) is the standard reconciliation but is an interpretive overlay, not a
  one-to-one identity — flagged accordingly [@web_cunliffe_correspondences].
- **Scene descriptions** follow Waite's *Pictorial Key* and the public-domain art; where a detail
  is conventional rather than in Waite's text it is kept brief.
- **Grammar errors observed** (beyond the keyword-dedup already fixed): see the deck dossier's
  "Open questions" — the court-naming mismatch and the elemental-trumps "modern outer-planet" note
  are the two to carry into the grammar's `_research`.
