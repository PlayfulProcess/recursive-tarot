# Tarot de Besançon / Swiss 1JJ — Card-by-Card

**Deck:** Tarot de Besançon / Swiss 1JJ (`tarot-de-besancon`) · **Parent:**
Tarot de Marseille (`tarot-de-marseille-conver`) · **Count:** 78 (the grammar samples 13
representative cards: 9 trumps + 4 minors).

**Source of imagery:** public-domain **Swiss 1JJ** scans (Wikimedia Commons, Category: Tarot
1JJ); some cards are **trilingually titled** in French / German / English
[@web_aeclectic_1jj; @web_learntarot_1jj].

**The one rule of this deck.** It is a **Marseille Type-II variant** whose *only* departure
from the parent is the substitution of **Juno for the Popess (II)** and **Jupiter for the
Pope (V)**. Every other card is **standard Tarot de Marseille** (C-order, Justice VIII /
Strength XI, unnamed Death XIII, non-scenic pips). The **"Changed from parent"** line is
therefore load-bearing only for Juno and Jupiter; for the rest it records *"unchanged —
standard Marseille."* [@web_besancon_wp; @web_th_besancon; @dummett1980].

---

## II — Juno (Junon; in place of La Papesse)
- **Depicts:** The goddess **Juno** standing with her **peacock** — the trump that, in the
  Marseille, is the Popess (a tiara-crowned woman with a book). One of the two "J" cards that
  give the **1JJ** its name [@web_besancon_wp; @web_aeclectic_1jj; @web_th_besancon].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** none natively — a game card. The High-Priestess-style
  reading-meanings later attached to the Popess archetype are an occult overlay, not part of
  the Besançon object [@decker1996; @dummett1980]. (Grammar maps it loosely to
  *arcana:the-high-priestess*.)
- **Changed from parent:** **THE defining change.** The Marseille's doctrinally awkward female
  "Pope" (Popess II) is **replaced by Juno with her peacock** — a secular classical deity of
  the right "rank" (queen of the Roman gods) that gives no offence to Protestant or partisan
  Catholic card-buyers. The number (II) and trump position are unchanged; only the figure and
  title change [@web_besancon_wp; @web_th_besancon; @web_swiss1jj_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_besancon_wp], [@web_th_besancon], [@web_aeclectic_1jj], [@dummett1980]
- **Confidence:** high

## V — Jupiter (in place of Le Pape)
- **Depicts:** **Jupiter** enthroned with his **eagle** (and commonly a **thunderbolt**) —
  the trump that, in the Marseille, is the blessing Pope. The second "J" of the 1JJ
  [@web_besancon_wp; @web_aeclectic_1jj].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** none natively — a game card; Hierophant-style meanings are a
  later overlay [@decker1996; @dummett1980]. (Grammar maps it loosely to
  *arcana:the-hierophant*.)
- **Changed from parent:** the **second defining change.** The Marseille's **Pope (V)** is
  **replaced by Jupiter with his eagle** — again a neutral classical substitute of matching
  rank (king of the gods). With Juno, this **removes both Church figures from the deck**, the
  hallmark of the Besançon / Swiss type. Number (V) and position unchanged
  [@web_besancon_wp; @web_th_besancon; @web_swiss1jj_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_besancon_wp], [@web_th_besancon], [@web_aeclectic_1jj], [@dummett1980]
- **Confidence:** high

## Emergence node — Juno & Jupiter: The Besançon Substitution
(*composite of Juno II + Jupiter V — the grammar's `emg-juno-jupiter` item*)
- **What changed:** the deck's **single defining move** — Popess (II) → **Juno** (peacock),
  Pope (V) → **Jupiter** (eagle), removing both Catholic clergy. The two "J" cards name the
  **Swiss 1JJ** [@web_besancon_wp; @web_swiss1jj_wp; @web_th_besancon].
- **Why:** made for **Protestant and mixed-confessional regions** (eastern France,
  Switzerland, the Rhineland) where the clerical figures were unwelcome; the neutral classical
  deities let the Marseille pattern travel into Reformed territory without disturbing the game.
  Often printed **trilingually** (French/German/English) [@web_besancon_wp; @web_th_besancon;
  @web_aeclectic_1jj].
- **Confidence:** high (motive stated as the standard inference, not a documented maker's
  statement [@web_besancon_wp]).
