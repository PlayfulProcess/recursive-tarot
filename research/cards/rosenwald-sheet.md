# The Rosenwald Sheet — Trump Images on the Sheets

**Deck:** `rosenwald-sheet` · **Surviving:** three uncut sheets (one trump, two court/number) that
**do not complete any known pack** [@web_th_rosenwald; @web_tsr_rosenwald] ·
**Source of imagery:** National Gallery of Art, Rosenwald Collection, open access, c. 1500
Florence [@nga_rosenwald]. The trumps are woodcut and plain, and carry **no native divinatory
meaning** (a printed game sheet) [@dummett1980]. This file documents the **A-order trump repertoire**
and the diagnostic ordering — *not* a settled, complete card list (confidence: low). "Means" and
"Changed from parent" lines omitted.

## The diagnostic A-order feature

The single most load-bearing observation: the trump **ordering follows the A (Florentine–Bolognese)
order**, with the Virtues sequenced **Justice, Fortitude, Chariot, Wheel** — the marker that places
the sheet in the southern branch (against the Milanese C-order) [@web_th_rosenwald; @dummett1980].
The sheet also includes a **Popess** among the early trumps [@web_th_rosenwald].

- **Confidence:** medium (the A-order placement is well established)

## Trump repertoire on the trump sheet

The trump sheet carries the standard early A-order tarot trumps — including the celestial-light
group (**Star, Moon, Sun**), the high trumps (**World**, the Angel/**Judgement**), **Death**, the
**Tower**/**Devil** group, **Temperance**, the **Hanged Man**, the **Wheel of Fortune**, the
**Chariot**, **Justice**, **Fortitude**, the **Lovers**, the papal/imperial group (**Pope, Popess,
Emperor, Empress**), the **Bagatto (Magician)** and the **Fool** [@web_th_rosenwald;
@web_ladyheather_rosenwald] (confidence: low — the exact roster and which trumps are fully vs partly
preserved is not verified here; treat as the A-order repertoire, not an inventory).

- **Depicts (all):** the corresponding A-order trump image, woodcut, plain, untitled
  [@web_th_rosenwald].
- **Confidence:** low

## Court and number cards

The other **two sheets** carry **court and number (pip) cards** in the Italian four-suit system
[@web_th_rosenwald; @web_tsr_rosenwald].

- **Confidence:** low

## Note

Because a **fourth block is missing**, the surviving sheets do **not** complete a pack; do not infer
a card count [@web_tsr_rosenwald]. The value of the material is that it is among the **earliest
woodcut witnesses of the A-order (Florentine) tarot**, stylistically tied to minchiate
[@web_th_rosenwald; @web_minchiate_early_wp].
