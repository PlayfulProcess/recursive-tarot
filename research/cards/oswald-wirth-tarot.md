# Oswald Wirth Tarot — Card-by-card

**Deck:** `oswald-wirth-tarot` (`derives_from: [tarot-de-marseille-conver]`).
**Count:** 22 Major Arcana only (no minors, no courts).
**Source of imagery:** Wirth's drawings, **1889 BnF edition** of *Les 22 Arcanes du Tarot
Kabbalistique* (and the 1926 revision where noted), via Wikimedia Commons [@web_wirth_wp;
@web_gallica_wirth].

## How to read the "Changed from parent" line

Wirth's parent is the **Tarot de Marseille**. He keeps each trump's **Marseille name, number
and basic scene** but (a) **adds a Hebrew letter** per **Éliphas Lévi's** scheme, (b) layers on
**Kabbalistic / alchemical / Masonic** symbolism, and (c) **keeps the Marseille order** —
crucially **Strength = XI, Justice = VIII** (he refused the Golden Dawn swap)
[@web_gd_swap; @web_levi_arrangement]. The decisive correspondence fact for every card:
**Lévi assigns Aleph to Trump I (the Magician) and runs the alphabet up the numbered trumps in
order; the Fool (Le Mat = Shin) is unnumbered and sits between Trump XX and Trump XXI** — *not*
the Golden Dawn's Fool-first-as-Aleph arrangement [@hermetics_marathakis;
@web_levi_arrangement].

## 1 — The Magician (Le Bateleur)
- **Depicts:** A young juggler at a table bearing the four suit-emblems (cup, coin, sword,
  baton), one hand raised to heaven and one lowered to earth; a **lemniscate (∞)** hovers over
  his head in Wirth's design [@web_wirth_wp; @web_22arcanes].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Will, initiative, mastery of the four elements, conscious
  beginning / trickery, misused skill, empty cleverness.
- **Changed from parent:** Marseille's *Bateleur* is a fairground conjuror; Wirth makes him the
  **human will gathering the four elements**, adds the **lemniscate** of eternal force, and
  assigns him **א Aleph ("ox"), the first letter** — because Lévi held "his body and arms
  constitute the letter Aleph," the seed of the whole trump↔alphabet scheme [@hermetics_marathakis;
  @web_levi_arrangement]. In the **1926** redraw the wand's blue/red ends are inverted and the
  table carries **one** object rather than three [@web_cartomancer].
- **Sources:** [@web_wirth_wp], [@hermetics_marathakis], [@web_levi_arrangement], [@web_cartomancer]
- **Confidence:** high

## 2 — The High Priestess (La Papesse)
- **Depicts:** A veiled, enthroned woman with an open book on her knees and a lunar crescent
  near her; she is Gnosis, the secret doctrine.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Intuition, latent knowledge, the inner sanctuary /
  secrets misused, surface piety, sterile knowledge.
- **Changed from parent:** Keeps Marseille's "female Pope," recasts her as **passive Kabbalistic
  wisdom**, and assigns **ב Beth ("house")**, the **2nd** letter. **Most-revised card between
  editions:** in **1889** she has **dark skin and black-and-red columns** (a fin-de-siècle
  Egyptianising touch); in **1926** she is Westernised to **light skin with blue-and-red
  columns** [@web_cartomancer; @web_wirth_wp].
- **Sources:** [@web_cartomancer], [@web_wirth_wp], [@web_levi_arrangement]
- **Confidence:** high

## 3 — The Empress (L'Impératrice)
- **Depicts:** A winged, crowned woman with sceptre and an eagle-emblazoned shield, seated amid
  abundance; generative Nature.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Fecundity, creativity, abundance / sterility, vanity,
  squandered creative energy.
- **Changed from parent:** Retains the Marseille Empress but stresses **Venusian generation**
  and clothes spirit in matter; assigns **ג Gimel ("camel")**, the **3rd** letter [@web_levi_arrangement].
- **Sources:** [@web_wirth_wp], [@web_levi_arrangement]
- **Confidence:** high

## 4 — The Emperor (L'Empereur)
- **Depicts:** A bearded sovereign in profile, one leg crossed in the figure of four, sceptre in
  hand, eagle at his side; realised, ordering power.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Authority, stability, reason imposing order / tyranny,
  rigidity, oppression.
- **Changed from parent:** Keeps the Marseille Emperor; emphasises **structure and law**;
  assigns **ד Daleth ("door")**, the **4th** letter [@web_levi_arrangement]. *(The grammar's
  "Jupiter, Aries in later systems" note conflates Lévi's planetary attribution with the Golden
  Dawn's zodiacal one — flag.)*
- **Sources:** [@web_wirth_wp], [@web_levi_arrangement]
- **Confidence:** high

## 5 — The Hierophant (Le Pape)
- **Depicts:** A tiara'd pontiff blessing two kneeling acolytes, crossed keys at his feet; the
  revealer who mediates between heaven and seeker.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Teaching, religion, transmission of doctrine / dogmatism,
  hollow orthodoxy.
- **Changed from parent:** Keeps Marseille's *Pape*; frames him as **the bridge / teaching
  principle**; assigns **ה He ("window")**, the **5th** letter — Lévi explicitly gives the fifth
  letter to the fifth trump [@hermetics_marathakis; @web_levi_arrangement].
- **Sources:** [@web_wirth_wp], [@hermetics_marathakis], [@web_levi_arrangement]
- **Confidence:** high

## 6 — The Lovers (L'Amoureux)
- **Depicts:** A youth between two women while a winged Cupid draws his bow above — vice on one
  side, virtue on the other; the will at a crossroads.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Love, union, and above all **moral choice** / indecision,
  temptation, a union founded on weakness.
- **Changed from parent:** Reads the Marseille "choice" scene as a **test of the will**; assigns
  **ו Vau ("nail, hook")**, the **6th** letter [@web_levi_arrangement].
- **Sources:** [@web_wirth_wp], [@web_levi_arrangement]
- **Confidence:** high

## 7 — The Chariot (Le Chariot)
- **Depicts:** A crowned prince in a canopied chariot drawn by **two sphinxes** (one light, one
  dark) pulling in different directions; he governs opposing forces by will, holding no reins.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Victory, mastery, holding contraries in harness / loss of
  control, conflict, triumph turned to defeat.
- **Changed from parent:** **Major iconographic departure** — Wirth follows **Lévi in replacing
  the Marseille horses with two Egyptian-style sphinxes** (active/passive principles), one of the
  clearest "Lévi-isms" on the card; assigns **ז Zain ("sword")**, the **7th** letter
  [@web_th_chariot; @web_levi_arrangement].
- **Sources:** [@web_th_chariot], [@web_levi_arrangement], [@web_wirth_wp]
- **Confidence:** high

## 8 — Justice (La Justice)
- **Depicts:** An enthroned woman with upright sword and level balance, eyes open; the law of
  equilibrium and exact return.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Balance, fairness, accountability / injustice, imbalance,
  severity without mercy.
- **Changed from parent:** Keeps Marseille's Justice **at VIII** — **Wirth did NOT adopt the
  Golden Dawn swap** that moves Justice to XI; he explicitly **emphasised the number 8** for
  Justice (balance, not the zodiacal reshuffle) [@web_gd_swap]. Assigns **ח Cheth ("fence,
  field")**, the **8th** letter [@web_levi_arrangement].
- **Sources:** [@web_gd_swap], [@web_levi_arrangement]
- **Confidence:** high

## 9 — The Hermit (L'Ermite)
- **Depicts:** A cloaked old man with a lantern half-shielded by his mantle, leaning on a staff;
  prudence and the inner light carried through the dark.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Wisdom, introspection, the patient search / isolation,
  miserliness with one's light, avoidance.
- **Changed from parent:** Keeps the Marseille Hermit; frames the shielded lantern as **prudent,
  guarded illumination**; assigns **ט Teth ("serpent")**, the **9th** letter [@web_levi_arrangement].
- **Sources:** [@web_wirth_wp], [@web_levi_arrangement]
- **Confidence:** high

## 10 — Wheel of Fortune (La Roue de Fortune)
- **Depicts:** A wheel on its axle: a creature rising on one side, another falling on the other,
  a **sword-bearing sphinx in equilibrium above**.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Change, destiny, the turning of circumstance / bad luck,
  resistance to change, being dragged by the wheel.
- **Changed from parent:** Follows **Lévi's "cosmogonical wheel"** almost exactly — a rising
  figure (Hermanubis) and a falling one (Typhon) with **a sphinx holding a sword above**, the
  card Lévi tied to his **ROTA/TARO** wheel-of-the-word; assigns **י Yod ("hand")**, the **10th**
  letter [@web_th_wheel; @web_levi_rota; @web_levi_arrangement].
- **Sources:** [@web_th_wheel], [@web_levi_rota], [@web_levi_arrangement]
- **Confidence:** high

## 11 — Strength (La Force)
- **Depicts:** A serene woman, a **lemniscate** over her head, calmly opening (or closing) the
  jaws of a lion; spiritual force quietly governing instinct.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Courage, gentle mastery of passion, force directed by spirit /
  brute force, domination by appetite.
- **Changed from parent:** Keeps Strength **at XI** — again, **no Golden Dawn swap** (Golden
  Dawn moves Strength to VIII to align with Leo) [@web_gd_swap]. Adds the **lemniscate** echoing
  the Magician; assigns **כ Kaph ("palm of the hand")**, the **11th** letter [@web_levi_arrangement].
- **Sources:** [@web_gd_swap], [@web_levi_arrangement], [@web_wirth_wp]
- **Confidence:** high

## 12 — The Hanged Man (Le Pendu)
- **Depicts:** A youth hanging by one foot from a gibbet between two trees, hands behind his
  back, free leg crossed; his face calm — voluntary sacrifice and the world seen anew.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Sacrifice, suspension, surrender of ego / useless sacrifice,
  stagnation, attachment refusing to let go.
- **Changed from parent:** Keeps the Marseille hanging figure; reads the inverted posture as
  **willed reversal / initiation**; assigns **ל Lamed ("ox-goad")**, the **12th** letter — the
  **French/Lévi** attribution. **Note the divergence:** the **Golden Dawn** gives this card
  **Mem (water)** instead; Wirth follows Lévi's **Lamed**, *not* Mem [@web_hangedman_letters;
  @web_levi_arrangement]. *(The grammar's "element of Water" note is the Golden Dawn reading and
  contradicts Wirth's Lamed — flag.)*
- **Sources:** [@web_hangedman_letters], [@web_levi_arrangement]
- **Confidence:** high

## 13 — Death (La Mort)
- **Depicts:** A skeleton with a scythe mowing a field where hands, feet and crowned heads
  sprout from the soil; often left **unnamed** in Marseille decks. Transformation, not
  extinction.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Transformation, necessary endings, the clearing that makes
  room / stagnation, fear of change, decay resisted.
- **Changed from parent:** Keeps the Marseille trump XIII (Wirth reads it as **regeneration**);
  assigns **מ Mem ("water")**, the **13th** letter in Lévi's sequence [@web_levi_arrangement].
- **Sources:** [@web_wirth_wp], [@web_levi_arrangement]
- **Confidence:** high

## 14 — Temperance (La Tempérance)
- **Depicts:** A winged angel pouring an unbroken stream between two vessels; the circulation of
  the life-force.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Moderation, harmony, the blending of opposites / imbalance,
  impatience, dissipation.
- **Changed from parent:** Keeps the Marseille angel; frames the pouring as **vital circulation**;
  assigns **נ Nun ("fish")**, the **14th** letter [@web_levi_arrangement].
- **Sources:** [@web_wirth_wp], [@web_levi_arrangement]
- **Confidence:** high

## 15 — The Devil (Le Diable)
- **Depicts:** A horned, bat-winged figure with a torch over two small bound figures chained to
  its pedestal; the fascination of matter that binds when unmastered.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Instinct, desire, the binding power of appetite — neutral
  energy that enslaves only when unconscious / liberation, or deeper enslavement and obsession.
- **Changed from parent:** Keeps the Marseille Devil but reads it through **Lévi's magnetism /
  the Baphomet current** (instinctual force, not a literal demon); assigns **ס Samekh ("prop,
  support")**, the **15th** letter [@web_levi_wp; @web_levi_arrangement].
- **Sources:** [@web_wirth_wp], [@web_levi_arrangement]
- **Confidence:** high

## 16 — The Tower (Le Feu du Ciel)
- **Depicts:** Lightning striking the crown from a tower; two figures fall as fire rains down.
  Wirth **titles it "Le Feu du Ciel"** — the Fire of Heaven.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Sudden upheaval, the breaking of illusions, liberation through
  catastrophe / disaster resisted, an inevitable collapse, clinging to ruins.
- **Changed from parent:** Keeps the Marseille *Maison-Dieu* image but **renames it Le Feu du
  Ciel**, reframing the struck tower as a higher force shattering false structures; assigns
  **ע Ayin ("eye")**, the **16th** letter [@web_22arcanes; @web_levi_arrangement]. *(Title on the
  BnF plate — confidence: medium.)*
- **Sources:** [@web_22arcanes], [@web_levi_arrangement]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 17 — The Star (Les Étoiles)
- **Depicts:** A naked woman kneeling by water, pouring from two urns onto land and stream,
  beneath a great eight-pointed star and seven lesser stars; hope, nature renewing herself.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Hope, inspiration, serenity, renewal after catastrophe /
  despair, disillusion, hope clouded.
- **Changed from parent:** Keeps the Marseille Star-pourer; assigns **פ Pe ("mouth")**, the
  **17th** letter [@web_levi_arrangement].
- **Sources:** [@web_wirth_wp], [@web_levi_arrangement]
- **Confidence:** high

## 18 — The Moon (La Lune)
- **Depicts:** Two dogs baying under a dripping moon while a crayfish climbs from a pool, between
  two towers; illusion, instinct, the perilous nocturnal path of the soul.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** The unconscious, dreams, the dim winding path / deception,
  fear, illusions taken for truth.
- **Changed from parent:** Keeps the Marseille Moon scene; assigns **צ Tzaddi ("fish-hook")**,
  the **18th** letter — the **Lévi/Continental** attribution. **Note:** the Golden Dawn's later
  "Tzaddi is not the Star" controversy concerns *their* reshuffled scheme, not Wirth's; in Lévi's
  order Tzaddi simply falls on the 18th trump (the Moon) [@web_levi_arrangement].
- **Sources:** [@web_wirth_wp], [@web_levi_arrangement]
- **Confidence:** high

## 19 — The Sun (Le Soleil)
- **Depicts:** Two children within a walled garden beneath a radiant, human-faced sun; daylight,
  consciousness, restored innocence.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Joy, clarity, vitality, conscious life / vanity, a clouded
  sun, false brilliance.
- **Changed from parent:** Keeps the Marseille Sun-with-children; assigns **ק Qoph ("back of the
  head")**, the **19th** letter [@web_levi_arrangement].
- **Sources:** [@web_wirth_wp], [@web_levi_arrangement]
- **Confidence:** high

## 20 — Judgement (Le Jugement)
- **Depicts:** An angel sounding a trumpet from the clouds; below, the dead rise from a tomb
  with arms raised; awakening and regeneration.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Awakening, renewal, the call to rise / stagnation, a call
  refused, the trumpet unheard.
- **Changed from parent:** Keeps the Marseille resurrection scene; assigns **ר Resh ("head")**,
  the **20th** letter [@web_levi_arrangement].
- **Sources:** [@web_wirth_wp], [@web_levi_arrangement]
- **Confidence:** high

## — The Fool (Le Fou / Le Mat)
- **Depicts:** A wanderer in motley striding forward with a bundle on a staff, a creature tearing
  at his leg and a crocodile waiting ahead; the ungovernable spirit.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Freedom, the impulse to move without calculation, spirit that
  cannot be domesticated / aimlessness, madness, dispersion of force.
- **Changed from parent:** Keeps the Marseille *Mat* (unnumbered vagabond) and assigns **ש Shin
  ("tooth, fire")** — Lévi was **the first to link the Fool with Shin**, and Wirth follows him
  [@web_levi_arrangement; @hermetics_marathakis]. **Critical placement point:** in **Lévi's and
  Wirth's scheme the Fool is unnumbered and sits between Trump XX (Judgement) and Trump XXI (The
  World)** — it is *not* "card 0" at the head of the deck. That Fool-first-as-0 = Aleph
  arrangement is the **Golden Dawn's** different system. *(The grammar lists Le Fou at
  sort_order 0 with Shin; the letter is right but the position implies the Golden Dawn order —
  flag.)* [@web_levi_arrangement; @web_gd_swap]
- **Sources:** [@web_levi_arrangement], [@hermetics_marathakis], [@web_gd_swap]
- **Confidence:** high

## 21 — The World (Le Monde)
- **Depicts:** A dancing figure within a laurel wreath surrounded by the four living creatures —
  man, eagle, lion, bull (the four fixed signs / four elements); the completed Great Work.
- **Means (upright / reversed):** Completion, fulfilment, integration, the cosmos harmonised /
  incompletion, a goal almost but not reached, a cycle that fails to close.
- **Changed from parent:** Keeps the Marseille World; assigns **ת Tav ("mark, cross")**, the
  **22nd and final** letter — the seal of the alphabet on the last trump, with the Fool/Shin
  falling just before it in Lévi's order [@web_levi_arrangement].
- **Sources:** [@web_wirth_wp], [@web_levi_arrangement]
- **Confidence:** high

## Corrections owed to the grammar (`tarot/oswald-wirth-tarot/grammar.json`)

1. **Le Fou (arcanum-00-le-fou) is placed at sort_order 0.** The letter **Shin is correct**, but
   in **Lévi's/Wirth's** scheme the Fool is **unnumbered, between XX and XXI** — not at the head
   as card 0. As listed, the grammar silently implies the **Golden Dawn** arrangement, which the
   deck dossier explicitly says Wirth **rejected**. Recommend a note clarifying the Fool's
   Lévi placement.
2. **Le Pendu (12) "Astrological association: the element of Water."** This is the **Golden
   Dawn** reading (Mem = water). Wirth follows Lévi's **ל Lamed**; the Water/Mem note contradicts
   the deck's own correspondence and should be corrected or flagged.
3. **Minor astrology drift.** Several cards (Emperor "Aries in later systems," Hierophant
   "Taurus," etc.) mix Lévi's planetary attributions with Golden-Dawn zodiacal ones under one
   "Astrological association" label. Verify each against Wirth's *Le Tarot des imagiers* before
   treating as Wirth's own.
4. **Typo:** the World card's Correspondences read "the **21th** letter" — should be "the **22nd**
   letter" (Tav is the 22nd Hebrew letter; the World is the 21st *trump*). Note also the
   numbered-trump letters in the grammar are otherwise **correct** for Lévi (Aleph on the
   Magician through Tav on the World).
