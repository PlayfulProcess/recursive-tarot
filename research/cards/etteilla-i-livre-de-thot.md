# Etteilla I — Livre de Thot — card-by-card

**Source of imagery:** Tarot de Marseille trump and suit iconography, re-engraved by
Pierre-François Basan (1788) under Etteilla's direction and overlaid with new titles, numbers,
and printed upright/reversed keywords [@web_pdr_etteilla; @web_etteilla_org]. **Reading the
"Changed from parent" line:** the parent is the Tarot de Marseille trump (or pip) and the
Gébelin order it answers to. Where the scholarship supports a clean correspondence it is given;
where it does not, the block says so and is marked `low`. **Do not normalise** the cards that
have no Marseille equivalent.

> A note on numbering. Etteilla numbers 1–78 in Arabic numerals; the **Fool (La Folie)** is the
> one unnumbered/zero trump and sits last in the file (No. 78 in the deck's running sheet order)
> [@web_etteilla_org]. The male querent significator is **Card 1 (Le Chaos)**, the female is
> **Card 8 (Le Repos)** — both also function as cosmogony trumps [@web_pdr_etteilla].

## The trumps (1–21) + the Fool

## 1 — Le Chaos (Chaos)
- **Depicts:** the primordial void before creation; a turbulent, unformed field. Doubles as the
  **male querent's significator** [@web_pdr_etteilla; @web_etteillastrumps].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — the (eternal) Truth, the primal matter, the
  question as it stands, full of unformed potential; the male consultant himself. Reversed —
  read as the female/animus pole or "physics/material world" in Etteilla's commentary; in
  practice, the matter felt as void rather than promise [@web_etteillastrumps;
  @web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Changed from parent:** **No Marseille equivalent.** This is a *new* trump heading a
  creation sequence Etteilla claimed to read off the Temple of Memphis; nothing in the TdM
  corresponds to it, and it carries the querent-significator function the Marseille game deck
  never had [@web_pdr_etteilla].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_pdr_etteilla], [@web_benebell_etteilla]
- **Confidence:** high (identity); medium (exact keyword list)

## 2 — La Lumière (Light)
- **Depicts:** the first day of creation — "let there be light"; illumination breaking on the
  void [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — light, clarification, enlightenment, the dawning of
  understanding (Etteilla's *éclaircissement*). Reversed — fire/ardour turned destructive;
  clarity flaring into heat [@web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Changed from parent:** **No Marseille equivalent.** A cosmogony card with no TdM trump
  behind it [@web_pdr_etteilla].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_pdr_etteilla]
- **Confidence:** high (identity); medium (keywords)

## 3 — Les Plantes (Plants / Vegetation)
- **Depicts:** the **third** day of creation — the earth bringing forth grass, herbs and trees
  (Etteilla's day-order is non-sequential by number) [@web_pdr_etteilla].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — growth, what is proposed taking root, discourse that
  bears fruit. Reversed — things still fluid, nothing yet rooted [@web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Changed from parent:** **No Marseille equivalent** [@web_pdr_etteilla].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_pdr_etteilla]
- **Confidence:** high (identity); medium (keywords)

## 4 — Le Ciel (The Heavens / Sky)
- **Depicts:** the **second** day — the firmament dividing the waters [@web_pdr_etteilla].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — favour from above, expansion, protection. Reversed —
  a blessing withheld or delayed [@web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Changed from parent:** **No Marseille equivalent** [@web_pdr_etteilla].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_pdr_etteilla]
- **Confidence:** high (identity); medium (keywords)

## 5 — L'Homme et les Quadrupèdes (Man and Quadrupeds)
- **Depicts:** the **sixth** day — land animals and humankind [@web_pdr_etteilla].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — the living world of work, sustenance, natural
  strength. Reversed — the brute in man, appetite unchecked [@web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Changed from parent:** **No Marseille equivalent** [@web_pdr_etteilla].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_pdr_etteilla]
- **Confidence:** high (identity); medium (keywords)

## 6 — Les Astres (The Stars / Celestial Bodies)
- **Depicts:** the **fourth** day — sun, moon and stars set to govern time [@web_pdr_etteilla].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — the stars favourable; hope, guidance, a kindly
  destiny. Reversed — an ill or clouded star, hope deferred [@web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Changed from parent:** **No clean Marseille equivalent.** Imagery overlaps the TdM **Star**
  (XVII), but Etteilla's card is a *creation*-of-the-luminaries card, not the Marseille Star
  figure; treat the overlap as thematic only (confidence: low on any one-to-one mapping)
  [@web_pdr_etteilla; @web_etteillastrumps].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_pdr_etteilla]
- **Confidence:** high (identity); low (parent mapping)

## 7 — Les Oiseaux et les Poissons (Birds and Fishes)
- **Depicts:** the **fifth** day — creatures of air and water [@web_pdr_etteilla].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — air and water teeming; news, messages, journeys,
  plenty. Reversed — idle gossip, scattered news, a delayed voyage [@web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Changed from parent:** **No Marseille equivalent** [@web_pdr_etteilla].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_pdr_etteilla]
- **Confidence:** high (identity); medium (keywords)

## 8 — Le Repos (Rest)
- **Depicts:** the **seventh** day — the Sabbath, God resting and sanctifying rest. Doubles as
  the **female querent's significator** [@web_pdr_etteilla; @web_etteillastrumps].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — nature, rest, tranquillity, retreat, the solitary or
  religious life, silence; the female consultant herself. Reversed — imitation, the Garden of
  Eden, effervescence, fermentation [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Changed from parent:** **No Marseille equivalent.** A cosmogony trump that *also* carries the
  female-querent function the TdM never had. (Corrects the repo grammar, which mislabels Card 78
  / La Folie as the female significator.) [@web_pdr_etteilla; @web_etteillastrumps]
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_pdr_etteilla]
- **Confidence:** high

## 9 — La Justice (Justice)
- **Depicts:** a crowned figure with scales and sword [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — lawful judgment, equity, balance restored. Reversed —
  injustice, bias, "the jurist" / the law misused [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Changed from parent:** answers to the **Marseille Justice (VIII)** — one of the few trumps
  Etteilla keeps recognisable — but renumbered to **9** and re-read as one of his four cardinal
  virtues (cards 9–12) [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps]
- **Confidence:** high

## 10 — La Tempérance (Temperance)
- **Depicts:** a figure pouring liquid between vessels [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — moderation, discretion, continence, sobriety,
  frugality, patience, calm. Reversed — "the wise man," or excess and imbalance
  [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Changed from parent:** answers to the **Marseille Temperance (XIIII)**, renumbered to **10**
  and grouped as a cardinal virtue [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps]
- **Confidence:** high

## 11 — La Force (Strength / Fortitude)
- **Depicts:** a figure mastering a lion (the TdM Strength) [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — strength, ability, moral force, magnanimity,
  perseverance, courage. Reversed — "the sovereign," or strength misused as domination
  [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Changed from parent:** answers to the **Marseille Strength/Fortitude (XI)**, renumbered to
  **11** as a cardinal virtue [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps]
- **Confidence:** high

## 12 — La Prudence (Prudence)
- **Depicts:** a sandalled, caduceus-bearing goddess pausing before a serpent
  [@web_pdr_etteilla; @web_etteillastrumps].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — foresight, careful counsel, practical wisdom.
  Reversed — "the people," imprudence, rashness [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Changed from parent:** answers to the **Marseille Hanged Man (XII)** — Etteilla's most
  ingenious substitution. Turning the Hanged Man upside-down and reading the rope as a serpent
  yields an image of Prudence ("there are snakes in that grass"); he changed the figure's gender
  and renamed it to complete the quartet of virtues. The Hanged Man as a distinct trump
  **disappears** from the deck [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_pdr_etteilla]
- **Confidence:** high

## 13 — Le Mariage (Marriage / Union)
- **Depicts:** a marriage / union scene (the Etteilla II/III line later re-titles it
  "Le Grand Prêtre," a High Priest officiating a marriage — a *later* drift, not Etteilla I's
  own emphasis) [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — marriage, union, a binding agreement. Reversed —
  a union broken, an unequal or false bond [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Changed from parent:** answers to the **Marseille Lovers / L'Amoureux (VI)**, renumbered to
  **13** and narrowed to "Marriage/Union" (Papus and the Etteilla tradition correlate it to the
  Lovers) [@web_etteillastrumps; @web_benebell_etteilla]. **— Corrects the repo grammar, which
  maps Card 13 to the Hierophant/High Priest.** The "High Priest" title is a posthumous Etteilla
  II/III re-labelling of the *image*; the card's identity is the Lovers, its meaning marriage.
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_benebell_etteilla]
- **Confidence:** medium-high (Lovers correspondence well attested; title drift documented)

## 14 — Force Majeure / Le Diable (Major Force / The Devil)
- **Depicts:** the Devil figure of the TdM, re-read [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — Force Majeure, absolute necessity, overpowering
  circumstance, raw force. Reversed — Force Mineure; fury, violence, bondage to one's passions
  [@web_etteillastrumps; @web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Changed from parent:** answers to the **Marseille Devil (XV)**, renumbered to **14** and
  re-conceived as impersonal *force/necessity* rather than only "evil" — Etteilla reads the
  Devil as energy [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_benebell_etteilla]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 15 — Maladie / Le Magicien ou le Bateleur (Illness / The Magician)
- **Depicts:** the juggler/mountebank of the TdM Bateleur [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — illness, malady, a matter of the body; in Etteilla's
  commentary, mental derangement (sorcerers thought to "cast" on the mind). Reversed — a
  charlatan, a false remedy, the malady worsening [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Changed from parent:** answers to the **Marseille Bateleur / Magician (I)** — the *first*
  Marseille trump — but Etteilla demotes it to **15** and assigns it the wholly new meaning of
  **Illness**, severing it from "skill/the conjuror as beginning" [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 16 — Le Jugement Dernier (The Last Judgement)
- **Depicts:** an angel with a trumpet; the dead rising — the TdM Judgement [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — judgement, a decisive change, awakening, renewal.
  Reversed — a verdict postponed, the summons unheeded [@web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Changed from parent:** answers to the **Marseille Judgement (XX)**, renumbered to **16**;
  iconography and core meaning largely preserved [@web_etteillastrumps; @web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_benebell_etteilla]
- **Confidence:** medium-high

## 17 — La Mort / Mortalité (Death / Mortality)
- **Depicts:** the scythe-bearing skeleton of the TdM Death [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — mortality, death, ending, destruction, corruption,
  putrefaction. Reversed — nothingness, inertia, paralysis, lethargy, annihilation
  [@web_etteillastrumps; @web_aeclectic_etteilla].
- **Changed from parent:** answers to the **Marseille Death (XIII)** — and this is **the famous
  renumbering**. In every Marseille deck Death is trump XIII (usually *unnamed*); Etteilla moves
  it to **17** and titles it "Mortalité," deliberately breaking the number-13 association the
  later tradition treats as fixed [@web_etteillastrumps; @web_aeclectic_etteilla]. *(The repo
  prompt frames this as "13 → a different position"; the destination is 17. The repo grammar
  already has 17 correct.)*
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_aeclectic_etteilla]
- **Confidence:** high

## 18 — Le Capucin (The Capuchin / Hermit)
- **Depicts:** a hooded monk (Capuchin) with staff — the TdM Hermit reframed
  [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — the solitary, concealment, a thing hidden, a
  traitor or prudent withdrawal. Reversed — a secret enemy unmasked, treachery exposed
  [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Changed from parent:** answers to the **Marseille Hermit (VIIII)**, renumbered to **18** and
  re-cast as the **Capuchin** with a strong added sense of concealment/treachery
  [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps]
- **Confidence:** medium-high

## 19 — Le Temple Foudroyé (The Temple Struck by Lightning)
- **Depicts:** a temple/tower struck and thrown down by lightning [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — sudden ruin, downfall, a structure broken open.
  Reversed — calamity narrowly escaped, oppression or confinement [@web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Changed from parent:** answers to the **Marseille Tower / La Maison Dieu (XVI)**, renumbered
  to **19** and re-titled the "struck Temple," with the Egyptian-temple framing
  [@web_etteillastrumps; @web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_benebell_etteilla]
- **Confidence:** medium-high

## 20 — La Roue de Fortune (Wheel of Fortune)
- **Depicts:** the turning wheel of the TdM [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — fortune rising, increase, elevation, luck. Reversed —
  a fall, fortune reversed, gain turning to loss [@web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Changed from parent:** answers to the **Marseille Wheel of Fortune (X)**, renumbered to
  **20**; meaning largely preserved [@web_etteillastrumps; @web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_benebell_etteilla]
- **Confidence:** medium-high

## 21 — Le Despote Africain (The African Despot)
- **Depicts:** an enthroned tyrant/sovereign; later editions associate him with Rehoboam, the
  son of Solomon who divided the kingdom [@web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — dissension, discord, great power, a powerful (and
  dangerous) protector. Reversed — arrogance, tyranny [@web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Changed from parent:** answers to the **Marseille Chariot (VII)** — *not* the Emperor — which
  Etteilla reworks into a "despotic sovereign" at **21** (benebell wen titles her reconstruction
  of this key "The Tyrant Embodied in The Chariot Card (Etteilla Tarot Key 21)")
  [@web_benebell_etteilla]. **— Corrects the repo grammar, which maps Card 21 to the Emperor.**
  (The Emperor's authority material is dispersed elsewhere in Etteilla's reworking; there is no
  clean single "Emperor" trump — confidence: medium on the Chariot correspondence, low on any
  Emperor mapping.)
- **Sources:** [@web_benebell_etteilla], [@web_etteillastrumps]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 78 / 0 — La Folie (The Fool / Madness)
- **Depicts:** the wandering fool of the TdM; the only **unnumbered / zero** trump
  [@web_etteilla_org; @web_pdr_etteilla].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — folly, the matter unbound, open to any turn; the wild
  card outside the count. Reversed — recklessness, a question asked in folly
  [@web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Changed from parent:** answers to the **Marseille Fool (unnumbered)**. Etteilla's innovation
  is to assign it the **number 0** and the title **"La Folie"** — reportedly the first deck to do
  both [@web_etteilla_org]. **— Corrects the repo grammar, which labels this the "female
  querent."** La Folie is the Fool; the female significator is Card 8 (Le Repos)
  [@web_pdr_etteilla; @web_etteilla_org].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteilla_org], [@web_pdr_etteilla], [@web_benebell_etteilla]
- **Confidence:** high (identity); medium (keywords)

## The minors (22–77)

Etteilla keeps the four Latin suits of the Marseille pack but assigns each an **element and a
domain**, and reads every **court card as a person** (upright = well-disposed, reversed =
ill-disposed) — the origin of the modern "court card = a person in the querent's life"
convention [@web_aeclectic_etteilla; @web_pdr_etteilla]. The pips carry astrological
correspondences (e.g. the Coins map to the seven planets, the Lot of Fortune, and the two lunar
nodes) absent from the Marseille game deck [@web_pdr_etteilla; @web_benebell_etteilla]. The
**"changed from parent"** for the whole minor sequence is therefore the same move: an undignified
Marseille *game* pip becomes a keyworded, element-coded, upright/reversed *divinatory* card with
printed meanings. Per-card divinatory wordlists are catalogued in benebell wen's reconstruction
and the etteillastrumps project; they are summarised here rather than reproduced verbatim.

### Bâtons / Wands — element **Fire**; domain: enterprise, work, the countryside, growth
- **22 Roi de Bâton** — a person: an enterprising, honourable man of business or the country.
- **23 Reine de Bâton** — a person: a capable, warm, industrious woman.
- **24 Chevalier de Bâton** — a person/event: a young man setting out; departure, enterprise on the move.
- **25 Valet de Bâton** — a person/event: a messenger, news of work or family.
- **26–35 Dix → As de Bâton** — pips of Fire: completion/surfeit (10) down through the suit to a pure onset of enterprise (Ace), each with upright and reversed keywords.
- **Changed from parent:** Marseille Bâtons pips/courts → element-coded, keyworded divinatory cards; courts now signify persons [@web_aeclectic_etteilla; @web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Confidence:** medium (suit/element framing high; individual keyword lists medium)

### Coupes / Cups — element **Water**; domain: love, affection, friendship, the heart
- **36 Roi de Coupe** — a person: a fair, kindly man, often of feeling or of the Church.
- **37 Reine de Coupe** — a person: a loving, fair-natured woman.
- **38 Chevalier de Coupe** — a person/event: an arrival, a lover or friend approaching; a proposal.
- **39 Valet de Coupe** — a person/event: a fair youth bringing affection or good news.
- **40–49 Dix → As de Coupe** — pips of Water, completion (10) to pure onset (Ace), upright/reversed.
- **Changed from parent:** as above, in the domain of the heart [@web_aeclectic_etteilla; @web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Confidence:** medium

### Épées / Swords — element **Air**; domain: conflict, law, arms, sharp trouble
- **50 Roi d'Épée** — a person: a man of law or arms — judge, soldier, official; often an adversary.
- **51 Reine d'Épée** — a person: a sharp, discerning woman, often a widow.
- **52 Chevalier d'Épée** — a person/event: a soldier; the approach of conflict.
- **53 Valet d'Épée** — a person/event: a watcher or spy; vigilance, sharp news.
- **54–63 Dix → As d'Épée** — pips of Air, completion (10) to pure onset (Ace), upright/reversed.
- **Changed from parent:** as above, in the domain of conflict [@web_aeclectic_etteilla; @web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Confidence:** medium

### Deniers / Coins — element **Earth**; domain: money, trade, property, practical gain
- **64 Roi de Denier** — a person: a man of means — merchant or man of property.
- **65 Reine de Denier** — a person: a prosperous, practical woman.
- **66 Chevalier de Denier** — a person/event: money arriving; a dependable man.
- **67 Valet de Denier** — a person/event: a student or clerk; news of money or study.
- **68–77 Dix → As de Denier** — pips of Earth, completion (10) to pure onset (Ace), upright/reversed; the Coins carry the planetary correspondences (Ace=Sun, Two=Mercury, Three=Venus, …) [@web_benebell_etteilla].
- **Changed from parent:** as above, in the domain of money; the planetary mapping is wholly new vs. the Marseille game pip [@web_benebell_etteilla; @web_pdr_etteilla].
- **Confidence:** medium (planetary mapping medium-high per benebell wen)

## Open questions / corrections owed (cards)

- **Grammar errors to fix** (in `tarot/etteilla-i-livre-de-thot/grammar.json`):
  - Card **13** `archetype` should be **the Lovers / "Le Mariage"**, not `arcana:the-hierophant`.
  - Card **21** `archetype` should be **the Chariot** ("Le Despote Africain"), not `arcana:the-emperor`.
  - Card **78 (La Folie)** is the **Fool**, not the **female querent**; the female significator
    is **Card 8 (Le Repos)**. The grammar's Card 1 keyword `etteilla:significator-male` is
    correct; the female-significator keyword belongs on Card 8, not Card 78.
  - Card **15** is correctly "Maladie/Illness" but is the **Bateleur/Magician (TdM I)**, worth
    noting explicitly as a *demotion* of the first Marseille trump.
- The cosmogony cards (1–8) and several mid-trumps have **no clean Marseille parent** by design;
  keep them un-normalised.
- Per-pip divinatory wordlists (cards 22–77) should be transcribed from a primary
  (Etteilla's *Troisième cahier*) before being raised above `medium`.
