# Etteilla III — Grand Jeu de l'Oracle des Dames — card-by-card

**Source of imagery:** a wholesale **re-drawing** of the Etteilla pattern, lithographed from
G. Régamey's c.1865 designs — "completely redrawn," "larger, more ornate," with a 19th-century
Gothic look "only vaguely related to the original" Basan engravings [@web_phantho_egyptian;
@web_tarotgarden_grandetteilla]. **The system is unchanged** from Etteilla I/II: same 78-card
count, same trump order, same printed upright/reversed keywords, same "court = a person"
convention. **Reading the "Changed from parent" line:** the parent is **Etteilla II** (and behind
it Etteilla I); the delta is **production (re-drawn imagery)** and, on a handful of trumps,
**titling** — *not* a new dictionary. Where a card only inherits, the block says so briefly and
the meanings are not re-derived (they live in `research/cards/etteilla-i-livre-de-thot.md`).

> Numbering. Cards 1–78 in Arabic numerals; **La Folie** is the unnumbered/0 trump and sits last
> in the running sheet (No. 78). The **male querent is Card 1 (Le Chaos)**, the **female querent
> is Card 8 (Le Repos)** — both double as cosmogony trumps. The grammar's tag of Card 78 as the
> female significator is an **error** carried from Etteilla I (see Corrections owed).
> [@web_etteillastrumps; @web_steve_grandetteilla]

## The trumps (1–21) + the Fool — the titling delta

The cosmogony (1–8) and virtue (9–12) trumps keep Alliette's titles and meanings; only the
**re-drawn imagery** changes. The titling delta concentrates on **13–21**.

## 1 — Le Chaos (male querent)
- **Depicts:** the primordial void; doubles as the **male querent significator** [@web_steve_grandetteilla].
- **Changed from parent:** **re-drawn only.** Title, meaning and significator function inherited unchanged from Etteilla II/I [@web_steve_grandetteilla; @web_etteillastrumps].
- **Sources:** [@web_steve_grandetteilla], [@web_etteillastrumps]
- **Confidence:** high (inheritance); medium (imagery detail)

## 2 — La Lumière (Light) · 3 — Les Plantes · 4 — Le Ciel · 5 — L'Homme et les Quadrupèdes · 6 — Les Astres · 7 — Les Oiseaux et les Poissons
- **Depicts / Means:** the cosmogony days, as in Etteilla I (see that file for the day-order and keywords) [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Changed from parent:** **re-drawn only** — titles and the seven-days reading are inherited; the Régamey imagery is heavier and more ornate than Basan's [@web_phantho_egyptian].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_steve_grandetteilla], [@web_phantho_egyptian]
- **Confidence:** high (inheritance); medium (imagery)

## 8 — Le Repos (Rest) — female querent
- **Depicts:** the seventh day / Sabbath; doubles as the **female querent significator** [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Changed from parent:** **re-drawn only.** Title and meaning inherited. **— Corrects the repo grammar**, which strips this to "Repos" with no significator keyword and instead tags Card 78 as the female querent; the female significator is **Card 8**, here [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_steve_grandetteilla]
- **Confidence:** high

## 9 — La Justice · 10 — La Tempérance · 11 — La Force · 12 — La Prudence
- **Depicts / Means:** the four cardinal virtues, as in Etteilla I (Prudence at 12 is Alliette's substitute for the Hanged Man) [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Changed from parent:** **re-drawn only** — titles and meanings inherited intact [@web_etteillastrumps; @web_steve_grandetteilla].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_steve_grandetteilla]
- **Confidence:** high (inheritance); medium (imagery)

## 13 — Le Grand Prêtre (The High Priest)
- **Depicts:** a high priest officiating — the III/Grimaud-line **title** for the image at 13 [@web_steve_grandetteilla; @web_etteillastrumps].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — counsel, sanction, marriage, spiritual authority, a trusted guide; reversed — hollow dogma, a bad counsellor, hypocrisy [@web_steve_grandetteilla; @web_etteillastrumps].
- **Changed from parent:** **a re-titling, not a new card.** By *identity* this is Alliette's **Le Mariage / the Lovers (TdM L'Amoureux)**; the "High Priest" label is the posthumous Etteilla II/III emphasis on the officiant in the image, settled here as the standard title. The Lovers/marriage meaning persists underneath [@web_etteillastrumps; @web_benebell_etteilla]. **— The repo grammar maps Card 13 `arcana:the-hierophant` ("exact"); the *title* is right for III but the *identity* is the Lovers — see Corrections owed.**
- **Sources:** [@web_steve_grandetteilla], [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_benebell_etteilla]
- **Confidence:** medium-high (title attested for III; Lovers identity well attested)

## 14 — Le Diable (The Devil)
- **Depicts:** the Devil figure, re-drawn [@web_steve_grandetteilla].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — raw force, vehemence, power over circumstance (Etteilla reads the Devil as *energy*, his *Force Majeure*); reversed — fury, ravage, bondage to one's passions (*Force Mineure*) [@web_steve_grandetteilla; @web_etteillastrumps].
- **Changed from parent:** **title foregrounded.** Where Alliette's reading is the abstract *Force Majeure / Force Mineure*, the III/Grimaud line settles the title as **"Le Diable,"** putting the Devil image first while keeping the "force/necessity" meaning [@web_steve_grandetteilla; @web_etteillastrumps].
- **Sources:** [@web_steve_grandetteilla], [@web_etteillastrumps]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 15 — Le Magicien ou le Bateleur (Magician/Juggler) — Illness
- **Depicts:** the juggler/mountebank at his table, re-drawn [@web_picryl_etteilla3].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — **Illness / Maladie**, a matter of the body (a Grimaud-line booklet has the card counsel "see a doctor"); reversed — a charlatan, a false remedy, the malady worsening [@web_steve_grandetteilla; @web_etteillastrumps].
- **Changed from parent:** **re-drawn; dual title retained.** The III/Pussey/Grimaud cards keep the "Le Magicien ou le Bateleur" label while the *meaning* stays Alliette's demotion of the first Marseille trump to **Illness** [@web_picryl_etteilla3; @web_steve_grandetteilla].
- **Sources:** [@web_picryl_etteilla3], [@web_steve_grandetteilla], [@web_etteillastrumps]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 16 — Le Jugement Dernier (Last Judgement) · 17 — La Mort (Death) · 20 — La Roue de Fortune
- **Depicts / Means:** Judgement (16), Death (17, the famous renumbering off XIII), Wheel of Fortune (20) — titles and meanings as in Etteilla I [@web_etteillastrumps; @web_steve_grandetteilla].
- **Changed from parent:** **re-drawn only** — inherited intact [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_steve_grandetteilla]
- **Confidence:** high (inheritance); medium (imagery)

## 18 — Le Capucin (The Capuchin / Hermit / Traitor)
- **Depicts:** a hooded Capuchin monk with staff, re-drawn [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — the solitary, concealment, a thing hidden, the **traitor**, prudent withdrawal; reversed — a secret enemy unmasked, treachery exposed [@web_steve_grandetteilla; @web_etteillastrumps].
- **Changed from parent:** **re-drawn; title settled.** "Le Capucin" with the concealment/traitor sense is carried through from Alliette's reframing of the Hermit; III fixes it as the standard title [@web_etteillastrumps; @web_steve_grandetteilla].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_steve_grandetteilla]
- **Confidence:** medium-high

## 19 — Le Temple Foudroyé (The Struck Temple)
- **Depicts:** a temple/tower struck and thrown down by lightning, re-drawn [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — sudden ruin, downfall, a structure broken open; reversed — calamity narrowly escaped, but oppression/confinement [@web_steve_grandetteilla; @web_etteillastrumps].
- **Changed from parent:** **re-drawn only** — the "struck Temple" title (Alliette's Egyptian re-framing of the TdM Tower) is inherited [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_steve_grandetteilla]
- **Confidence:** medium-high

## 21 — Le Despote Africain (The African Despot) — "Dissension"
- **Depicts:** an enthroned tyrant/sovereign, re-drawn; the Grimaud-line booklet identifies him with **Rehoboam**, Solomon's son who divided the kingdom [@web_houndofhecate_despot; @web_benebell_chariot].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** **upright — "Dissension / Discord,"** great power, a powerful (and dangerous) protector; reversed — arrogance, tyranny, discord stirred from above [@web_houndofhecate_despot; @web_etteillastrumps].
- **Changed from parent:** the brief's flagged **re-titling to "Dissension."** By identity this reworks the **Chariot (TdM VII)** — moved to the end of the trumps and "rechristened The African Despot" — and the III/Grimaud line fixes its **upright keyword as Dissension/Discord**, with the Rehoboam gloss [@web_houndofhecate_despot; @web_benebell_chariot; @web_etteillastrumps]. **— The repo grammar maps Card 21 `arcana:the-emperor` ("loose"); the correspondence is the Chariot, not the Emperor — see Corrections owed.**
- **Sources:** [@web_houndofhecate_despot], [@web_benebell_chariot], [@web_etteillastrumps]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 78 / 0 — La Folie (The Fool)
- **Depicts:** the wandering fool; the only unnumbered/0 trump, re-drawn [@web_steve_grandetteilla].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** upright — folly, the matter unbound, the wild card outside the count; reversed — recklessness, a question asked in folly [@web_etteillastrumps].
- **Changed from parent:** **re-drawn only.** **— Corrects the repo grammar**, which labels this the "female querent" with `etteilla:significator-female`. La Folie is the **Fool**; the female significator is **Card 8 (Le Repos)** [@web_etteillastrumps; @web_steve_grandetteilla].
- **Sources:** [@web_etteillastrumps], [@web_steve_grandetteilla]
- **Confidence:** high (identity); medium (keywords)

## The minors (22–77) — re-drawn, system unchanged

Etteilla III keeps the four Latin suits with their elements and domains and reads every **court
card as a person** (upright well-disposed, reversed ill-disposed) — exactly as Etteilla I/II
[@web_steve_grandetteilla; @web_aeclectic_etteilla]. The pips keep their element-coded
upright/reversed keywords and astrological correspondences.

- **22–35 Bâtons / Wands** — Fire; enterprise, work, the countryside, growth.
- **36–49 Coupes / Cups** — Water; love, affection, friendship, the heart.
- **50–63 Épées / Swords** — Air; conflict, law, arms, sharp trouble.
- **64–77 Deniers / Coins** — Earth; money, trade, property, practical gain (the Coins carry the planetary mapping).

- **Changed from parent (whole minor sequence):** **re-drawn only.** Suit/element framing,
  court-as-person rule, and the per-card divinatory wordlists are **inherited unchanged** from the
  Alliette dictionary (carried through Etteilla II); the III contribution is the **ornate Régamey
  re-drawing**, not new meanings. Per-card wordlists are not re-derived here — see
  `research/cards/etteilla-i-livre-de-thot.md` [@web_steve_grandetteilla; @web_etteillastrumps].
- **Confidence:** medium (inheritance high; individual keyword lists medium; imagery detail medium)

## Open questions / corrections owed (cards)

- **Grammar errors to fix** (in `tarot/etteilla-iii-oracle-des-dames/grammar.json`):
  - **Card 78 (`La Folie`)** is the **Fool / 0**, not the female querent. Move the
    `etteilla:significator-female` keyword (and the "female querent" framing) to **Card 8
    (`Repos`)**, whose name should read **"Le Repos (Rest)"** and which should carry the
    significator keyword. Same error class as Etteilla I.
  - **Card 13 (`Le Grand Prêtre`)** — `archetype: arcana:the-hierophant`, `mapping_confidence:
    exact`. The III-era **title** "Le Grand Prêtre" is authentic, but the card's **identity** is
    the **Lovers / Le Mariage**; "exact" to the Hierophant overstates a re-label. Flag as
    needs-review (note the title-vs-identity split rather than silently re-mapping).
  - **Card 21 (`Le Despote Africain`)** — `archetype: arcana:the-emperor`, `mapping_confidence:
    loose`. The correspondence is the **Chariot**, not the Emperor. Upright keyword **"Dissension/
    Discord"** and the **Rehoboam** gloss should be recorded.
- The cosmogony (1–8) and several mid-trumps have **no clean Marseille parent** by design — keep
  un-normalised, as in Etteilla I.
- Imagery descriptions are from low-res scan summaries (steve-p.org, PICRYL, Wikimedia Commons
  Etteilla III category); a primary look at a dated Régamey/Haugard-Meuge or Grimaud set would
  raise the per-card imagery notes above `medium`.
