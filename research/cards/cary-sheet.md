# The Cary Sheet — Trump Images on the Sheet

**Deck:** `cary-sheet` · **Surviving:** a partial uncut woodcut sheet (no complete pack) ·
**Source of imagery:** Yale Beinecke / Cary Collection, c. 1500 Milan [@beinecke_cary;
@dummett1980]. The trumps are **untitled and unnumbered** on the sheet, and carry **no native
divinatory meaning** (a printed game sheet) [@web_tm_carysheet; @dummett1980]. The cards below are
the **visible trump repertoire** reported in secondary summaries — *not* a settled, complete list
(confidence: low — inventories disagree; primary verification owed). "Means" and "Changed from
parent" lines omitted.

## What is most clearly visible (the diagnostic Marseille trumps)

These three are the load-bearing identifications — the images that prove the Marseille pattern was
already fixed by c. 1500 [@web_th_carysheet_15c; @web_tm_carysheet]:

### The Tower
- **Depicts:** The **lightning-struck Tower** — the diagnostic Marseille image; significant because
  the Tower (and Devil) are absent from the surviving hand-painted Milanese sets and first appear
  here [@web_th_carysheet_15c].
- **Confidence:** medium

### The Star
- **Depicts:** The **Star woman pouring water from two jars** — the Marseille Star already in place
  [@web_th_carysheet_15c].
- **Confidence:** medium

### The Moon
- **Depicts:** The **Moon with two dogs/towers and a crayfish** below — the Marseille Moon
  composition [@web_th_carysheet_15c].
- **Confidence:** medium
