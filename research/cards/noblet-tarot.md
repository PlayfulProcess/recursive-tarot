# Jean Noblet Tarot — card-by-card

**Source of imagery:** the single surviving BnF copy, Gallica `ark:/12148/btv1b105109641`,
as transmitted through the Flornoy (2007) and Peterson (2016) restorations
[@web_gallica_noblet; @web_th_noblet_vs; @web_peterson_noblet].

**No divinatory meanings.** Noblet is a 17th-century **game** deck; it carries no native
upright/reversed meanings. Those are a later occult overlay (Court de Gébelin 1781 onward),
documented elsewhere [@dummett1980; @decker1996]. The blocks below describe **iconography**
only. Because Noblet is a **root** deck, the "Changed from parent" line is replaced by
**"Type II later altered"** — i.e. what the canonical Conver-line decks changed *from* this
Type-I state [@depaulis1984; @web_th_typeI_typeII].

The 56 minors are standard Italian-suited Marseille pips (Deniers, Coupes, Épées, Bâtons;
Ace–Ten + Roy/Dame/Cavalier/Valet), emblematic not scenic; the surviving set **lacks the Six
through Ten of Swords** [@web_wopc_noblet; @web_aeclectic_noblet]. Only the **22 trumps** are
catalogued individually below, in C-order.

---

## — — The Fool (LE FOV / Le Fou)

- **Depicts:** an unnumbered wanderer striding with a staff and bundle, an animal at his legs.
  Two famous Noblet peculiarities: the animal reads more like a **cat** than the usual dog, and
  the figure is **sexually explicit** — his rump is bared and the cat claws at exposed
  genitals, among the franker images in tarot history [@web_aeclectic_noblet; @web_th_noblet_vs].
- **Means:** none native (game deck).
- **Type II later altered:** the explicit nudity is **sanitised** in later decks; the title
  also normalises — Noblet's **"Le Fou"** is reportedly unique against the usual *Le Mat* /
  *Le Fol* (confidence: medium) [@web_th_typeI_typeII; @web_aeclectic_noblet].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_noblet], [@web_th_noblet_vs], [@web_th_typeI_typeII]
- **Confidence:** medium

## I — The Mountebank (LE BATELEVR)

- **Depicts:** a juggler/conjuror at a table set with the tools of his act — **cups, dice, and
  knives** — performing the cup-and-ball and handkerchief tricks. On the Noblet block his
  **wand is truncated and three middle fingers are missing** — an apparent **carver's omission**
  printed uncorrected (no ghost of the missing elements survives), often noted alongside the
  card's phallic gesture [@web_aeclectic_noblet; @web_th_noblet_vs].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** later blocks render the hand and wand intact; the table furniture
  stabilises into the canonical Bateleur kit [@web_th_typeI_typeII; @dummett1980].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_noblet], [@web_th_noblet_vs]
- **Confidence:** medium

## II — The Popess (LA PAPESSE)

- **Depicts:** a seated, tiara'd/veiled female figure with a book — the standard Marseille
  Popess [@dummett1980; @web_aeclectic_noblet].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** broadly stable across both types; the Swiss/Besançon offshoot
  later replaces her with **Juno**, but that is a separate pattern, not Type II [@dummett1980;
  @web_besancon_wp].
- **Sources:** [@dummett1980], [@web_aeclectic_noblet]
- **Confidence:** medium

## III — The Empress (LIMPERATRICE)

- **Depicts:** a crowned, enthroned woman with sceptre and shield/eagle — standard Marseille
  Empress [@dummett1980; @web_aeclectic_noblet].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** no signature Type-I/II divergence reported [@web_th_typeI_typeII].
- **Sources:** [@dummett1980], [@web_aeclectic_noblet]
- **Confidence:** medium

## IIII — The Emperor (LEMPEREVR)

- **Depicts:** a crowned emperor in profile with sceptre and shield-eagle, seated/leaning.
  Noblet's Emperor is noted as an **exception** to the Type-I set: it **lacks the floating "4"**
  in front of the face that some Type-I decks show [@web_th_typeI_typeII].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** Type-I/II treatments of the Emperor are close; the "4" detail is
  the diagnostic variable, and Noblet sits on the no-"4" side [@web_th_typeI_typeII].
- **Sources:** [@web_th_typeI_typeII], [@dummett1980]
- **Confidence:** medium

## V — The Pope (LE PAPE)

- **Depicts:** an enthroned pope blessing two tonsured clerics. **Type-I mark:** Noblet's Pope
  holds a **bishop's crook (crozier)** — as on the Cary Sheet — rather than the later triple
  cross/papal staff [@web_th_typeI_typeII; @web_th_celebrating_typeI].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** Type II regularises the staff toward the triple cross
  [@web_th_typeI_typeII].
- **Sources:** [@web_th_typeI_typeII], [@web_th_celebrating_typeI]
- **Confidence:** medium

## VI — The Lover(s) (LAMOREVX)

- **Depicts:** a young man between two women beneath a winged Cupid drawing his bow — the
  Marseille "choice" composition [@dummett1980; @web_aeclectic_noblet].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** no signature Type-I/II divergence reported for Noblet
  [@web_th_typeI_typeII].
- **Sources:** [@dummett1980], [@web_aeclectic_noblet]
- **Confidence:** medium

## VII — The Chariot (LE CHARIOT)

- **Depicts:** a crowned charioteer under a canopy drawn by two horses. The **escutcheon on
  the chariot carries Noblet's monogram "I. N."** — the maker signing his own trump, and an
  exception within the otherwise impersonal Type-I details [@web_th_typeI_typeII;
  @web_bnf_essentiels_noblet].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** later makers substitute their **own** shield initials (e.g.
  Conver's "V T"), keeping the device as a signature slot [@web_th_typeI_typeII;
  @web_vs_conver_heritage].
- **Sources:** [@web_th_typeI_typeII], [@web_bnf_essentiels_noblet]
- **Confidence:** medium

## VIII — Justice (LA IVSTICE)

- **Depicts:** an enthroned, crowned Justice with sword and scales — **numbered VIII**, the
  pre-Golden-Dawn placement the Marseille keeps [@dummett1980; @web_th_typeI_typeII].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** the **Justice VIII / Force XI** order is shared by Type I and II;
  it is the later Rider-Waite tradition that swaps them [@dummett1980].
- **Sources:** [@dummett1980], [@web_th_typeI_typeII]
- **Confidence:** medium

## VIIII — The Hermit (LERMITE)

- **Depicts:** a hooded old man with a staff, lifting a lantern — standard Marseille Hermit
  [@dummett1980; @web_aeclectic_noblet].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** no signature divergence reported [@web_th_typeI_typeII].
- **Sources:** [@dummett1980], [@web_aeclectic_noblet]
- **Confidence:** medium

## X — The Wheel of Fortune (LA ROVE DE FORTVNE)

- **Depicts:** a wheel with rising and falling animal-figures and a crank — standard Marseille
  composition [@dummett1980; @web_aeclectic_noblet].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** no signature Type-I/II divergence reported for Noblet
  [@web_th_typeI_typeII].
- **Sources:** [@dummett1980], [@web_aeclectic_noblet]
- **Confidence:** medium

## XI — Strength / Force (LA FORCE)

- **Depicts:** a woman prising open (or closing) a lion's jaws — **numbered XI** in the
  pre-Golden-Dawn order [@dummett1980; @web_th_typeI_typeII].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** position shared by both Marseille types; later swapped to VIII
  only in the Rider-Waite line [@dummett1980].
- **Sources:** [@dummett1980], [@web_th_typeI_typeII]
- **Confidence:** medium

## XII — The Hanged Man (LE PENDV)

- **Depicts:** a man suspended by one ankle from a beam between two trees, hands behind back.
  Noblet is among the decks giving figures **legs of contrasting colours** [@web_secretsdutarot_noblet;
  @web_aeclectic_noblet].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** the inverted posture is stable; the colour conventions normalise
  [@dummett1980].
- **Sources:** [@web_secretsdutarot_noblet], [@web_aeclectic_noblet]
- **Confidence:** medium

## XIII — Death (LAMORT)

- **Depicts:** a skeleton mowing with a scythe over a field of severed heads, hands and feet.
  **Noblet titles it "LAMORT"** — distinctive, since most Marseille decks leave the thirteenth
  trump **unnamed**; several sources call Noblet the only TdM to name Death (confidence: medium
  on "only") [@web_wopc_noblet; @web_th_noblet_vs; @web_aeclectic_noblet].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** Type II keeps the image but **drops the name**, leaving XIII
  un-titled [@web_wopc_noblet; @dummett1980].
- **Sources:** [@web_wopc_noblet], [@web_th_noblet_vs], [@web_aeclectic_noblet]
- **Confidence:** medium

## XIIII — Temperance (TEMPERANCE)

- **Depicts:** a winged figure pouring liquid between two vessels — standard Marseille
  Temperance. Noblet is described as having **renewed** the celestial/virtue group's imagery
  (Temperance, Devil, Tower, Star, Moon, Sun) toward the form that became "the Marseille"
  (confidence: medium — claim of authorship of the new icons) [@web_secretsdutarot_noblet].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** broadly stable [@web_th_typeI_typeII].
- **Sources:** [@web_secretsdutarot_noblet], [@dummett1980]
- **Confidence:** medium

## XV — The Devil (LE DIABLE)

- **Depicts:** a horned, winged, clawed Devil on a pedestal with two bound, horned imps.
  **Type-I mark:** the Devil's **belly carries a second human face**; Noblet's wings reportedly
  show **drops of blood** — read by restorers as a wounded/suffering being [@web_th_typeI_typeII;
  @web_th_noblet_vs; @web_aeclectic_noblet].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** Type II gives the Devil a **smooth belly** (no face) and a more
  decorative, less visceral treatment [@web_th_typeI_typeII; @web_druidcraft_typeI].
- **Sources:** [@web_th_typeI_typeII], [@web_th_noblet_vs], [@web_druidcraft_typeI]
- **Confidence:** medium

## XVI — The Tower / House of God (LA MAISON DIEV)

- **Depicts:** a tower struck at its crown, figures falling, with falling particles/flames —
  standard Marseille "Maison Dieu." Part of the group Noblet is credited with renewing
  [@web_secretsdutarot_noblet; @dummett1980].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** no signature Type-I/II divergence singled out for Noblet beyond
  the renewed imagery [@web_th_typeI_typeII].
- **Sources:** [@web_secretsdutarot_noblet], [@dummett1980]
- **Confidence:** medium

## XVII — The Star (LESTOILLE)

- **Depicts:** a kneeling nude woman pouring two ewers by a stream under a large star and
  smaller stars — the Marseille Star [@dummett1980; @web_aeclectic_noblet].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** broadly stable across types for Noblet; counted among the renewed
  celestial group [@web_secretsdutarot_noblet; @web_th_typeI_typeII].
- **Sources:** [@dummett1980], [@web_aeclectic_noblet]
- **Confidence:** medium

## XVIII — The Moon (LA LVNE)

- **Depicts:** a crayfish in a pool, two dogs/wolves baying, two towers, and a large moon.
  **Type-I mark:** Noblet's **Moon is rendered full-face** (a frontal face in the disc); Type II
  shows it in **profile** [@web_th_typeI_typeII; @web_druidcraft_typeI; @web_peterson_noblet].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** the disc turns to **profile** in the Conver line
  [@web_th_typeI_typeII].
- **Sources:** [@web_th_typeI_typeII], [@web_druidcraft_typeI]
- **Confidence:** medium

## XVIIII — The Sun (LE SOLEIL)

- **Depicts:** a great rayed sun over two human figures by a wall. **Type-I mark:** Noblet's
  pair are an **adult man and woman** in scant loincloths; Type II reduces them to **two
  children**. The figures also show Noblet's contrasting-leg colouring [@web_th_typeI_typeII;
  @web_druidcraft_typeI; @web_secretsdutarot_noblet].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** the adult couple becomes **two infants** of indeterminate sex
  [@web_th_typeI_typeII; @web_druidcraft_typeI].
- **Sources:** [@web_th_typeI_typeII], [@web_druidcraft_typeI]
- **Confidence:** medium

## XX — Judgement (LE IVGEMENT)

- **Depicts:** an angel with a trumpet above figures rising from a tomb — standard Marseille
  Judgement [@dummett1980; @web_aeclectic_noblet].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** no signature Type-I/II divergence reported for Noblet
  [@web_th_typeI_typeII].
- **Sources:** [@dummett1980], [@web_aeclectic_noblet]
- **Confidence:** medium

## XXI — The World (LE MONDE)

- **Depicts:** a central figure within a vesica/wreath, flanked by the four living creatures
  (eagle, angel, ox, lion). **Type-I mark:** Noblet's figure wears a **loincloth and a cape**;
  Type II drapes her only in a **scarf** with the **left leg bent** [@web_th_typeI_typeII;
  @web_druidcraft_typeI; @web_world_wp]. (In Peterson's restoration the head/arms shape was
  lightly retouched — a restorer's intervention, not an original feature) [@web_th_noblet_vs].
- **Means:** none native.
- **Type II later altered:** the drapery is reduced to a scarf and the stance changes (bent
  leg) in the Conver line [@web_th_typeI_typeII; @web_druidcraft_typeI].
- **Sources:** [@web_th_typeI_typeII], [@web_druidcraft_typeI], [@web_world_wp]
- **Confidence:** medium
