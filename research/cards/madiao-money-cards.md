# Ma Diao — the Four Money Suits

A money-suited pack has no "trumps" and no per-card meanings (it is a game, not an oracle), so
the meaningful unit to document card-by-card is the **suit**. The four suits are ascending
denominations of currency; this is the structural signature of the East Asian tradition and the
feature that makes the pack ancestral to the later Latin suit-system [@web_madiao_wiki]. The
"Changed from parent" line is omitted throughout — Ma Diao is a root with no `derives_from`; the
relevant diff is the *downstream* mapping onto the Mamluk/European suits, given per suit below.

Source of imagery: public-domain Skokloster Castle (Sweden) holdings via Wikimedia Commons
[@web_madiao_themahjong]; the individual survivors are not separately identified by suit/value.

## 1 — Cash / Coins (文 · wén)

- **Depicts:** Single copper coins; the lowest money suit. In the Madiao pack the cash suit runs
  1–9 with a half-cash and a zero/empty card (11 cards) [@web_madiao_wiki].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** None — a game suit has no native cartomantic meaning.
- **Downstream mapping:** The coin is the suit that survived the long westward diffusion most
  recognisably, becoming the **coins/dinari** suit of the Mamluk pack and the **Coins/Pentacles**
  suit of the Latin and tarot packs [@web00c_islamic_chinese], [@web_suit_wiki].
- **Sources:** [@web_madiao_wiki], [@web00c_islamic_chinese], [@web_suit_wiki]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 2 — Strings of Cash (索 · suǒ)

- **Depicts:** Ropes or strings on which coins were threaded — each string a bundle of (about) a
  hundred cash. A denomination, not an abstract pip (9 cards, 1–9) [@web_madiao_wiki].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** None.
- **Downstream mapping:** No clean single descendant; the "strings" idea has been compared
  speculatively to the Islamic **polo-stick / baton** suit, but the visual mapping beyond *coins*
  is inference, not documentation [@web_suit_wiki]. (confidence: low)
- **Sources:** [@web_madiao_wiki], [@web_suit_wiki]
- **Confidence:** low

## 3 — Myriads of Strings (萬 · wàn)

- **Depicts:** Ten-thousand units. In many money-card sets this suit is illustrated with the
  **108 bandit-heroes of the novel *Water Margin* (水滸傳)** — the famous "Water Margin cards"
  (水滸牌), still made in folk workshops [@web_madiao_themahjong] (9 cards, 1–9)
  [@web_madiao_wiki].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** None — though the figural Water Margin imagery is iconographic,
  not divinatory.
- **Downstream mapping:** No clean descendant in the Latin line; cited here for its figural
  honour-card character, a feature later packs developed independently in their court cards.
- **Sources:** [@web_madiao_wiki], [@web_madiao_themahjong]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 4 — Tens of Myriads (十萬 · shí wàn)

- **Depicts:** The highest suit, carrying the honour cards; in the Madiao pack it runs from
  20-myriad up through hundred-, thousand-, and myriad-myriad (11 cards) [@web_madiao_wiki].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** None.
- **Downstream mapping:** No clean Latin descendant; documented here to complete the four-suit
  ascending-money structure that is the pack's ancestral signature [@web00c_islamic_chinese].
- **Sources:** [@web_madiao_wiki], [@web00c_islamic_chinese]
- **Confidence:** medium
