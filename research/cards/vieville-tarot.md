# Jacques Viéville Tarot — card-by-card

**Source of imagery:** the single surviving copy at the Bibliothèque nationale de France,
public-domain on Gallica (ark:/12148/btv1b10510963k) [@bnf_gallica; @web_letarot_vieville].

**How to read the "Changed from parent" line.** The Viéville is **not a descendant of the
Marseille** — it is an **independent, ancestral, non-Marseille pattern** (the head of the
Rouen-Brussels / Belgian line, traced by Dummett to Bologna via Piedmont/Savoy) [@dummett1980;
@web_th_rouen_bruxelles]. So the line below is a **synchronic diff against the Marseille
(Conver) trump that occupies the comparable role**, flagging each divergence — *not* a claim
that Viéville copied or altered Marseille. Where the two patterns agree, that is noted too.
A **game** deck: no native upright/reversed meanings.

---

## 1 — Le Bateleur (I · Le Bateleur)
- **Depicts:** The mountebank/conjuror at his table — the deck opens, as the Parisian pattern does, with the Bateleur [@web_pollett_vieville].
- **Means (upright / reversed):** None native — a game card [@dummett1980].
- **Changed from parent (vs Marseille):** Substantially the same subject and position as Marseille's I; the low trumps are the most shared territory between the two patterns [@web_th_french_connection].
- **Sources:** [@web_pollett_vieville], [@dummett1980]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 2 — La Papesse (II · La Papesse)
- **Depicts:** A seated figure with the papal tiara; described in the grammar as winged — the Viéville's idiosyncratic Popess [@web_pollett_vieville].
- **Means:** None native — game card.
- **Changed from parent:** Same trump *role* as Marseille's Papesse, but the figure's treatment is idiosyncratic; in the later Belgian descendants this slot is replaced entirely (by the "Spanish Captain" in Vandenborre), so the Papesse here marks Viéville as *earlier/more conservative* than the mature Belgian pattern [@web_th_spanish_captain; @web_th_french_connection].
- **Sources:** [@web_pollett_vieville], [@web_th_spanish_captain]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 3 — L'Impératrice (III · L'Impératrice)
- **Depicts:** Enthroned with an eagle shield and a cross-staff — the Empress.
- **Means:** None native — game card.
- **Changed from parent:** Broadly concordant with Marseille's Empress.
- **Sources:** [@web_pollett_vieville]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 4 — L'Empereur (IIII · L'Empereur)
- **Depicts:** The crowned Emperor.
- **Means:** None native — game card.
- **Changed from parent:** Broadly concordant with Marseille's Emperor (replaced by "Bacus" in the later Belgian pattern, but not here) [@web_th_bodet].
- **Sources:** [@web_pollett_vieville], [@web_th_bodet]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 5 — Le Pape (V · Le Pape)
- **Depicts:** The Pope / Hierophant.
- **Means:** None native — game card.
- **Changed from parent:** Same role as Marseille's Pape; like the Papesse, this papal card survives in Viéville where the mature Belgian decks later swap it out [@web_th_french_connection; @web_th_bodet].
- **Sources:** [@web_pollett_vieville]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 6 — L'Amoureux (VI · L'Amoureux)
- **Depicts:** The Lovers beneath a radiant cupid drawing his bow.
- **Means:** None native — game card.
- **Changed from parent:** Concordant in subject with Marseille's L'Amoureux.
- **Sources:** [@web_pollett_vieville]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 7 — La Justice (VII · La Justice)  ← DIVERGENCE
- **Depicts:** Justice with sword and scales, **numbered VII** [@web_aeclectic_vieville].
- **Means:** None native — game card.
- **Changed from parent:** **Order divergence.** Marseille places Justice at **VIII**; Viéville puts it at **VII**. The four-card virtue/Chariot/Hermit reshuffle (Justice VII, Chariot VIII, Force VIIII, Hermite XI) is a fingerprint of the non-Marseille, Bologna-derived lineage [@dummett1980; @web_th_french_connection; @web_aeclectic_vieville].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_vieville], [@dummett1980]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 8 — Le Chariot (VIII · Le Chariot)  ← DIVERGENCE
- **Depicts:** The Chariot under its canopy, **numbered VIII**; pulled by **sphinx-like / human-faced beings** rather than horses [@web_aeclectic_vieville].
- **Means:** None native — game card.
- **Changed from parent:** **Order divergence** — VIII here is the slot Marseille gives to Justice. Plus an **iconographic divergence**: the human-faced/sphinx draught animals instead of Marseille's horses [@web_aeclectic_vieville].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_vieville]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 9 — La Force (VIIII · La Force)  ← DIVERGENCE
- **Depicts:** Strength — the figure mastering a lion — at **VIIII** [@web_aeclectic_vieville].
- **Means:** None native — game card.
- **Changed from parent:** **Order divergence.** Marseille places Strength at **XI**; Viéville at **VIIII**. (The much later Golden Dawn/RWS swap is a *different*, third re-ordering; Viéville's is the older Belgian-line arrangement, unrelated to it) [@dummett1980; @web_aeclectic_vieville].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_vieville], [@dummett1980]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 11 — L'Hermite (XI · L'Hermite)  ← DIVERGENCE
- **Depicts:** The cloaked old man with his lantern, at **XI** [@web_aeclectic_vieville].
- **Means:** None native — game card.
- **Changed from parent:** **Order divergence.** Marseille places the Hermit at **VIIII** and Strength at XI; Viéville reverses this (Force VIIII, Hermite XI), completing the four-card virtue reshuffle [@web_aeclectic_vieville; @dummett1980].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_vieville], [@dummett1980]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 12 — Le Pendu (XII · Le Pendu)  ← DIVERGENCE
- **Depicts:** The Hanged Man — but shown **upright** rather than suspended head-down [@web_aeclectic_vieville].
- **Means:** None native — game card.
- **Changed from parent:** **Posture divergence.** Marseille's Pendu hangs inverted by one ankle; the Viéville figure is **upright** — one of the deck's signature departures [@web_aeclectic_vieville].
- **Sources:** [@web_aeclectic_vieville]
- **Confidence:** medium — the upright reading is reported across review/forum sources; a primary-scan check is owed.

- **Sources:** [@web_pollett_vieville], [@dummett1980]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 16 — La Foudre / La Maison-Dieu (XVI)  ← MAJOR DIVERGENCE
- **Depicts:** **A tree under a shower of fire/hail, with a shepherd-like figure gazing up at the sky** — the card the Belgian tradition names **La Foudre / La Fouldre** ("the Lightning") [@web_th_rouen_bruxelles; @web_th_french_connection; @web_belgian_wp].
- **Means:** None native — game card.
- **Changed from parent:** **The signature divergence.** Marseille's XVI is *La Maison-Dieu*, a **tower struck by celestial fire**, figures tumbling. Viéville substitutes a **tree** and a **shepherd** under a rain of fire — a tree, not a tower [@web_th_rouen_bruxelles; @web_belgian_wp]. (The grammar's card text still gives the Marseille "tower struck and falling" reading — flagged for correction.)
- **Sources:** [@web_th_rouen_bruxelles], [@web_belgian_wp], [@web_th_french_connection]
- **Confidence:** medium-high (this divergence is the most consistently corroborated point)

## 17 — L'Étoile (XVII · L'Étoile)  ← DIVERGENCE
- **Depicts:** An **astronomer / astrologer holding compasses (calipers)** beneath the star(s) [@web_tarotforum_vieville_sms; @web_th_celestial].
- **Means:** None native — game card.
- **Changed from parent:** **Iconographic divergence.** Marseille's Star is a **kneeling woman pouring water** from two ewers under a great star. Viéville shows instead a **standing astronomer measuring with compasses** — a learned, not a pastoral/nude, figure [@web_tarotforum_vieville_sms; @web_th_celestial].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarotforum_vieville_sms], [@web_th_celestial]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 18 — La Lune (XVIII · La Lune)  ← DIVERGENCE
- **Depicts:** A **woman under a tree holding a spindle**, beneath the moon [@web_tarotforum_vieville_sms; @web_th_celestial].
- **Means:** None native — game card.
- **Changed from parent:** **Iconographic divergence.** Marseille's Moon shows two towers, two dogs/wolves baying, and a crayfish in a pool. Viéville shows a **spinning woman** — an image Tarot Heritage traces to a **Bolognese Sun card**, transferred onto the Viéville *Moon* (concrete support for the Bologna ancestry Dummett posits) [@web_th_celestial; @dummett1980].
- **Sources:** [@web_th_celestial], [@web_tarotforum_vieville_sms]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 19 — Le Soleil (XVIIII · Le Soleil)  ← DIVERGENCE

- **Means:** None native — game card.
- **Changed from parent:** **Iconographic divergence.** Marseille's Sun shows **two children** (twins) before a wall under a rayed sun. Viéville shows a **single horseman** — a different scene entirely [@web_tarotforum_vieville_sms; @web_th_french_connection].
- **Sources:** [@web_tarotforum_vieville_sms], [@web_th_french_connection]
- **Confidence:** medium

## 21 — Le Monde (XXI · Le Monde)  ← DIVERGENCE
- **Depicts:** The World.
- **Means:** None native — game card.
- **Changed from parent:** **Iconographic divergence.** Marseille's World is a **dancing figure in a mandorla/wreath, ringed by the four living creatures** (man, lion, ox, eagle). ## The 56 suit cards (Épées, Deniers, Bâtons, Coupes)

Four Italian suits — **Épées (Swords), Deniers (Coins), Bâtons (Batons), Coupes (Cups)** —
each **Roi, Reine, Cavalier, Valet** and ten **non-scenic / emblematic pips**, woodcut in the
common late-medieval style; the suit cards are essentially conventional and carry **no
divergence of note** from the Marseille minors beyond cutting style and the occasional mirror-
reversed court figure [@web_pollett_vieville; @web_th_french_connection]. **Pip ranks in the
grammar are assigned by BnF folio position and may warrant correction** [@web_pollett_vieville].
As trumps, none carries native divinatory meaning — this is a **game** pack [@dummett1980].

- **Changed from parent (suits, collectively):** No substantive iconographic divergence from
  the Marseille minors; the Viéville's distinctiveness lives entirely in its **trumps**
  [@web_th_french_connection].
- **Sources:** [@web_pollett_vieville], [@web_th_french_connection], [@dummett1980]
- **Confidence:** medium
