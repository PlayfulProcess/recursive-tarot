# "Charles VI" Tarot (Gringonneur)

## At a glance

Seventeen surviving hand-painted trumps held at the Bibliothèque nationale de France,
historically — and **wrongly** — said to be French cards painted in 1392 for King
Charles VI. They are in fact **Italian**, late-15th-century, and a key witness to the
**B-order** (Ferrarese) trump sequence. A textbook case of a name preserving an
exploded myth.

## Origin & dating

The legend comes from a real account-book entry: a 1392 payment to **Jacquemin
Gringonneur** for three gilded card packs for Charles VI of France. Nineteenth-century
writers attached that entry to these cards. But the surviving deck is a **Northern
Italian** product (Ferrara or possibly Florence), dated to roughly **1450–1480** — about
a century too late to be Gringonneur's. The names "Charles VI" and "Gringonneur" survive
only as labels of convenience.

## Provenance & evidence

17 trumps in the BnF, hand-painted and gilded. Style and iconography place it in the
Ferrarese orbit; its trump order is the main reason it matters — it helps define the
**B-order** that Dummett distinguishes from Bologna's A-order and Milan's C-order.

## Game or divination?

**Game.** No cartomantic use; the divinatory association is, again, a post-1781 import.

## The fear question

**No fear.** A gilded court object of the same luxury class as the Visconti decks. The
only "deception" in its history is scholarly, not social — the mis-dating, not any
hidden practice.

## Counter-voices

The bogus 1392 French date was itself an early piece of **mythmaking** that made tarot
look older and more mysterious than it is — a reminder that romantic over-dating (toward
Egypt, toward medieval France) has repeatedly run ahead of the documents. Correcting it
is not "suppressing" an occult past; it is removing a 19th-century embellishment.

## Open questions / corrections owed

- Ferrara vs. Florence origin is unresolved; date range c.1430s–1470s is still argued.
- The deck is a fragment; the full original trump set is reconstructed, not observed.
