# Etteilla II — Grand Livre de Thot

> Part of the Etteilla lineage — hub at `10-etteilla.mdx`; see also `10a` and `10c`.

## At a glance

The **second-generation** Etteilla: a posthumous re-edition of Alliette's system,
published **c.1838–1840** (often associated with the publisher **Simon Blocquel** and the
likely-pseudonymous **"Julia Orsini"**). It carries Etteilla's divinatory framework
forward with **altered titling and imagery** — a brand continued by his "school," not by
his hand.

## Origin & dating

Issued roughly **1838 (or 1840)**, decades after Alliette's death (1791). Marketed under
the *Grand Livre de Thot* title and the name **Julia Orsini** — believed to be a publisher's
pseudonym rather than a real cartomancer. Cards were **hand-water-coloured**.

## Provenance & evidence

Surviving decks and accompanying booklets; catalogued in collector and reconstruction
literature. It is a **variant** of Etteilla I — same divinatory DNA, different drawings,
some renamed cards and revised keywords.

## Game or divination?

**Divination** — squarely within the cartomantic tradition Etteilla founded; never a game
deck.

## The fear question

By the 1830s–40s, cartomancy in France was a **commercial publishing genre** — printed,
titled, sold openly with instruction booklets. The "fear" register here is essentially
zero for the publisher: fortune-telling decks were a normal product of the Paris print
trade. (The criminalised, precarious end of the practice remained the *itinerant* reader,
not the boxed retail deck.)

## Counter-voices

The proliferation of Etteilla editions shows how quickly a **manufactured tradition**
("ancient Egyptian tarot") becomes a self-sustaining market. That is not evidence of
suppressed antiquity; it is evidence of a **successful 19th-century commercial myth**.
