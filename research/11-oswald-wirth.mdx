# Oswald Wirth Tarot

## At a glance

The **first overtly occult tarot deck** — 22 Major Arcana only, designed in **1889** by
**Oswald Wirth** under the direction of the Kabbalist **Stanislas de Guaita**, realising
the program of **Éliphas Lévi**. It fixes the marriage of tarot trumps to the **Hebrew
alphabet** and the Kabbalah that defines Continental (and later English) esoteric tarot.

## Origin & dating

Wirth, immersed in the French Occult Revival, was commissioned by de Guaita to draw 22
trumps "restored to hieroglyphic purity" per Lévi's ideas. Published **1889** as *Les 22
Arcanes du Tarot Kabbalistique* (hand-colored, gilt-edged like a grimoire). Wirth issued
a revised, wider-known version in **1926**. It reinterprets the **Marseille** trumps
through Kabbalah, alchemy, Hermeticism and Masonic/Rosicrucian symbolism.

## Provenance & evidence

The 1889 and 1926 decks and Wirth's accompanying writings survive. The lineage is
explicit and documented: **Lévi → de Guaita → Wirth**, the French esoteric current that
Lévi launched in the 1850s by asserting a tarot-Kabbalah correspondence.

## Game or divination?

**Esoteric/divinatory by design** — never a game deck. Note it is **trumps only** (22
cards): a philosophical/meditative object, the Continental sibling of what the Golden
Dawn was doing in England.

## The fear question

**Openly published, but inside a subculture.** Wirth and de Guaita were public figures of
a fashionable Parisian occult *movement* (salons, orders, presses). There was no legal
peril for these bourgeois esotericists; the "secrecy" was the deliberate mystique of
initiatory orders, not fear of prosecution. Contrast again the jobbing fortune-teller:
respectable occult *philosophy* was safe; commercial *fortune-telling* was the
criminalised, déclassé version of the same impulse.

## Counter-voices

Wirth's deck embodies the esoteric tradition's own self-understanding: that it is
*restoring* ancient symbolic truth (Lévi's Kabbalah-tarot link). The historical caveat:
Lévi's Hebrew-letter assignment was an **invention of the 1850s**, not a medieval
survival. But unlike the Egyptian myth, the Wirth deck is honest about being a
*construction* — it openly builds a new symbolic system on the Marseille, which is
exactly what the grammar should record (a deliberate esoteric overlay, well documented,
not a false antiquity claim).

## Open questions / corrections owed

- Distinguish the 1889 and 1926 versions in the grammar (de Guaita-directed vs. Wirth's
  mature revision).
- Wirth's Hebrew-letter/path assignments differ from the Golden Dawn's — do **not**
  conflate the two correspondence systems.
